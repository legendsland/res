Summary

斯蒂尔演讲者在 ACM OOPSLA 上的演讲重点关注不断发展的编程语言的设计。演讲者反对设计一种大型的、预定义的语言，而是提倡一种可以随着时间推移而发展的语言。关键点包括：

This ACM OOPSLA talk, by a Steele speaker, focuses on the design of growing programming languages. The speaker argues against designing a large, pre-defined language, advocating instead for a language that can evolve over time. Key points include:

  • 发展而非静止：语言的设计应考虑到发展，允许根据需要进行添加和更改。
  • Evolving, not static: A language should be designed with growth in mind, allowing for additions and changes as needed.
  • 用户参与：演讲者强调了用户输入在语言发展过程中的重要性。用户参与和修改语言的“奇特”方法比由单个设计师主导的“大教堂”方法更受欢迎。
  • User involvement: The speaker emphasizes the importance of user input in shaping the language’s development. A “bizarre” approach, where users contribute and modify the language, is preferred over a “cathedral” approach with a single designer.
  • 从小处着手，有计划地发展：从一门小型语言开始，可以更轻松地学习和采用，并能够在以后添加功能。
  • Small beginnings, planned growth: Starting with a small language allows for easier learning and adoption, with the ability to add features later.
  • 定义术语：演讲者强调即使在不断发展的语言中，清晰简洁地定义术语的重要性。
  • Defining terms: The speaker stresses the importance of defining terms clearly and concisely, even in a growing language.
  • 灵活性和适应性：语言的设计应适应未来的需求和变化。泛型和重载运算符是关键的补充。
  • Flexibility and adaptability: The language should be designed to accommodate future needs and changes. Generic types and overloaded operators are highlighted as crucial additions.
  • 库，而不是内置：演讲者建议新功能应该以库的形式实现，而不是内置到核心语言中，从而允许用户选择使用哪些功能。
  • Libraries, not built-ins: The speaker suggests that new features should be implemented as libraries rather than built into the core language, allowing users to choose which features to use.

It looks strange.

PL/1 增长不大。在很大程度上，它和刚推出时一样。这可能是因为缺乏使用。另一方面，缺乏使用可能有两个原因。

PL/1 has not grown much. It is, for the most part, just as it was when it first came out. It may be that this is from lack of use. The flip side is that the lack of use may have two causes.

第一：PL/1 的设计初衷不是增长。它从一开始就被设计成满足所有程序员的所有需求。

Number one: PL/1 was not designed to grow. It was designed to be all things to all programmers right from the start.

第二：在当时，PL/1 一开始规模就很大。没有人知道 PL/1 的全部。有人说，没有人能够知道 PL/1 的全部。

Number two: For its time, it started out large. No one knew all of PL/1. Some said that no one could know all of PL/1.

Pascal 稍稍发展，并被用于构建许多大型程序。最初的设计的一个主要缺点是字符串难以使用，因为它们都是固定大小的。如果不改变这一点，Pascal 将无法用于构建用于现实世界的大型程序。但是，Ver 并没有计划让该语言以这种方式改变，事实上，几乎没有做出任何改变。

Pascal grew just a tad and was used to build many large programs. One main fault of the first design was that strings were hard to use because they were all a fixed size. Pascal would have been of no use for the building of large programs for use in the real world if this had not been changed. But, Ver had not planned for the language to change in such ways, and in fact, few changes were made.

有时，我们认为 C 是一种从零开始设计的小语言，但它是从一种名为 B 的较小语言发展而来的，后来发展成为一种名为 C++ 的较大语言。如果将 C++ 这样的大语言强行推向世界，它不可能传播得如此广泛。移植起来会非常困难。

At times, we think of C as a small language designed from whole cloth, but it grew out of a smaller language called B and is since grown to be a larger language called C++. A language as large as C++ could not have spread so wide if it had been forced on the world all at once. It would have been too hard to port.

造词还有一条规则：如果我们在动词词干上添加音节“er”，我们就会形成一个名词，用来指代执行动词所指操作的人或事物。例如，buyer 是购买者；user 是使用者。现在你可能已经猜到了，我和坐在前排的好朋友 Dick Gabriel 的想法一致，他写了一篇著名的长篇大论“Worse is Better”。他的真名是 Lisp。好消息、坏消息、如何大获全胜——这些都是只有一个音节的单词。这对我来说似乎是一种好运，但事实上 Dick Gabriel 知道如何选择有冲击力的单词。然而，大多数人首先想到的是标题为“Worse is better”的部分，所以他们就是这样引用它的。

One more rule for making words: If we add the syllable “er” to a verb stem, we make a noun that names the person or thing that does what the verb says to do. For example, a buyer is one who buys; a user is one who uses. As you may by now have guessed, I am of like mind with my good friend Dick Gabriel, here in the front row, who wrote the well known screed “Worse is Better.” The real name was Lisp. Good news, bad news, how to win big—which is all words of just one syllable. Which might seem like good luck for me, but the truth is that Dick Gabriel knew how to choose words with punch. Yet, what first comes to mind for most people is the part headed “Worse is better,” and so that’s how they cite it.

其要点是，让一种语言被许多人使用的最佳方式不是设计和构建正确的东西，因为那会花费太长时间。在竞赛中，一种有缺陷的小语言将击败一种设计良好的语言，因为用户不会等待正确的东西。他们会使用快速且便宜的语言，并忍受缺陷。一旦一种小语言填补了一个空白，就很难接管。

The gist of it is that the best way to get a language used by many people is not to design and build the right thing, because that will take too long. In a race, a small language with warts will beat a well designed language, because users will not wait for the right thing. They will use the language that is quick and cheap, and put up with the warts. Once a small language fills a niche, it is hard to take over.

那么，正确的做法难道不是开发一种小型语言，既不难移植，又没有缺陷吗？我想这是可以做到的，但我自己就没那么聪明，或者没那么幸运。但事实上，我认为这是做不到的。

Well, then, could not the right thing be a small language, not hard to port, but with no warts? I guess it could be done, but I, for one, am not that smart, or have not had that much luck. But in fact, I think it cannot be done.

二十五年前，当用户对语言的要求不是很高时，可以尝试 Scheme。那是我最好的尝试。但现在，用户不会高兴地选择一种只提供 Scheme 或 Pascal 所提供功能的语言。他们需要在屏幕上以明亮而狂野的色彩绘制位、线和框。他们需要通过网络与打印机和服务器通信。他们需要即时加载。他们需要他们的程序与他们不信任的其他代码一起工作。他们需要在多线程和多台机器上运行代码。他们需要处理世界上所有语言的文本和谚语。

Five and twenty years ago, when users did not want that much from a language, one could try Scheme. That was my best shot at it. But users will not now, with glad cries, glom onto a language that gives them no more than what Scheme or Pascal gave them. They need to paint bits, lines, and boxes on the screen in hues bright and wild. They need to talk to printers and servers through the net. They need load on the fly. They need their programs to work with other code they don’t trust. They need to run code in many threads and on many machines. They need to deal with text and sayings in all the world’s languages.

小型编程语言根本无法胜任。因此，小型语言无法很好地完成工作，而大型语言则需要很长时间才能起步。

A small programming language just won’t cut it. So, a small language cannot do the job right, and a large language takes too long to get off the ground.

难道我们注定要使用有许多缺陷的小语言，因为这是世界上唯一一种可以一次性成功的设计吗？这个想法让我感到沮丧，但后来我发现我的想法存在缺陷。我说用户不会等待正确的东西，而是会使用先出现的东西并忍受缺陷。但用户不会永远忍受缺陷。不久他们就会尖叫、呻吟并乞求改变。

Are we doomed to use small languages with many warts, because that is the sole kind of design that can make it in the world at one time? This thought filled me with gloom, but then I saw a gap in my thinking. I said that users will not wait for the right thing, but will use what comes first and put up with warts. But users will not put up with warts for all time. It is not long till they scream and moan and beg for changes.

小语言会成长。瑕疵会被刮除或修补。如果一个人做所有的工作，那么成长会很慢。

The small language will grow. The warts will be shaved off or patched. If one person does all the work, then growth will be slow.

但如果让用户帮忙做工作，发展就会很快。如果许多人并肩工作，并且以细心和高雅的态度添加最好的作品，那么在短时间内就可以增加很多东西。APL 是由一个人创建的，一个聪明的人。

But if one lets the user help do the work, growth can be quick. If many people work side by side, and the best work is added with care and good taste, a great deal can be added in a short time. APL was built by one man, a smart man.

我喜欢 APL，但它有一个缺陷，我认为这个缺陷几乎毁了它。用户无法顺利地发展语言。在大多数语言中，用户至少可以定义一些新词来代表其他代码片段，然后可以以新词看起来像原语的方式调用这些代码片段。通过这种方式，用户可以构建一个更大的语言来满足他的需求。但在 APL 中，用户定义的新词看起来根本不像语言原语。

And I love APL, but it had a flaw that I think has all but killed it. There was no way for a user to grow the language in a smooth way. In most languages, a user can at least define some new words to stand for other pieces of code that can then be called in such a way that the new words look like primitives. In this way, a user can build a larger language to meet his needs. But in APL, new words defined by the user do not look like language primitives at all.

一段用户代码的名称是一个单词，但是对于内置的或用奇怪的字形命名的东西，要添加看起来像是新原语的东西以保持语言的感觉，需要真正的黑客和大量的工作。这阻止了用户帮助发展语言。APL 已经发展了一些，但这项工作仅由拥有源代码的少数程序员完成。如果用户向 APL 添加内容，并且他添加的内容在负责该语言的黑客看来是个好主意，他们可能会将其内置。但使用它的代码看起来不一样。用户必须将他的代码更改为新形式，因为 APL 对内置内容的使用看起来根本不像对用户代码的调用。

The name of a piece of user code is a word, but things that are built in, or named by strange glyphs, to add what looked like new primitives to keep the feel of the language takes a real hacker and a ton of work. This has stopped users from helping to grow the language. APL has grown some, but the work has been done by just a few programmers that have the source code. If a user adds to APL, and what he added seems like a good idea to the hackers in charge of the language, they might then make it be built in. But the code to use it would not look the same. The user would have to change his code to a new form, because an APL use of what is built in does not look at all like a call to user code.

Lisp 是由一个人设计的，一个聪明的人，但它的工作方式我认为是他没有计划好的。在 Lisp 中，用户定义的新词看起来像原语。而且，所有原语看起来都像用户定义的词。换句话说，如果用户在定义新词方面很有品味，那么最终会产生一种没有主题的更大的语言。负责的设计者几乎不需要做任何工作就可以发展语言，只需从许多用户的工作中精心选择即可。而且 Lisp 的发展速度比 APL 快得多，因为许多用户可以尝试并将他们最好的代码放在那里供其他人使用并添加到语言中。

Lisp was designed by one man, a smart man, and it works in a way that I think he did not plan for. In Lisp, new words defined by the user look like primitives. And what is more, all primitives look like words defined by the user. In other words, if a user has good taste in defining new words, what comes out is a larger language that has no themes. The designer in charge can grow the language with close to no work on his part, just by choosing with care from the work of many users. And Lisp grew much faster than APL did, because many users could try things out and put their best code out there for others to use and to add to the language.

Lisp 的使用不像以前那么频繁了，但是它的部分功能在某些语言中仍然存在。

Lisp is not used quite as much as it used to be, but parts of it live on in languages.

其中最好的一个叫做垃圾收集。我现在不会试图告诉你它是什么意思。简而言之，我把它留给你作为一项业余时间尝试的任务。这并不难。

The best of which is called garbage collection. And I will not try to tell you now what that means. Words of one syllable, I leave it to you as a task to try in your spare time. It is not that hard.

这使我想说，从现在开始，设计语言的主要目标应该是规划增长。

This leads me to claim that from now on, a main goal in designing a language should be to plan for growth.

语言必须从小处着手，随着用户群的增长，语言也必须不断成长。现在我认为，作为协助 Java 编程语言的语言设计者，我需要问的不是Java 编程语言是否在成长，而是 Java 编程语言应该如何成长。成长有多种方式，实现成长的方法也多种多样。但是，正如我们将看到的，如果目标是快速完成工作，那么一种模式可能比其他所有模式都要好得多。

The language must start small, and the language must grow as the set of users grows. I now think that, as a language designer assisting with the Java programming language, I need to ask not if the Java programming language is growing, but how the Java programming language should grow. There is more than one kind of growth and more than one way to do it. But, as we shall see, if the goal is to be quick and yet to do good work, one mode may be better by far than all others.

语言的发展有两种。一种是改变词汇，另一种是改变表示一串单词含义的规则。库是一种词汇，旨在添加到编程语言中以扩大其词汇量。真正的库不会改变语言的含义规则；它只会添加新词。从中可以看出，在我看来，让你在 C 中进行长跳转的代码不是真正的库。当然，用户必须有一种方法可以创建库。但关键点是，库中的新词应该看起来就像语言的原语一样。

There are two kinds of growth in a language. One can change the vocabulary, or one can change the rules that say what a string of words means. A library is a vocabulary designed to be added to a programming language to make its vocabulary larger. A true library does not change the rules of meaning for the language; it just adds new words. You can see from this that, in my view, the code that lets you do a long jump in C is not a true library. Of course, there must be a way for a user to make libraries. But the key point is that the new words, by a library, should look just like the primitives of the language.

有些语言是这样的，有些则不是。那些不是这样的语言很难在用户的帮助下成长。

Some languages are like this, and some are not. Those that are not are harder to grow with the help of users.

为语言添加含义规则的方法也很好。有些方法比其他方法效果更好。但同样，语言应该让用户完成的工作看起来就像设计的那样。

It may be good, as well, to have a way to add to the rules of meaning for a language. Some ways to do this work better than others. But, in the same way, the language should let work done by a user look just like what was designed.

我希望以某种方式发展 Java 品牌编程语言，以便用户可以做更多这样的事。

I would like to grow the Java brand programming language in such a way that users can do more of this.

同样，发展也有两种方式。一种是由一个人或一个小团队负责，并吸收、测试、判断和添加其他人所做的工作。另一种方式是，一旦事情开始运作，就把源代码放出来，让每个人随心所欲地做。有一个人负责可能会减慢速度，但没有人负责会让许多人的工作变得更加困难。

In the same way, there are two ways to do the growing. One is for one person, or a small group, to be in charge and to take in, test, judge, and add the work done by others. The other way is to just put the source code out there once things start to work and let each person do as he wills. To have a person in charge can slow things down, but to have no one in charge makes it harder to add up the work of many persons.

我认为比其他所有方法都更快、更好的方法是：将源代码发布出来，让所有人都能使用它，但要有一个负责人，他能快速判断好的作品，并能快速接受和发布。你不必使用他发布的东西，也不必交还你的工作。但他为所有人提供了一种快速的方式，将新代码传播给需要它的人。

The way that I think is faster and better than all others does both: put the source code out there and let all persons play with it, but have a person in charge who is a quick judge of good work and who will take it in and shove it back out fast. You don’t have to use what he ships, and you don’t have to give back your work. But he gives all persons a fast way to spread new code to those who want it.

这种做法的最好例子就是 Linux，它是一种操作系统，即一种跟踪计算机中其他程序并让每个程序在时间和空间上得到应有发挥的程序。你喜欢吗？你应该读一读 Eric Raymond 对 Linux 诞生的看法。

The best example of this way to do things is Linux, which is an operating system—a program that keeps track of other programs in a computer and gives each its due in time and space. You like that? You ought to read what Eric Raymond had to say of how Linux came to be.

等我有更多话要说时，我会告诉你一些。大教堂是一座巨大的教堂。它可能用石头建造。它可能让你心生敬畏。但埃里克·雷蒙德心中的关键思想是，只有一个设计，一个宏伟的计划，可能需要很长时间，很多年才能实现。随着时间的推移，计划很少发生变化。建造它需要很多人，但只有一位设计师。

I shall tell you a bit of it once I have more words for you. A cathedral is a huge church. It may be made of stone. It may fill you with awe. But the key thought in the mind of Eric Raymond is that there is but one design, one grand plan, which may take a long time, many years, to make real. As the years pass, few changes are made to the plan. Many, many persons are needed to build it, but there is just one designer.

集市是一个有许多小商店或摊位的地方，你可以从那里的许多人那里买东西。这里的关键思想是每个人都卖他想卖的东西，每个人都买他想买的东西。没有一个计划，每个卖家或买家都可能随时改变主意。

A bazaar is a place with many small shops or stalls where you can buy things from many persons who are there to sell their wares. The key thought here is that each sells what he wants to sell, and each one buys what he wants to buy. There is no one plan, and each seller or buyer may change his mind at any time.

Eric Raymond 写了一篇短文，名为《大教堂与集市》，他在文中探讨了程序是如何构建的，或者过去是如何构建的。你可以在他的网站上找到它。他谈到了如何在 200 多名用户的帮助下构建一个邮件提取程序。他引用了 Fred Brooks 的名言“用户越多，发现的 bug 就越多”，并用这个以集市风格构建程序的例子来支持他的观点。

Eric Raymond wrote a short work called “The Cathedral and the Bazaar,” in which he looks at how programs are built, or have been built in the past. You can find it on his website. He talks of how he built a mail fetching program with the help of more than 200 users. He quotes Fred Brooks’s saying, “More users find more bugs,” and backs it up with tales from this example of building a program in the bazaar style.

对于负责的程序员的角色，Eric Raymond 说，提出好的想法是可以的，但是当你在别人的作品中看到这些想法时，了解它们就更好了。

As for the role of the programmer in charge, Eric Raymond says that it is fine to come up with good thoughts, but much better to know them when you see them in the works of others.

通过这种方式你可以完成更多的事情。

You can get a lot more done that way.

Linux 以同样的方式声名鹊起，并得到广泛使用，但规模要大得多。从更远的角度看，也许可以这样写一个操作系统：让一百万只猿猴在一百万台打字机上工作，然后找出偶然出现的好作品，并将它们拼凑起来，形成一个整体。但这可能需要很长时间。我想，太糟糕了。

Linux rose to its heights of fame and wide use in much the same way, though on a much larger scale. To take this thought to the far end of the line, it may be that one could write an operating system by putting a million apes to work at a million typing machines, then just spotting the bits of good work that come out by chance and pasting them up to make a whole. But that might take a long time. I guess, too bad.

但是集市的关键点不在于你可以让很多人与你一起完成一项任务（教堂建造者也得到了很多帮助）。关键点也不在于你在设计和建造方面都得到了帮助，尽管这实际上是一个巨大的胜利。不，关键点在于，在集市风格的程序构建或语言设计中，或者无论你愿意怎么说，计划可以实时更改以满足正在从事这项工作的人的需求。

But the key point of the bazaar is not that you can get many persons to work with you on a task (cathedral builders had a great deal of help too). Nor is the key point that you get help with the designing as well as the building, though that is in fact a big win. No, the key point is that in the bazaar style of building a program or designing a language, or whatever you will, the plan can change in real time to meet the needs of those who are working on it.

这往往会让用户坚持使用它。随着时间的推移，如果他们知道自己的需求和需要有一定的分量，并且他们的努力可以改善计划，他们就会乐于努力工作并提供帮助。这让我想到了我演讲的高潮：似乎至少在过去几年里，如果有人被邀请在这次会议上发言，他必须引用克里斯托弗·亚历山大的话。

This tends to make users stay with it. As time goes by, they will take joy in working hard and helping out if they know their wants and needs have some weight and their hard work can improve the plan. This brings me to the high point of my talk: it seems that in the last few years, at least, if one is asked to speak at this meeting, one must quote Christopher Alexander.

我了解他的一些作品，虽然不是很多，但我必须感谢迪克·加布里埃尔向我指出了一句与本次演讲要点密切相关的引言。我很遗憾地说，我不知道这句话的意思，因为克里斯托弗·亚历山大使用了许多多音节的单词，而且没有先定义它们。但我已经学会了死记硬背这些词，也许你们可以从中得到一些对你有用的想法。

I know a bit of his work, though not a lot, and I must say thanks to Dick Gabriel for pointing out to me a quote that has a lot to do with the main point of this talk. I am sad to say that I do not know what this quote means, because Christopher Alexander uses many words of more than one syllable and does not define them first. But I have learned to say these words by rote, and it may be that you out there can glean some thoughts of use to you.

克里斯托弗亚历山大说道：

Christopher Alexander says:

“总体规划还有另外两个不健康的特征。首先，总体规划的存在会疏远用户。毕竟，总体规划的存在本身就意味着，从定义上讲，社区成员对社区的未来形态影响甚微，因为大多数重要决定都已经做出。从某种意义上说，在总体规划下，人们生活的未来是冻结的，只能影响相对次要的细节。当人们失去对所处环境的责任感，意识到自己只是别人机器上的一个齿轮时，他们怎么能感受到对社区的认同感或在那里的任何目标感呢？”

“Master plans have two additional unhealthy characteristics. To begin with, the existence of a master plan alienates the users. After all, the very existence of a master plan means, by definition, that the members of the community can have little impact on the future shape of their community, because most of the important decisions have already been made. In a sense, under a master plan, people are living with a frozen future, able to affect only relatively minor details. When people lose the sense of responsibility for the environment they live in and realize they are merely cogs in someone else’s machine, how can they feel any sense of identification with the community or any sense of purpose there?”

我认为这在某种程度上意味着给你的用户一个购买和参与的机会是件好事。这对他们有好处，对你也有好处。

I think this means, in part, that it is good to give your users a chance to buy in and pitch in. It is good for them, and it is good for you.

事实上，许多大教堂都是以这种奇怪的方式建造的。那么，这是否意味着设计毫无用处呢？一点也不。但我们不是设计一个事物，而是设计一种做事方式，这种做事方式必须现在做出一些选择，而将其他选择留到以后。

In point of fact, a number of cathedrals were built in this bizarre mode. Does this mean, then, that it is of no use to design? Not at all. But instead of designing a thing, we need to design a way of doing, and this way of doing must make some choices now but leave other choices to a later time.

这让我想到了你们一直在等待的词：模式。

Which brings me to the word you have all been waiting for: a pattern.

图案和设计

Patterns and Design

模式是一种由多个部分组成的计划，它向您展示了每个部分如何与其他部分相对应，每个部分如何与其他部分连接或分离，每个部分如何完成其​​工作，其他部分如何帮助或拖累它，以及如何将所有部分作为一个整体来把握，并将其作为一个整体用于某个更高的目标或作为更大模式的一部分。模式应该给出提示或线索，说明何时何地最好使用它。

A pattern is a plan that has some number of parts and shows you how each part turns a face to the others, how each joins with the others or stands off, how each part does what it does and how the others aid it or drag it down, and how all the parts may be grasped as a whole and made to serve as one thing for some higher goal or as part of a larger pattern. A pattern should give hints or clues as to when and where it is best put to use.

此外，模式的某些部分可能是孔或槽，以后可以放置其他东西。一个好的模式会说明随着时间的推移可以做出哪些更改。因此，一些计划选择是内置在模式中的，而其他计划选择是在使用模式时选择的。这样，模式就代表了一个设计空间，您可以在其中随时选择自己的成长和变化路径。设计更好的东西是件好事，而设计模式要困难得多，最重要的是知道何时使用模式。

What is more, some of the parts of a pattern may be holes or slots in which other things may be placed later. A good pattern will say how changes can be made in the course of time. Thus, some plan choices are built into the pattern, while others are selected when the pattern is used. In this way, a pattern represents a design space where you can choose your own path for growth and change on the fly. It is good to design a thing better, and far harder to design a pattern best of all is to know when to use a pattern.

Now, for some more computer words. A datum is a set of bits.

计算机文字和数据

Computer Words and Data

数据是指一组数据的总称。对象是一种数据，其各部分的含义由一组语言规则规定。在 Java 编程语言中，这些规则使用类型来明确对象的哪些部分可以指向其他对象。对象可以分组形成类。了解对象的类可以告诉您有关该对象如何运作的大部分信息。

Data is the mass noun for a set of datums. An object is a datum, the meanings of whose parts are laid down by a set of language rules. In the Java programming language, these rules use types to make clear which parts of an object may site other objects. Objects may be grouped to form classes. Knowing the class of an object tells you most of what you need to know about how that object acts.

对象可能有字段。对象可以保存数据；它保存的数据可能会随时间而变化。每个字段可能有一个类型，它告诉您在运行时该字段中可以包含哪些数据，更重要的是，它告诉您在运行时该字段中不能包含哪些数据。方法是属于对象的一段命名代码。如果您可以命名或定位一个对象，那么您就可以调用该对象的方法。然后该方法就会执行其操作。

Objects may have fields. An object can hold a datum; which datum it holds may change from time to time. Each field may have a type, which tells you what data can be in that field at runtime, and what is more, it tells you what data cannot be in that field at runtime. A method is a named piece of code that is part of an object. If you can name or site an object, then you can call a method of that object. The method then does its thing.

Java 编程语言具有对象、类、字段、方法和类型。

The Java programming language has objects, classes, fields, methods, and types.

Java 编程语言

Java Programming Language

现在，我要谈谈 Java 编程语言尚未具备的一些东西。泛型类型是从一个或多个类型到类型的映射。换句话说，泛型类型是构建类型的一种模式。许多团体已经提出了向 Java 编程语言添加泛型类型的方法。每种方法都有自己的优点。在不到 3 20 小时前的这次会议上，我们听了三场关于向 Java 编程语言添加泛型类型的演讲。

Now, I shall speak of some things that the Java programming language does not yet have. A generic type is a map from one or more types to types. Put another way, a generic type is a pattern for building types. A number of groups of persons have set forth ways to add generic types to the Java programming language. Each way has its own good points. We heard three talks on adding generic types to the Java programming language at this meeting, less than three and 20 hours ago.

运算符是一种符号，例如加号，在语言中可以像单词一样使用。在 C 语言或 Java 编程语言中，与英语一样，称为“and”的符号本身就是运算符。但是句号或引号不是运算符。如果一个单词被赋予两个或多个含义，则称其为超载，听者必须根据所说的其余内容来选择含义。

An operator is a glyph, such as a plus sign, that can be used in a language as if it were a word. In the C or the Java programming language, as in English, the sign known as “and” per se is an operator. But a full stop or a quote mark is not an operator. A word is said to be overloaded if it is made to mean two or more things, and the hearer has to choose the meaning based on the rest of what is said.

例如，使用本演讲开始时定义的规则，动词形式（如“painted”）可能是过去时或过去分词，而当我说这句话时，由您（听者）来判断我的意思。有时，重载的单词称为多态的，这意味着该单词具有多种形式。但事实是，该单词只有一种形式和多种含义。单词的定义可能是多态的，但该单词本身不是可以在 C++ 中重载的运算符。但目前，Java 编程语言中的运算符不能由程序员重载，即使方法名称可以重载。

For example, using the rules defined near the start of this talk, a verb form such as “painted” might be a past tense or a past participle, and it is up to you, the hearer, to make the call as to which I mean when I say it. Sometimes an overloaded word is called polymorphic, which means that the word has many forms. But the truth is that the word has but one form and many meanings. The definition of a word may be polymorphic, but the word itself is not an operator that can be overloaded in C++. But right now, operators in the Java programming language cannot be overloaded by the programmer, even though the names of methods may be overloaded.

我想改变这一点。我过去说过，现在还要说，我认为 Java 编程语言添加泛型类型并让用户定义重载运算符是一件好事，就像用户可以像编写内置方法一样编写方法一样。用户应该有一种方法来为用户定义的类定义运算符，这些运算符的使用方式与内置运算符相同。

I would like to change that. I have said in the past, and will say now, that I think it would be a good thing for the Java programming language to add generic types and to let the user define overloaded operators, just as a user can code methods in just the same way as methods that are built in. The user ought to have a way to define operators for user defined classes that can be used in just the same way as operators that are built in.

更重要的是，我会添加一种轻量级类，其对象可以随意克隆而不会造成任何损害，因此可以将其保存在堆栈中以提高速度，而不仅仅是在堆中。

What is more, I would add a kind of lightweight class whose objects can be cloned at will with no harm and so could be kept on a stack for speed, and not just in the heap.

轻量级的类和数字

Lightweight Classes and Numbers

这种类型的类非常适合用作用户定义的数字类型，但也有其他用途。您可以在 James Gosling 的网页上找到所有这些的计划。

Classes of this kind would be well suited for use as user defined number types, but would have other uses too. You can find a plan for all this on a web page by James Gosling.

我们还可以添加其他一些内容，例如尾部调用和读取和写入浮点数的机器标志的方法，但这些只是泛型类型和重载运算符旁边的语言小调整。如果我们以这几种方式发展语言，那么我认为我们就不需要以其他一百种方式发展它了。用户可以完成其余任务来了解原因。

There are a few other things we could add as well, such as tail calls and ways to read and write those machine flags for numbers who float, but these are small language tweaks next to generic types and overloaded operators. If we grow the language in these few ways, then I think we will not need to grow it in a hundred other ways. The users can take on the rest of the task to see why.

Some parts of the programming vocabulary are fit for all programs, but other parts are just for their own niches.

It would not be fair to weigh down all programmers with the need to have or to learn all the words for every niche use. We should not make the Java programming language a cathedral, but a plane. Bizarre might be too loose. What we need is more like a shopping mall, where there are not quite as many choices, but most of the goods are well designed, and sellers stand behind what they sell.

I could speak at length on the ways in which a shopping mall is like a cathedral, but not here, not now.

Now, the user could define objects for such numbers to have methods that act in the right ways. To use such numbers would look strange and be slow. 泛型类型和重载运算符可以让用户对所有这些进行编码，并且使它们在各方面看起来都与内置类型一样。

Generic types and overloaded operators would let a user code up all of these, and in such a way that they would look in all ways just like the types that are built in.

它们将允许用户以流畅、干净的方式提高他们的 Java 编程技能，并且不仅限于数值应用。例如，泛型类型对于编码哈希集非常有用。并且还有许多其他用途。每个用户不必为自己的用途编写这样的数字类。当一种语言为您提供正确的工具时，少数人可以编写此类类，然后将其作为库供其他人使用或不使用，这取决于他们选择。

They would allow users to grow their Java programming skills in a smooth and clean way, and it wouldn’t be limited to numerical applications. Generic types would be good for coding hash sets, for example. And there are many other uses. Each user would not have to code up such number classes each for his own use. When a language gives you the right tools, such classes can be coded by a few and then put up as libraries for others to use or not, as they choose.

但它们不必作为基础语言的一部分内置。如果你给一个人一条鱼，他可以吃一天。如果你教一个人钓鱼，他可以吃一辈子。如果你给一个人工具，他可以制作一根钓鱼竿和许多其他工具。他可以用钓鱼竿制造一台机器。这样，他就可以帮助别人捕鱼。

But they don’t have to be built in as part of the base language. If you give a person a fish, he can eat for a day. If you teach a person to fish, he can eat his whole life long. 元语言设计

Meta-Language Design

元意味着你从自己的位置退后一步。你过去所做的就是现在你看到的。你过去所做的就是现在你采取行动的依据。动词变成了名词。你过去认为的模式现在被视为可以放入另一个模式的插槽中的东西。元对象是一个“foo”，你可以在其插槽中放入“foo”的各个部分。在某种程度上，老式的语言设计是程序的模式。

Meta means that you step back from your own place. What you used to do is now what you see. What you were is now what you act on. Verbs turn to nouns. What you used to think of as a pattern is now treated as a thing to put in the slot of another pattern. A meta object is a “foo” in whose slots you can put parts of a “foo.” In a way, a language design of the old school is a pattern for programs.

但现在我们需要转向元语言。我们现在应该将语言设计视为语言设计的一种模式——一种用于制作更多同类工具的工具。

But now we need to go meta. We should now think of a language design as being a pattern for language designs—a tool for making more tools of the same kind.

这就是我想说的要点。语言设计不再只是一种事物。它必须是一种模式——一种成长的模式，一种成长的模式，一种定义程序员可用于实际工作和主要目标的模式的模式。

This is the nub of what I want to say. A language design can no longer be a thing. It must be a pattern—a pattern for growth, a pattern for growing the pattern for defining the patterns that programmers can use for their real work and their main goal.

我的观点是，当今时代，优秀的程序员不仅仅是编写程序。优秀的程序员会构建工作词汇表。换句话说，优秀的程序员会进行语言设计，尽管不是从零开始，而是在基础语言框架的基础上进行构建。在这次演讲的过程中，由于我从一种小语言开始，所以我必须定义 50 个或更多的新单词或短语以及 16 个人或事物的名称。我列出了六条规则，用于以这种方式从旧单词中创造新单词。

My point is that a good programmer in these times does not just write programs. A good programmer builds a working vocabulary. In other words, a good programmer does language design, though not from scratch, but building on the frame of a base language. In the course of giving this talk, because I started with a small language, I’ve had to define 50 or more new words or phrases and 16 names of persons or things. And I laid out six rules for making new words from old ones in this way.

我添加了基础语言。如果我要从单音节单词开始写一本书，我肯定必须定义数百个新单词。任何人都不会忘记，要正确编写一个包含大量代码的程序，可能需要数百个新单词。也就是说，在基础语言的基础上构建一种新语言——我敢说，没有其他方法可以做到这一点。

I added to the base language. If I were to write a book starting from just the words of one syllable, I am sure that I would have to define hundreds of new words. It should give no one pause to note that the right writing of a program, having a menagerie of code, might need many, many hundreds of new words. That is to say, a new language built on the base language—I will be so bold as to say that it cannot be done any other way.

可能还有另一种方法：使用经过数十或数百年发展起来的大型、丰富的编程语言，它包含表达我们所需功能所需的一切。

There may be another way: using a large, rich programming language, developed over tens or hundreds of years, that contains everything necessary to express our desired functionality.

这是我们成长过程中学到的，也是理所当然的。也许 100 年后会出现一种编程语言，到那时，这种语言经受住了时间的考验，大多数用途都不需要再做任何修改，所有编写程序的人都会使用它，因为每个孩子在学校都会学习它。但我们现在的情况并非如此。

That we are taught as we grow up and take for granted. It may be that 100 years from now there will be a programming language that, by then, has stood the test of time, needs no more changes for most uses, and is used by all persons who write programs because each child learns it in school. But that is not where we are now.

所以，语言设计与 30 年前或 20 年前的工作完全不同。

So, language design is not at all the same kind of work it was 30 years ago or 20 years ago.

那时，你可以着手设计一门完整的语言，然后自己或与一个小团队一起构建它，因为它很小，而且因为你用它做的事情也很小。

Back then, you could set out to design a whole language and then build it yourself, or with a small team, because it was small, and because you would then do with it what you would then do with it was small.

现在，程序是一堆大杂烩，有许多需求。小型语言无法胜任这项工作。如果你试图一次性设计出一种大型语言，然后尝试一次性构建它，那将失败。你最终会迟到，而其他一些较小的语言将取代你的位置。

Now, programs are big messes with many needs. A small language won’t do the job. If you try to design a big language all at once and then try to build it all at once, it will fail. You will end up late, and some other, smaller language will take your place.

如果有一种小型编程语言让人感觉很庞大，就像 Basic English 一样，虽然很小但在某些方面让人感觉很大，那就太好了。但我不知道该怎么做，而且我有充分的理由怀疑它是否真的可以做到。

It would be great if there were some small programming language that felt large, the way Basic English is small but feels large in some ways. But I don’t know how to do it, and I have good cause to doubt that it can be done at all.

在当时，APL 是一种让人感觉很庞大的小语言。但是我们的需求已经增长，而 APL 并没有良好的增长模式。所以，我认为获胜的唯一方法是规划增长。在用户的帮助下规划增长。这对用户来说是一种胜利，因为他们可以发表意见，并可以根据自己的需求调整增长。

In its day, APL was a small language that felt large. But our needs have grown, and APL did not have a good pattern for growth. So, I think the sole way to win is to plan for growth. To plan for growth with help from users. This is a win for the users because they get to have their say and get to bend the growth to their needs.

但是你需要有一个或多个人，或者一个或多个小组，来承担判断、测试和筛选用户所做和所说的任务，并将他们认为最好的内容添加到一大堆代码中，希望其他用户能够相信他们所说的话，而不必为了自己而费尽心机去测试、判断和筛选每个新的声明或每段新的代码。

But you need to have one or more persons, or one or more groups, to take on the task of judging and testing and sifting what the users do and say, and adding what they think best to the big pile of code, in the hope that other users will trust what they say and not have to go to all the work to test and judge and sift each new claim or each new piece of code, each for his own self.

语言的某些部分必须被设计来帮助语言的成长和发展。

Parts of the language must be designed to help the task of growth, of growing the language.

一套好的类型，为用户定义新词和新语言规则的方法，定义和使用各种模式。所有这些都是必需的。

A good set of types, ways for a user to define new words and new rules to the language, to define and use all sorts of patterns. All these are needed.

例如，设计者不应该在语言中定义 20 种数字类型。但总有用户会要求 20 种不同的数字。这种语言应该允许用户定义能够与加号和其他运算符以及各种位操作操作正确交互的数字类型。

The designer should not, for example, define 20 kinds of number types in the language. But there will be users who, all told, will beg for 20 different kinds of numbers. This language should allow users to define number types that interact correctly with plus signs and other operators, and with various bit manipulation operations.

一开始，你可能只定义一两种数字类型来展示应该如何做，然后让其他用户尽力帮助他们，并肩工作，而不是面对面。你可能会发现，你需要在设计中添加缺陷，这样你就可以快速完成它，目标是以后再解决这些缺陷。

One might define just one or two number types at the start to show how it ought to be done, then leave the rest of the users to help them all as best as you can, to work side by side and not nose to nose. You may find that you need to add warts as part of the design so that you can get it out the door fast, with the goal of taking up the warts at a later time.

现在，有缺陷，也有需要小心处理的缺陷。人们可以设计缺陷，以便以后去除或修补时不会太难。但是，如果你一开始不小心，你可能会在未来几年内一直被你没有计划的缺陷所困扰。有些缺陷不是你加进去的坏东西，而是你遗漏的好东西。如果你选择这样做，请制定计划，以后再添加这些好东西，并确保你的设计的其他部分不会在适当的时候阻止你添加这些好东西。

Now, there are warts, and then there are warts with care. One can design a wart so that it will not be too hard to take out or patch up later on. But if you do not take care at the outset, you may be stuck for years to come with a wart you did not plan. Some warts are not bad things you put in, but good things you leave out. Have a plan to add those good things later, if you should choose to do so, and make sure that other parts of your design don’t cut you off from adding those good things when the time is right.

Java 编程语言设计

Java Programming Language Design

我希望把这些想法应用到 Java 编程语言上。Java 编程语言之所以能发展到今天这样，是因为它从小处着手。我认为它并不难学，移植起来也不难。从那时起，它已经发展了不少。如果 Java 编程语言现在的设计是在三年前提出的，我敢肯定，它肯定会失败。程序员会哭诉，太大了，头发太多了——我无法应付所有这些。但在现实生活中，它运行良好，因为用户与语言一起成长，一点一点地学习它，他们接受它，因为他们对如何改变语言有一定的发言权。

I hope to bring these thoughts to bear on the Java programming language. The Java programming language has done as well as it has up to now because it started small. I think it was not hard to learn, and it was not hard to port. It has grown quite a bit since then. If the design of the Java programming language as it is now had been put forth three years ago, it would have failed, I am sure. Programmers would have cried, too big, too much hair—I can’t deal with all that. But in real life, it worked out fine because the users have grown with the language and learned it piece by piece, and they buy into it because they’ve had some say in how to change the language.

Java 编程语言还需要进一步发展，但我希望不会发展太多。至少，我认为只需要增加一些规则，其余的可以通过库来实现，这些库大部分是由用户而不是 Sun 构建的。如果我们在 Java 品牌编程语言中添加数百个新东西，我们将拥有一门庞大的语言，但这需要很长时间才能实现。

The Java programming language needs to grow yet some more, but I hope not a lot more. At least, I think only a few more rules are needed, and the rest can be done with libraries, most of them built by users and not by Sun. If we add hundreds of new things to the Java brand programming language, we will have a huge language, but it will take a long time to get there.

但如果我们添加一些东西——只是添加正确的东西——泛型、运算符重载、用户定义的轻量级数字和小向量类型等，以及一些旨在让用户自己制作和添加东西的东西——我认为我们可以走得更远，而且速度更快。我们需要将语言发展的工具交到用户手中。我希望我们能以这样或那样的方式设计一种编程语言，让我们似乎不必花时间用单音节的单词说话和写作。

But if we add a few things—just the right things—generic types, operator overloading, user defined types of lightweight for use numbers and small vectors, and such, and some other things that are designed to let users make and add things for their own use—I think we can go a long way and much faster. We need to put tools for language growth in the hands of users. I hope that we can, in this way or some other, design a programming language where we don’t seem to spend our time talking and writing in words of just one syllable.

我能说的关于短词的一个好处是，它们可以做简短的演讲。他们给了我一个半小时的时间做这个演讲，而我用了不到一个小时。我讲完后，你们就有时间去大厅喝杯茶，和朋友们聊天。但首先，我想告诉你们我从设计这个演讲的任务中学到了什么。在选择放弃我从小就认识的许多长词时——这些词有很多细微的含义——我让这件事变得比本该的要困难得多。

One of the good things I can say for short words is that they make for short talks. They gave me an hour and a half for this talk, and I’ve used less than an hour. Once I am done, you’ll have time to go out in the hall, have a cup of tea, and talk with your friends. But first, I would like to tell you what I have learned from the task of designing this talk. In choosing to give up the many long words that I have come to know since I was a child—words that have many fine shades of meaning—I made this much harder than it needs to be.

我希望你不会觉得这听起来太难听。但我发现坚持这条规则让我思考。我必须花时间仔细考虑如何表达每个想法。对于每个新词，我都有这样的选择：是否值得花功夫去定义它，还是我应该坚持使用我已有的词语？

I hope that you have not found it too hard on your ears. But I found that sticking to this rule made me think. I had to take time to think through how to phrase each thought. And there was this choice for each new word: Is it worth the work to define it, or should I just stick with the words I have?

我应该定义一个新词，比如“镜子”，还是每次我想举一个新词作为例子时就直接说“镜子”？我不止一次想说出“ly”规则，即创造改变动词含义的新词。但最后，我把这些词都抛在一边，凑合着用。我差点就定义了“without”这个词，但每次，不管好坏，我都找到了其他方式来表达我的想法。

Should I do the work of defining a new word, such as “mirror,” or should I just say “looking glass” each time I want to speak of one as an example? I was tempted more than once to state the “ly” rule for making new words that change what verbs mean. But in the end, I cast all such words aside and made do. I came that close to defining the word “without,” but each time, for better or worse, I found some other way to phrase my thought.

我年轻时从斯特伦克和怀特等伟大写作老师的书中学到，尽可能选择简短的单词是最好的。

I learned in my youth from the books of such great teachers of writing as Strunk and White that it is better to choose short words when I can.

我不应该选择冗长难懂的词语，只为了让别人觉得我懂很多。我应该尽量让我的想法清晰。如果这些想法清晰正确，那么其他人就能对我的作品做出应有的评判。我相信你会的。

I should not choose long, hard words just to make other persons think that I know a lot. I should try to make my thoughts clear. If they are clear and right, then other persons can judge my work as it ought to be judged. And I’m sure you will.

从策划这次演讲的工作开始，我尝试比过去更进一步地遵循这一简短词语规则。我发现，在大多数情况下，它们是正确的。如果我们选择得当，简短词语会发挥很好的作用。因此，我认为编程语言需要更像我们所说的语言。但如果我们使用我们所说的语言的方式更像我们使用编程语言，也许会更有益处。总的来说，如果那些统治我们的人——那些位高权重的人、那些负责国家工作的人、那些评判我们行为的人，最重要的是那些制定法律的人——用单音节词语来定义他们的术语并表达其他一切，这可能是一件好事。

From the work of planning this talk, I have tried to go with this rule of short words much farther than in the past. I found that, for the most part, they were right. Short words work well if we choose them well. Thus, I think that programming languages need to be more like the languages that we speak. But perhaps it would be beneficial if we used the languages we speak more like we use programming languages. Overall, it might be a good thing if those who govern us—those in high places, who conduct the work of the state, who judge our actions, and most of all, those who make the laws—defined their terms and expressed everything else in single syllable words.

哇哦！因为我发现这种说话方式很难让人接受。

Woah! For I have found that this mode of speech makes it hard to hedge.

要找到恰当的方式来表达自己想说的话，需要付出努力和极大的谨慎，还需要一定的技巧。但最终，你似乎别无选择，只能直言不讳。如果你不偏离事实，你就不得不直言不讳。我敦促你试一试。谢谢。

It takes work and great care, and some skill, to find just the right way to say what you want to say. But in the end, you seem to have no choice but to talk straight. If you do not veer wide of the truth, you are forced to hit it dead on. I urge you to give it a try. Thank you.
