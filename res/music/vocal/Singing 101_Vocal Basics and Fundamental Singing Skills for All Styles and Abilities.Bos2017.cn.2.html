2 呼吸基础知识
2 BREATH BASICS

这里有种不同寻常的方式来理解唱歌的机制：唱歌就是控制空气通过喉咙里的两层膜。就是这样；以可控的速度将空气通过喉咙里的两个小声带。如果你能用呼吸肌来控制通过声带（也就是喉头）的空气，你就能控制音调、能量和乐句的长度。

Here is an unusual way to think about the mechanics of singing; singing is the controlled release of air past two membranes in your throat. That’s all it is; releasing air at a controlled pace past two little vocal folds in your throat. If you can use your breathing muscles to control the air coming through your voice box (aka larynx) you will be able to control your pitch, energy, and length of phrase.

为了真正理解每章中的概念，我们将在每章中进行一项或多项练习。学习呼吸基础知识时，你需要有足够空间躺下。你需要抬高膝盖，让下背部感觉舒适。但如果你想在膝盖下放一个枕头，或者在下背部下放一个枕头，也是可以的。

In order to really understand the concepts in each chapter, we will do one or more activities in each. For Breath Basics you will need room to lie down on your back. You will elevate your knees so that your lower back is comfortable, but if you would like to put a pillow under your knees instead, or add a pillow under your lower back, that is fine.

有些活动会涉及发出大量声音。但这次活动的主题是无声呼吸。我们将重点关注身体在呼吸时的感觉，并逐步学习身体对其他声音（包括唱歌）的感受。

Some of the activities will involve making lots of sound. But this one is all about breathing without much sound. We will focus on how our bodies feel when we breath, and progress to how our bodies feel for other sounds, including singing.

1 仰卧在地板上。双膝舒适地弯曲，双脚放在地板上。双手放在腹部，深呼吸，保持平静。    
1     Lie down on the floor on your back. Bend your knees comfortably with your feet on the floor. Put your hands on your belly and take a nice, deep, calm breath.

6 再试一次，但这一次，当你呼出空气时，不要让你的胸腔塌陷并下陷得太快。慢慢来。试着保持你的胸腔宽阔、扩张，以可控的速度释放空气。一种思考方式是，想象一下如果你用手抓住你的胸腔和腹部周围，而不是让你的肋骨和腹肌内陷，你的手能够让你的核心保持宽阔，就像一根柱子。胸腔宽度周围的这种扩张或张力是控制空气释放的呼吸肌肉的另一部分。好了。让我们吸满气——然后叹气。这是可控的吗？这样就好多了。

This expansion, or tension, around the width of your rib cage is another part of your breathing muscles controlling the release of air. Here we go. Let’s fill up – and sigh. Was that controlled? That’s better.

这就是我们唱歌时采用的一种控制空气释放的方式。

That’s the type of controlled release of air that we use when we’re singing.

To review, you were controlling the release of air. You should have felt that control shared to some degree between your belly or abdominal area and the muscles of your lower rib cage. Here's where you should not feel it. You should not feel your shoulders go up. You should not feel your sternum go up like a soldier (Your sternum is the bone between your ribs over the middle of your chest) If you were to do this standing, you should not stretch taller when you breathe in – you should get wider! And you should not get shorter when you sing out – you should - very, very slowly - get narrower.

3 吸气，呼气
3 BREATHING IN, BREATHING OUT

躺在地板上也能学到很多关于呼吸的知识。但平躺呼吸比站着呼吸更简单，也更容易集中注意力于呼吸和身体。但平躺唱歌并非我们经常能享受到的奢侈。偶尔，舞台上的歌手会被要求躺在床上表演，但对我们大多数人来说，我们都没有这个机会。

We can learn a lot about breathing when we are lying on the floor. But breathing while horizontal is simpler than when we are standing. It also seems to be easier to mentally focus on the breath and body. But singing while horizontal isn’t a luxury we get very often. Occasionally singers on the stage are asked to perform stretched out in a bed, but for most of us, we won’t get the chance.

让我们站起来试试这个呼吸方法。当你在之前的水平练习中获得垂直呼吸的体验时，你会发现你的胸腔和腹肌如何最有效地互动。感觉会很不一样。当我们的肋骨加上头部、手臂和肩膀的重量叠加在腹肌上时，会改变我们自然的呼吸方式。唱歌时，人们可以通过几种不同的方式保持“良好”的呼吸。根据体型和肌肉张力，更多地使用胸腔扩张或腹部扩张，或者两者均衡扩张可能会感觉更好。现在是时候找到适合你独特体型的方法了。

Let’s give this breathing a try standing up. You’ll find out how your rib cage and abs interact the most efficiently for you when you have a vertical experience with the previous horizontal exercise. It will feel quite a bit different. When our ribs plus the weight of our head, arms, and shoulders are stacked on top of our abs it changes our natural breathing. There are a few different ways that a person can breathe “well” for singing. Depending on body type and muscle tone, it might feel better to use more rib cage expansion, or abdominal expansion, or share the expansion equally between the two. Now is your chance to find out what is right for your unique body.

Some people tend to squeeze their vocal folds for the onset of a vowel word, starting vowel sounds too rough. It is called a glottal onset. A glottal onset always sounds like a little cough or bark at the beginning of a vowel word, like “each.” The opposite is an aspirated onset and it sounds like breathy “h’s” on vowel words. “(h)Eli (h)eats (h)apples.” Got it? Neither one is particularly healthy for your throat. The best thing is called a balanced onset – it is an onset that you can't hear. It should sound like a gentle vowel without a puff of air, and without a tiny cough. You can use the glottal onset when you need to – when it's a stylistic choice, not because you have to and can't do it any differently. As long as you are doing this exercise, pay attention to those onsets and if you have trouble you'll need to spend extra effort on mastering control of onsets.

2 接下来，双脚分开与臀部同宽，站立不动，检查你的脚趾和小腿。它们放松吗？臀部稍微内倾了吗？下背部放松吗？肩膀放松吗？向后、向下？你的肩膀放松吗？如果没有，那就用腹肌支撑身体，而不是肩膀。这应该会有帮助。接下来，检查你的脖子——修长但柔软？你的头能自由转动吗？最后，放松你的脸、下巴和舌头。         
2          N ext, standing still with feet hips-width apart, check your toes and calves. Are they relaxed? Hips tipped in a little? Lower back soft and relaxed? Shoulders are loose, back, and down? Are your shoulders loose? If not, use activated abs to support your body, not your shoulders. That should help. Next, check your neck – long and tall but soft? Can you swivel your head freely? Finally, relax your face, jaw, and tongue.

3 现在，充满激情地说一些富有表现力且积极的话，看看它会如何影响你的姿势和紧张感。试试这样说：“你是最棒的厨师！”你注意到了什么？你的身体保持放松还是动了起来？感觉自然吗？说“你”这个词时，你的嘴唇会向前，说“是”时，你的下巴会张开。说“最好”时，你的嘴会张大，而说“厨师”时，你的嘴巴会更微妙地变成“你”。         
3          Now, with passion, say something expressive and positive and see how it affects your posture and tension. Try this: “You are the BEST COOK!” What do you notice? Did your body stay relaxed or did you move? Did it feel natural? For the word “You,” your lips came forward, for “are,” your jaw dropped. For “best,” your mouth spread wide, and “cook” was a more subtle version of “you.”

4 接下来，是你的腹肌。当你说“你是最棒的厨师！”的时候，你注意到你的腹肌收紧了吗？        
4         Next, y our abs. When you say “You are the BEST COOK!” Do you notice your abs tightening?

5 现在我们试着用戏剧性的方式唱这首歌。随便选一个音调，唱“你是最棒的厨师！”完全放松地唱也可以，但天哪，它失去了激情！       
5        N ow let’s try singing it with drama. Pick any pitches and sing, “You are the BEST COOK!” It is possible to sing it totally relaxed but boy it loses its passion!

6 好吧，我们来试试吧。绷紧下巴，试试看；唱“你是最棒的厨师！”你觉得怎么样？我感到很生气。如果你同意，那么你就知道一个表达愤怒的有效方法了。但假设你是那种总是咬紧牙关唱歌的人。也许你的下巴紧绷会限制你的表达能力，限制你的表演。       
6        O kay, let's mess with it. Tighten your jaw and give it a try; sing, “YOU ARE THE BEST COOK!” What do you think? I felt angry. If you agree then you know an effective way to communicate anger. But say you are one of those people who sings with a tight jaw all of the time. Perhaps you are limiting your expressiveness, limiting your performance, by having a tight jaw.

7 接下来，试着用气声唱“你是最棒的厨师”。对我来说，这听起来很亲切。不错，但传得不太好。我需要一个麦克风才能传达出这种表现力。     
7      N ext, try singing it with a breathy voice, “You are the best cook.” For me that is an intimate sound. Nice, but it wouldn't carry very well. I'd need a mic for that expressiveness to carry.

为了表达，我们自然会添加紧张感。为了减少紧张感，可以大声朗读你正在学习的任何歌曲的每个乐句。要自然地、用舒适的表达方式朗读。然后跟着音符唱，不要添加新的紧张感——避免皱起额头、张开下巴、脖子僵硬等动作。

For expression, we naturally add tension. To minimize the tension, try every phrase of any song you are learning by speaking it out loud. Speak it naturally with comfortable expressiveness. Then sing it with the notes without adding new tension – avoid things like a scrunched-up forehead, locked open jaw, stiff neck.

有时我们会养成一些毫无意义的紧张习惯，甚至自己都意识不到自己在紧张。但如果我们走得太远，太放松，我们的表现就会变得毫无生气。所以，要有意识地、刻意地运用紧张。

Sometimes we get in such useless habits of tension, tension we don't even know we are using. But if we go too far the other way, too relaxed, our performance becomes lifeless. So use tension consciously and deliberately.

对着镜子观察自己唱歌的样子。你可能会对自己的所见感到惊讶。

Watch yourself sing in a mirror. You might be surprised at what you see.

11 身体紧张
11 BAD BODY TENSION

所有歌手都应该注意一些常见的不良演唱紧张情况。我们经常在业余歌手中看到这种情况，有时也会在专业歌手中看到。严重的紧张 会影响表演效果。

There are some common bad singing tensions that all singers should be aware of. We see them all the time in amateur singers and sometimes in professionals. Bad tensions can take away from a performance.

它们会分散注意力，降低声音质量，如果使用频率和时间过长，可能会造成伤害。

They can be distracting, make for a lower quality sound, and if used often enough and long enough, can cause harm.

不良的紧张状态有很多种，可能出现在面部、舌头、下巴、颈部、喉咙、肩膀和身体上。但有些情况比其他情况更常见。以下是我们在许多业余歌手身上常见的三种紧张状态：

There are many possible bad tensions. They can be in the face, tongue, jaw, neck, throat, shoulders, and body. But some of them are more common than others. Here are three tensions that we see in many armature singers:

• 臀部向

• ​将颈部向前伸

• 呼吸嘈杂

• ​ Leaning forward at the hips

• ​ Stretching the neck forward

• ​ Noisy breathing

首先是臀部前倾。这会影响嗓音——它会改变喉咙，降低呼吸控制力。它也会严重影响歌手的形象。通常情况下，歌手站姿优美、放松、平静，但当他们喘着粗气吸完第一口气时，身体也会前倾。测试你是否存在这个问题的一个好方法是靠着一面平坦的墙站着。找到你靠墙唱歌时舒适的姿势。选择一首歌来开始演唱——你只需要一个乐句。一旦你准备好了，吸气开始唱歌。如果你发现自己向前倾，那么你需要改掉一些紧张的习惯。如果这对你来说是个问题，首先要记住的是如何让你的肺部充满空气（正如第二章到第六章所述）。接下来，你需要改掉身体前倾的习惯。你可以定期练习靠墙唱歌来检查你的进步。

First is leaning forward at the hips. It has an effect on the voice – it changes the throat and decreases breath control. It also has a major effect on how the singer looks. What often happens is the singer will be standing in a wonderful, relaxed, calm posture, but as they take the first breath of the song in a gasping way, they also lean forward. A good way to test out if you have this problem is by standing up against a flat wall. Find your comfortable posture for singing against the wall. Pick a song to break into – you will only need one phrase. As soon as you are ready, inhale and begin singing. If you find yourself moving forward, then you have some tension habits to break. If this is a problem for you, the first thing to remember is how to fill your lungs (as covered in chapters two through six). Next you need to break the habit of leaning forward. You can practice singing against the wall regularly to check your progress.

第二个不良的紧张习惯——颈部前倾或上抬。这会直接影响你的音质、音准，甚至影响你的形象。我们常常意识不到自己在这么做，因为一旦开始唱歌，我们就会把姿势抛在脑后。关键在于记住头部保持平衡的感觉。一只手轻轻放在下颌或下巴上，另一只手放在后颈。准备唱歌。感受头部向上或向前倾斜。如果你有这个坏习惯，你必须改掉它，否则你将永远无法发出轻松自然的声音，也无法保持音准。记住这一点，唱完整首歌。如果你发现自己的下巴向上翘，就需要改掉这个坏习惯了。

Second bad tension habit – stretching forward or up at the neck. This has a direct effect on the quality of your sound, accuracy of your pitch, and it affects your appearance. We often don't realize we are doing this because as soon as we start singing our mind leaves thoughts of posture behind. The key is to remember the feeling of a balanced head. Rest one hand gently on your jaw or chin and the other on the back of your neck. Get ready to sing your song. Feel for your head tipping up or forward. If you have this bad habit, you will have to break it or you will never have a relaxed, natural sound, and you will never be able to rely on your pitch accuracy. Sing a complete song with this in mind. If you found your chin going up you'll need to fix this bad habit.

帮助歌手戒掉这个习惯的常见方法是让歌手边唱边转动脖子，弯腰唱歌，或者趴在瑜伽球上唱歌。手持式颈部按摩器也有助于放松肌肉。

Common ways to work with a singer to eliminate this habit is to have a singer roll their neck while singing, sing bent over at the waist, or draped over a yoga ball. Hand-held neck massagers can also be useful in relaxing the muscles.

最后一个常见的问题就是呼吸时有杂音。也就是说，当你吸气的时候，我们能听到。在日常生活中，我们大多数人的呼吸几乎都是无声的。

The last common tension to cover is noisy breathing. That is, when you take air in we can hear it. In our everyday lives, most of us breathe almost silently.

即使在被要求深吸一口气时，我们通常也会张大喉咙，呼吸也非常安静。但对大多数人来说，一旦他们为了唱歌而呼吸，他们就忘记了张大喉咙。你很少会在商业 CD 上听到嘈杂的呼吸声。录音工程师的工作之一就是消除呼吸噪音。但你无法相信呼吸噪音有多么普遍。问题是，吸气时喉咙紧绷意味着唱歌时喉咙无法放松，因此声音会很紧，共鸣会降低，观众可能会分心。更好的选择是，在吸气时放松、张开喉咙，这意味着歌手在发声时可以有各种选择。

Even when instructed to take a deep breath, we usually have a wide open throat and the breath is pretty quiet. But for most people, as soon as they breath for singing, they forget to have that wide open throat. You will rarely hear noisy breathing on commercial CDs. One of the jobs of the recording engineer is to take out the breath noises. But you can't believe how common noisy breathing is. The problem is that a tight throat during the intake of air means your throat can't be relaxed when you are singing, so the sound will be tight, your resonance will be reduced, and your audience might be distracted. The better choice, a loose, open throat during inhalation, means the singer has all of their options open to them when it comes to creating sound.

把这一章收藏起来，反复翻阅。我们大多数人都能说出几乎所有流派中非常紧张的职业歌手的名字。但你会因为他们的紧张而尊重他们吗？可能不会。而且，这种紧张可能会导致歌手暂时或长期出现声音嘶哑，最终送往医院。

Bookmark this chapter and return to it over and over. Most of us can name professional singers in just about any genre who are very tense. But do you respect them for that tension? Probably not. And that tension can land a singer in a doctor’s chair, having caused temporary or chronic hoarseness.

提示

每个歌手必须遵守两条与紧张有关的规则：

• ​如果 感到疼痛，就不要做。

• 如果 它让你声音嘶哑，那么一定是哪里出了问题。

TIP

Two tension related rules every singer must live by:

• ​ If it hurts, don't do it.

• ​ If it makes you hoarse then something is wrong.

12 声音分类和注册
12 VOICE CLASSIFICATIONS AND REGISTRATION

分类 是指根据最佳音域和特征来标记你的声音类型。歌手的分类至少有两种：古典和非古典。首先，我来解释一下各种音乐风格的分类。男歌手主要分为三大类：男低音（最低）、男中音（这是最常见的、典型的男歌手音域）和男高音（或主唱）。流行和摇滚乐中的大多数男歌手的歌曲都是用男高音音域创作的。乡村音乐似乎比其他音乐类型给男中音提供了更多的机会。女歌手通常分为女低音（最低）和女高音（最高）。女高音在职业歌手中似乎更为常见。

Classifications means labeling your voice type by optimal pitch range and character. There are at least two different worlds of classifications for singers; classical and non-classical. First I'll explain the classifications for many styles of music. Men are divided into three main categories. Those categories are bass (the lowest), baritone (this is the most common, typical male range), and tenor (or lead). The majority of men's songs in pop and rock are written in the tenor range. Country seems to give more opportunities baritones than other genres do. Women usually divide as alto (the lowest) and soprano (the highest). Sopranos seem to be slightly more common among professionals.

古典独唱和合唱的分类类似。大多数合唱团从最低到最高分为男中音、男高音、女低音和女高音。但在歌剧中，特别是在德语 Fach 系统（fach 表示“类型”或“隔间”）中，声音类型扩展为男低音、男低男中音、男中音、男高音、假声男高音、女低音、女中音和女高音。此外，这些类别还细分为“戏剧女高音”或“抒情女高音”、“滑稽男高音”或“英雄男高音”等子类别。大约有 24 个细分类别。在合唱音乐界，女低音声部的歌手在古典音乐中通常被称为女中音。女男高音在歌剧中被称为女低音。

For classification in classical solo and choral singing, the divisions are similar. Most choruses are divided, from lowest to highest, into baritones, tenors, altos and sopranos. But in opera, and specifically in the German Fach system, (fach means “type” or “compartment”), the voice types are expanded out to Bass, Bass-Baritone, Baritone, Tenor, Countertenor, Contralto, Mezzo-soprano and Soprano. Additionally, those categories are divided into subcategories such as “dramatic soprano” or “lyric soprano”, “tenor buffo” or “heldentenor.” There are approximately 24 subdivisions. In the world of choral music, a singer in the alto section would usually be called a mezzo-soprano in classical music. A female tenor would be called a contralto in opera.

如果你想知道自己的嗓音类型，可以使用互联网上的工具或应用程序来识别你的嗓音类型。它们会通过确定你的最低音和最高音来对你的声音进行分类。但这只是确定嗓音类型的部分方法。我们还会考虑断音的位置以及声音的音色。为了更好地了解嗓音类型，歌手需要与经验丰富的合唱团指挥或声乐老师进行一系列的发声练习。

If you are wondering what your voice classification is, you might use tools on the internet or apps that will identify your voice type. They will classify your voice by determining your lowest notes and highest notes. But this is only a part of the formula for determining voice type. We also consider where the breaks are and what the color of the sound is. To get a good idea of voice type, a singer needs to work a series of vocal exercises with a knowledgeable choir director or voice teacher.

注册 与分类是两个不同的问题。

Registration is a separate issue from Classification.

一般来说，音色，或者说声音的品质，被称为音调。音调与声音的轻重、强弱、响度或气息压力有关。音调与情感表达密切相关——听众通过音调的选择，获得情感“信息”，了解歌手的感受。音调有很多不同的名称，但一些常见的名称包括：高声演唱、类似语音、真实演唱、胸腔共鸣、头声和混合音。

Generally tone colors, or voice  qualities, are known as registration. Registration has to do with how heavy or light a sound is, how driven or relaxed, how loud, or how much breath pressure. Registration is tied closely to emotional expression - listeners get emotioinal “messages,” telling them how the singer feels, through registration choices. There are many different names but some common ones are belting, speech-like, and legit, chest voice, head voice, and mix.

高声演唱 很难定义，因为它在不同的流派中听起来有所不同，而且根据歌手的音域舒适度，描述和演唱方式也有所不同。但本质上，高声演唱是指任何响亮、通常具有穿透力、充满情感能量的声音。

Belting is tricky to define because it sounds different in different genres, and can be described and produced differently depending on the pitch range comfort for that singer. But essentially, belting is anything that is loud and often piercing, and packed with emotional energy..

类似语音的 歌唱是一种对话式的歌唱。它需要麦克风来放大。

Speech-like is conversational singing. It requires a mic for amplification.

术语 legit用于描述女性柔和、流畅的歌声，通常表示高音。同样，男性的假声位于声音的最高部分，但假声这个词越来越不受欢迎，因为它有负面含义，好像听起来总是虚弱和气喘吁吁。相反，那些能自如地在音域最高处唱歌的男性（如 Bruno Mars、Barry Gibb 等）通常称之为高声。所有男性都有高声（或假声），无论声音类型如何。对于女性来说，高声、头声和legit可以互换。对于男性来说，高声、假声，有时还有头声，可以互换。Legit 是legitimate的缩写，falsetto 在意大利语中是小假声的意思。这些自然而普遍的人声部分的名称本身就有偏见，这难道不有趣吗？

The term legit is used for gentle, smooth singing for women and often means high range notes. Similarly, falsetto for men can be found at the highest part of the voice but the word falsetto is going out of favor as it has negative connotations, as if it always sounds weak and breathy. Instead, men who comfortably sing near the top of their range (like Bruno Mars, Barry Gibb, and others), often call it their high voice .  All men have a high voice (or falsetto), regardless of voice type. The terms high voice, head voice , and legit , for women, are interchangeable. For men, the words high voice, falsetto , and sometimes head voice , are interchangeable. Legit is short for legitimate and falsetto is Italian for little false . Isn’t it interesting that the very names of these natural and ubiquitous parts of the human voice are biased.

最后，音域最高的是哨音，或者说哨音音域。哨音位于男女嗓音的最高点。许多人，无论嗓音类型如何，通过练习都能发出几个这样的音，但这样做几乎没有实际意义。爱莉安娜·格兰德和玛丽亚·凯莉已经找到了将哨音融入表演的方法。哨音最高音的吉尼斯世界纪录保持者是一位名叫阿米尔侯赛因·莫莱伊的男子（2019年7月，演唱了5989赫兹的F#8）。

Finally, the highest pitch range is whistle tone, or whistle register. Whistle tone is at the very top of the female or male voice. Many people, no matter the voice type, can make a few notes up there with practice but there is very, very little practical reason to do so. Arianna Grande and Mariah Carey have found ways to make whistle tone part of their performances. The Guinness world record holder for the highest note, in whistle tone, is a man named Amirhossein Molaei (July 2019, singing an F#8 at 5989 Hz).

这些注册标签对歌手来说意味着什么？所有歌手都应该学会控制自己的高亢、朗诵、平滑和高音演唱风格，并能够协调自如地在这些风格之间切换。这项技能对于成为优秀的叙事者和技艺精湛的歌手至关重要。对于大多数未经训练的歌手来说，你的嗓音中某个部分会比其他部分强得多。这通常是指你说话时用到的那个部分。

What do all of these registration labels mean for a singer? All singers should learn to control their belting, speech-like, smooth, and high voice singing styles, and be able to switch between them comfortably in a coordinated way. This skill is essential to being good story-tellers and accomplished singers. For most untrained singers one part of your voice is much, much stronger than other parts. That would generally be the part of your voice that you use for speaking.

感到困惑？我不怪你。不同的歌唱音质有很多名称，其中很多都互相重叠。即使在声乐老师中，对于一些含义相近的常用术语，例如近义词“头声”、“高声”、“假声”、“假音”、“高音”、“头声”和“环甲膜优势音”（CT），也没有完全达成共识。所有这些名称都指的是一般的音高范围，但如果我们还考虑音质和响度，情况就变得更加复杂了。

Confused? I don’t blame you. There are so many names for different singing qualities, and many of them overlap. Even among voice teachers, there isn’t complete consensus about common terms that pretty much mean the same thing, like the near-synonyms head voice, high voice, falsetto, legit, treble, voce di testa, and cricothyroid dominant (CT). All of those labels refer to general pitch ranges, but once we also consider tone quality and loudness, the waters get even more muddy.

尽管如此，音准控制是学习唱歌的重要组成部分。我们不能回避它，所以，无论是否泥泞，让我们深入研究一下。

Nonetheless, registration is a big part of studying singing. We can’t shy away from it, so let’s dive in, muddy or not.

活动
Activity

● 让我们稍微练习一下音调，看看我们到底在说什么。用低沉、饱满、有力的声音，大声说一声“你好！”。然后，把它变成歌声。这听起来像你自然说话的声音吗？对很多人来说确实如此。当你唱歌或说话时，你可能会感觉到胸部有震动。这就是为什么它经常被称为胸腔共鸣。实际上，你的胸部并没有发出声音，但那里有一些美妙、活跃的感觉。      
●       Let’s try registration out a little to discover what we’re talking about. In a low, rich, strong sound, say a loud “Hello!” Next, turn that into singing. Does this feel like it  is in your natural speaking voice? For many people it will. When you sing or speak this, you might feel vibrations in your chest. That is why it is frequently called your chest voice. There's actually no sound-making going on in your chest, but there is some nice, lively sensation there.

● 现在让我们用你声音中柔和的高音部分试试，男女都可以。你可以把声音唱得很高，但请不要唱得像卡通般小声。保持声音洪亮饱满，就像茱莉亚·查尔德可能用过的那样。说“你好”。现在唱“你好”。有些人会很难做到，因为他们从未锻炼过控制这些音调的肌肉。如果你找不到高音，发出的声音更像是用皮带抽打或大喊，那么你需要花时间练习发展你声音的这一部分。你声音的这一部分通常被称为头声。你的头声唱得越高，你就越有可能感觉到头部某些部位的共振。    
●     Now let's try it in the gentle, high part of your voice, men and women both. You can go very high for this, but please don't go into a small, cartoony voice. Keep it big and full; like Julia Child might have used. Speak “Hello.” Now sing “Hello.” There will be some who will have a hard time with this because they've never developed the muscles that control these pitches. If you can't find the high notes and it comes out more like a belt or yell, then you will want to spend practice time developing this part of your voice. This part of your voice is often called your head voice. The higher you go into your head voice, the more likely you will feel the resonant vibrations in parts of your head.

● 现在我们来转换一下声部。从低沉的胸腔共鸣音开始，一路滑到最高的高音（或头腔共鸣，假声）。你听到一个小小的停顿了吗？在古典歌唱中，这叫做“ passaggio” （过渡音） ，在英语中我们称之为“ break”（停顿），但我喜欢称之为“ speed bump”（减速带）。人们经常会跳过它。如果你计划周全，可能听不到它；但如果用力过猛或过猛，就很难预料它会落在哪里了。在某些唱法中，比如约德尔唱法，强调停顿非常重要。     
●      Now we'll do a shift from one voice to the next. Start on a low chest-voice note, and slide all the way up to your highest high-voice (or head-voice, or falsetto) note. Did you hear a little break? In classical singing that is called your passaggio and in English we call it a break , but I like to call it a speed bump .  People often bounce right over it. If you plan well you might not hear it and if you slam into it too fast or heavy, there's no telling where it will land. In some styles, like in yodeling, it is important to emphasize the break.

● 转个角度，从最高音开始。滑到最低音。感觉怎么样？有些人很容易，有些人则异常困难。这取决于你的身体状况、肌肉力量和协调性。但只要投入一些时间和精力，每个人都有希望掌握它。       
●        Turn it around and start from the top – from your highest note. Slide down to your lowest note. How did it go? For some people it’s easy, for others it is extraordinarily difficult. It depends on how you are put together and what muscle strengths and coordination you have. But  there's hope for everyone to have control of it if you put in some time and effort.

13 利用共鸣增强歌唱效果
13 USING RESONANCE TO ENHANCE SINGING

共振 定义为：
Resonance is defined as :

a：通过补充振动来强化和丰富音乐音调
a: the intensification and enriching of a musical tone by supplementary vibration

b：通过解剖共振腔或腔体（如口腔或鼻腔）中的振动赋予浊音的一种品质
b: a quality imparted to voiced sounds by vibration in anatomical resonating chambers or cavities (such as the mouth or the nasal cavity)

c：丰富性或多样性的品质
c: a quality of richness or variety

“共鸣”，Merriam-Webster.com 词典，https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/resonance。
“resonance,” Merriam-Webster.com Dictionary, https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/resonance.

用日常的话来说，共振意味着声波在从嘴里出来时如何反弹。

To put it in everyday words, resonance means how sound waves bounce around on their way out of your mouth.

歌剧演唱者会尽可能地利用最大的共鸣，让歌声充满整个大厅，并在没有麦克风的情况下与管弦乐队一起演唱。民谣歌手通常使用非常小的共鸣来营造一种亲密的声音。他们必须使用麦克风才能在乐器和宽敞的房间里被听到。大多数其他风格的演唱会使用不同共鸣的组合，有时甚至在同一首歌曲中也会使用。

Opera singers use the biggest resonance possible to fill the hall and sing over the orchestra without mics. Folk singers often use a very small resonance to create an intimate sound. They must use mics to be heard over instruments and in large rooms. Most other styles use a combination of different resonances, sometimes in the same song.

有时人们会认为自己拥有一种真正的声音——一种他们认同的、真实的共鸣。但这极其局限。如果你在足球场上喊叫，你的嘴里会留出很大的空间，像扩音器一样发出声音。试试看。尽可能大声地喊“嘿”。你的嘴巴张得很大吗？这和低声说话完全不同，低声说话时，你的嘴巴很小，振动集中在口腔中间区域。试试用小声的“嘿”来表达。

Sometimes people think they have one, true sound – one authentic resonance that they identify with. But this is ridiculously limiting. If you are shouting across a football field, you use a big space in your mouth and project the sound like a megaphone. Give it a try. Yell “hey” as loud as you can. Was your mouth wide open? That is very different from whispering where you have a small mouth and keep the vibrations in the middle mouth area. Give that a try with a quiet, “hey.”

所以，如果你不限制自己的说话方式，为什么要限制自己的歌唱呢？任何人都可以前一分钟张大嘴巴唱歌，后一分钟又闭上嘴巴唱歌。换句话说，任何人都可以根据歌曲的需要，最大限度地或最大限度地减少共鸣。

So if you don't limit your speaking to one kind, why would you limit your singing? Anyone can sing with a big, open mouth one minute and with a small, closed mouth the next. In other words, anyone can maximize their resonance or minimize their resonance for what the song needs.

活动
Activity

正如你已经读到的，嘴型与此有很大关系。我们来实验一下。

As you've already read, mouth shape has a lot to do with it. Let's experiment with this.

1 重复“呼”3次。先把声音调大一点（呼 - 呼 - 呼）。再把声音调小一点（呼 - 呼 - 呼）。      
1       Repeat a hoot 3 times. Make it big (hoo – hoo – hoooo). Now make it small (hoo – hoo – hoooo).

2 接下来，我们要发出一个长“oo”的音，并尝试在这个元音上进行不同的共鸣。需要的时候吸一口气，然后再吸气。首先，发出一个优美、自然、从口腔中间发出的“oo”音。舌头向下，上颚向上。     
2      Next we are going to hold out a long “oo” and experiment with different resonance on that one vowel. Take a breath whenever you need to and come back in. Start by making a nice, neutral, middle-of-the-mouth “oo.” Your tongue is down, the roof of your mouth is up.

3 现在，将共鸣音向前移动，就像它就在你的嘴唇和牙齿上一样。通过缩小嘴巴后部来实现——将声音向前挤压。如果我说让它变得非常明亮，可能会有帮助。或者，如果我说感觉你把空气集中在鼻子上，也许会有帮助。         
3          Now move that resonance forward, like it's right on your lips and teeth. Do this by making your mouth smaller in the back – squishing the sound forward. It might help if I say to make it very bright. Or perhaps if I say to feel like you are focusing your air toward your nose.

4 接下来，我们要把“oo”音发回嘴部中间，舌头放低，上颚抬起。双手放在颈后，十指交叉。想象着把音发回手部。这可能意味着要把嘴巴和喉咙的后部张得更大。这可能听起来像歌剧里的音，甚至像吞咽音。        
4         Next we are going to send the sound toward the back. Go back to the middle of the mouth “oo,” with the lowered tongue and raised roof. Put your hands behind your neck and interlock your fingers. Visualize sending the sound back to your hands. It probably means opening the back of your mouth and throat more. This might sound like an opera sound or maybe even a swallowed sound.

5 接下来，将双手交叉放在头顶。你能把声音传到他们那里吗？这是一个很好听的高亢声音。这是我个人最喜欢的观感共鸣点。感觉就像在做大脑按摩，声音又好又响亮。然而，这种声音并不适合很多风格。朋克歌手或低吟歌手很少会喜欢这种声音。所以，选择适合你演唱歌曲的共鸣类型吧。     
5      Next put the interlocked hands on top of your head. Can you send the sound up to them? This is a nice tall sound. This is my personal favorite place to visualize the resonance. It feels like I'm getting a brain massage and the sound is nice and big. However it isn't the right sound for a lot of styles. A punk singer or a crooner would rarely want this sound. So pick the type of resonance that is good for the song you are singing.

聆听一些最受尊敬、最具创造力的歌手的歌声，例如戴夫·马修斯、琼尼·米切尔、席琳·迪翁、艾瑞莎·弗兰克林、克里斯汀·肯诺恩斯、纳·京·高尔、布鲁诺·马尔斯和瑞芭·麦肯泰尔等等。你会发现他们运用了各种各样的共鸣，以此来表达情感，展现个性。

Listen to some of the most respected and creative singers; Dave Matthews, Joni Mitchell, Celine Dion, Aretha Franklin, Kristin Chenoweth, Nat King Cole, Bruno Mars, and Reba McEntire to name a few. You'll hear that they use a wide variety of resonances. They use them for expressiveness, for emotion.

一般来说，选择感觉“自然”的共鸣，但不要害怕尝试不同类型的共鸣。是否存在“正确”的声音？没有，音乐会告诉你应该运用什么样的情绪，情绪又会告诉你应该使用什么样的共鸣。如果你使用麦克风，不必担心声音投射过大。如果没有麦克风，请在声道（即你的嘴和喉咙）中留出更大的空间，以最大限度地提高共鸣效果。

In general, choose the resonance that feels “natural” but don't be afraid to experiment with different types of resonance. Is there a “right” kind of sound? No, the music tells you the emotions to use and the emotions tell you what kind of resonance to use. If you are using a mic, don't worry about projecting as much. If you won't have a mic, use a bigger space in your vocal tract, which is your mouth and throat, to maximize your resonance.

14 种音符风格和双元音
14 NOTE STYLES AND DIPHTHONGS

音符风格 ：歌手的风格很大程度上体现在音符的连接方式上。选择有很多，但两种极端是连奏和断奏。

Note Style : A big part of style can be heard in how the singer connects the notes. There are countless options, but the two extremes are legato and staccato .

连奏——流畅的音符

断奏——断断续续或有弹性的音符。

Legato – smooth notes

Staccato – choppy or bouncy notes.

在歌曲开始时，在充分呼吸并获得良好平衡的开始之前，您必须选择 断奏、连奏或介于两者之间的某种方式。

At the beginning of a song, before taking a full breath and getting a nice balanced onset, you will have to choose staccato, legato , or someplace in between.

流畅演唱，又称 连奏——呼吸需要以有节奏、流畅的控制贯穿整个乐句，并将一个音符与下一个音符连接起来，只要这符合歌曲的节奏。连奏可能包括在音高之间滑奏。

Smooth singing, aka legato - The breath needs to carry you through the whole phrase with measured, smooth control, connecting one note to the next as long as it is right for the song. Legato might include sliding between pitches.

弹跳唱法，又称 断奏——呼吸仍然需要以有节奏的控制贯穿整个乐句，但这次音符之间不再连接——音符之间会留出一些空隙。如果你用断奏唱一个以元音开头的单词，请记住你的音首——不要太生硬，也不要太用力——音首要平衡。如果你想复习一下音首，请参阅第六章。断奏要求在音首处敲定准确的音高，不要出现滑音或削音。

Bouncy singing, aka staccato – The breath still needs to take you through the whole phrase with measured control, but this time the notes are not connected – there will be a little space in-between notes. If you are singing staccato on a word that begins with a vowel remember your onsets – not too harsh, not too breathy – balanced onsets. If you want a review of onsets, see Chapter 6. Staccato will require nailing the exact pitch at the onset, without scoops or slides.

考虑音乐的风格很重要。我们连接音符的方式有助于我们定义自己的演唱风格。

Considering the style of music is important. The way we connect our notes helps us define the style we are singing in.

单词发音 ：另一个需要考虑的因素是单词的发音，特别是双元音。双元音是指两个元音连在一起的单词。还有三元音，即三个元音连在一起的单词，例如“our”——“ah-o-oo-er”。

Word Pronunciation : Another consideration is word pronunciation - specifically diphthongs. Diphthongs are words that contain two vowel sounds together. There's also such a thing as triphthongs – that is 3 vowel sounds together as in “our” – “ah-o-oo-er.”

元音发音是地方口音的明显特征，在决定歌曲风格时至关重要。我以“play”这个词为例。当你说“play”时，你会听到两个清晰的元音——“a”和“e”（Pla-ey）。当你用长音唱“play”时，你必须选择如何从第一个元音切换到第二个元音。“a”更长，还是“e”更长，还是它们一样长？（唱“Plaaaaae, Plaeeeee, Plaaaeee”）你还能想到其他双元音吗？Eye、Care、Phone、Babe、Blind。还有很多其他的。

Vowel pronunciation is a dead give-away of a regional accent and is very import when deciding on the style of a song. Let me give you an example in the word “play.”  When you say “play” you hear 2 clear vowel sounds - “a” and “e”  “Pla-ey.” When you sing the word “play” on a long note you have to choose how to switch from the first vowel sound to the second vowel sound. Is the “a” longer, the “e” longer, or are they equal? (sing “Plaaaaae, Plaeeeee, Plaaaeee”) Can you think of other diphthongs? Eye, Care, Phone, Babe, Blind. And there are many more.

一般来说，古典歌唱会拉长第一个元音（像这样唱“Baaaaaebe”），乡村歌唱会拉长第二个元音（“Baeeeeebe”），而流行音乐的元音发音则较为均衡（“Baaeebe”）。所以，如果你在合唱团里，在古典歌曲和流行歌曲或民歌之间来回切换，请注意它们的发音会有什么不同。

As a general rule, classical singing draws out the first vowel sound (sing babe like this: “Baaaaaebe), country singing draws out the second vowel (“Baeeeeebe”) and popular music has balanced vowel sounds (“Baaeebe”). So if you are switching back and forth, say in a choir, between a classical song and a popular or folk song, notice how you will pronounce them differently.
