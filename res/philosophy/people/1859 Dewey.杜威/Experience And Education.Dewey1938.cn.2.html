前言

Preface

所有社会运动都涉及冲突，这些冲突在智力上反映在争论中。如果教育这样一个重要的社会利益不是实践和理论斗争的舞台，那就不是健康的标志。但对于理论，至少对于形成教育哲学的理论，实际冲突和在这些冲突层面上进行的争论只会提出一个问题。明智的教育理论的任务是查明现有冲突的原因，然后，不是偏袒一方，而是指出一个比争论双方的实践和思想所代表的更深、更全面的行动计划。

ALL SOCIAL movements involve conflicts which are reflected intellectually in controversies. It would not be a sign of health if such an important social interest as education were not also an arena of struggles, practical and theoretical. But for theory, at least for the theory that forms a philosophy of education, the practical conflicts and the controversies that are conducted upon the level of these conflicts, only set a problem. It is the business of an intelligent theory of education to ascertain the causes for the conflicts that exist and then, instead of taking one side or the other, to indicate a plan of operations proceeding from a level deeper and more inclusive than is represented by the practices and ideas of the contending parties.

这种对教育哲学工作的表述并不意味着教育哲学应该试图在对立的思想流派之间达成妥协，寻找一种中间途径，也并不意味着教育哲学应该把从各个流派中挑选出来的观点进行折衷组合。它意味着必须引入一种新的观念秩序，从而产生新的实践模式。正是因为这个原因，一旦背离传统和习俗，就很难发展出一种教育哲学。正是因为这个原因，以新的观念秩序为基础的学校的管理比走在老路上的学校的管理要困难得多。因此，朝着新的观念秩序和由它们指导的活动方向前进的每一步，迟早都会要求人们回归过去那些看起来更简单、更基本的观念和实践——正如目前在教育领域试图复兴古希腊和中世纪原则所体现的那样。

This formulation of the business of the philosophy of education does not mean that the latter should attempt to bring about a compromise between opposed schools of thought, to find a via media, nor yet make an eclectic combination of points picked out hither and yon from all schools. It means the necessity of the introduction of a new order of conceptions leading to new modes of practice. It is for this reason that it is so difficult to develop a philosophy of education, the moment tradition and custom are departed from. It is for this reason that the conduct of schools, based upon a new order of conceptions, is so much more difficult than is the management of schools which walk in beaten paths. Hence, every movement in the direction of a new order of ideas and of activities directed by them calls out, sooner or later, a return to what appear to be simpler and more fundamental ideas and practices of the past—as is exemplified at present in education in the attempt to revive the principles of ancient Greece and of the middle ages.

正是在这种背景下，我在本书的结尾建议，那些期待着一场适应现有新社会秩序需求的教育新运动的人，应该从教育本身的角度来思考，而不是从某种关于教育的“主义”的角度来思考，即使是“进步主义”这样的“主义”。因为尽管如此，任何以“主义”为思维和行动的运动都会陷入对其他“主义”的反抗，以至于不知不觉地被它们控制。因为它是通过反抗这些“主义”而不是通过对实际需求、问题和可能性进行全面、建设性的调查来形成自己的原则。本书中提出的论文所具有的任何价值都在于它试图引起人们对教育更大、更深层次问题的关注，从而提出适当的参考框架。

It is in this context that I have suggested at the close of this little volume that those who are looking ahead to a new movement in education, adapted to the existing need for a new social order, should think in terms of Education itself rather than in terms of some ’ism about education, even such an ’ism as “progressivism.” For in spite of itself any movement that thinks and acts in terms of an ’ism becomes so involved in reaction against other ’isms that it is unwittingly controlled by them. For it then forms its principles by reaction against them instead of by a comprehensive, constructive survey of actual needs, problems, and possibilities. Whatever value is possessed by the essay presented in this little volume resides in its attempt to call attention to the larger and deeper issues of Education so as to suggest their proper frame of reference.

约翰·杜威​

JOHN DEWEY

约翰·杜威可能是美国最具影响力的哲学家，他于 1859 年出生于佛蒙特州。从佛蒙特大学毕业后，他获得了约翰霍普金斯大学的博士学位，并在芝加哥大学和哥伦比亚大学等多所主要大学任教。在他 1952 年去世之前，他因其对哲学、心理学和自由主义政治的实用主义方法而获得了国际声誉。他在这些领域的重要著作包括：《我们如何思考》（1910 年）、《哲学的重建》（1920 年）、《经验与自然》（1925 年）和《逻辑：探究理论》（1938 年）。他领导的调查 1936-37 年莫斯科审判的委员会是他一生中对政治行动采取务实态度的一个例子，这种方法使他成为自由主义者中备受争议的人物（尽管共产党人普遍谴责他）。

JOHN DEWEY, probably the most influential of all American philosophers, was born in Vermont in 1859. After graduation from the University of Vermont, he received a Ph.D. from The Johns Hopkins University and taught at a number of major universities, including the University of Chicago and Columbia. Before his death in 1952 he had gained an international reputation for his pragmatic approach to philosophy, psychology, and liberal politics. Among his important books in these areas are: How We Think (1910), Reconstruction in Philosophy (1920), Experience and Nature (1925), and Logic: The Theory of Inquiry (1938). The commission, which he headed, that investigated the Moscow trials of 1936-37 is an example of the practical approach to political action which characterized him throughout his life and made him a controversial figure among liberals (though universally condemned by Communists).

杜威最持久的影响力很可能是在教育领域。杜威相信理论与实践的统一，他不仅就该主题撰写文章，还曾一度参与芝加哥大学儿童“实验学校”的教学。他在该领域的主要早期著作《民主与教育》（1916 年）最全面地阐述了他的观点。本书写于二十多年后，展示了杜威如何根据进步学校的实践经验以及他的理论所受到的批评重新阐述自己的思想。因此，它是二十世纪最重要的教育理论家对教育最简洁的陈述。此外，它可能是杜威对这一主题所做的最简单、最易读的详尽陈述。

In all likelihood, Dewey’s most enduring influence is in the field of education. Believing in the unity of theory and practice, Dewey not only wrote on the subject, but for a time participated in the “laboratory school” for children connected with the University of Chicago. His chief early work in this field, Democracy and Education (1916), was the most comprehensive statement of his position. The present work, written more than two decades later, shows how Dewey reformulated his ideas as a result of the intervening experience of the progressive schools and in the light of the criticisms his theories had received. Consequently, it represents the best concise statement on education by the most important educational theorist of the twentieth century. Moreover, it is probably the simplest and most readable extended statement on this subject that Dewey ever made.

编辑前言

Editorial Foreword

《经验与教育》完成了 Kappa Delta Pi 讲座系列的第一个十年周期。因此，本书在一定程度上是纪念杜威博士作为该协会的第一位和第十位讲师的周年出版物。虽然与作者的其他作品相比，《经验与教育》很简短，但它是对教育哲学的重大贡献。在普遍混乱的背景下，令人遗憾的是，这种混乱分散了美国教育的力量，并抬高了相互冲突的忠诚度标签，这本小册子为统一的教育阵线提供了明确而明确的指导。由于“新”教育的教师公开应用杜威博士的教诲，并强调经验、实验、有目的的学习、自由和其他众所周知的“进步教育”概念，因此了解杜威博士本人对当前教育实践的反应是有益的。为了清晰的理解和共同努力，Kappa Delta Pi 执行委员会要求杜威博士讨论一些目前尚无定论的问题，这些问题目前将美国教育分成了两个阵营，从而削弱了美国教育，而此时美国教育需要全力以赴，引导一个困惑的国家走出社会变革的危险。

Experience and Education completes the first ten-year cycle of the Kappa Delta Pi Lecture Series. The present volume therefore is, in part, an anniversary publication honoring Dr. Dewey as the Society’s first and tenth lecturer. Although brief, as compared with the author’s other works, Experience and Education is a major contribution to educational philosophy. Appearing in the midst of widespread confusion, which regrettably has scattered the forces of American education and exalted labels of conflicting loyalties, this little volume offers clear and certain guidance toward a united educational front. Inasmuch as teachers of the “new” education have avowedly applied the teachings of Dr. Dewey and emphasized experience, experiment, purposeful learning, freedom, and other well-known concepts of “progressive education,” it is well to learn how Dr. Dewey himself reacts to current educational practices. In the interest of clear understanding and a union of effort the Executive Council of Kappa Delta Pi requested Dr. Dewey to discuss some of the moot questions that now divide American education into two camps and thereby weaken it at a time when its full strength is needed in guiding a bewildered nation through the hazards of social change.

《经验与教育》对“传统”和“进步”教育进行了清晰的分析。书中描述了两者的根本缺陷。传统学校依靠学科或文化遗产来传授内容，而“新”学校则强调学习者的冲动和兴趣以及不断变化的社会中当前的问题。这两套价值观本身都不够充分。两者都是必不可少的。良好的教育经验首先涉及学习者与所学内容之间的连续性和互动。传统课程无疑需要严格的管制和忽视儿童天性能力和兴趣的纪律。然而，今天对这种学校教育的反应往往助长了另一个极端——不成熟的课程、过度的个人主义和自发性，这是一种欺骗性的自由指标。杜威博士坚持认为，无论是旧教育还是新教育都不充分。每一种都是错误的教育，因为它们都没有应用精心开发的经验哲学原则。本书的许多页面说明了经验的意义及其与教育的关系。

Experience and Education is a lucid analysis of both “traditional” and “progressive” education. The fundamental defects of each are here described. Where the traditional school relied upon subjects or the cultural heritage for its content, the “new” school has exalted the learner’s impulse and interest and the current problems of a changing society. Neither of these sets of values is sufficient unto itself. Both are essential. Sound educational experience involves, above all, continuity and interaction between the learner and what is learned. The traditional curriculum undoubtedly entailed rigid regimentation and a discipline that ignored the capacities and interests of child nature. Today, however, the reaction to this type of schooling often fosters the other extreme—inchoate curriculum, excessive individualism, and a spontaneity which is a deceptive index of freedom. Dr. Dewey insists that neither the old nor the new education is adequate. Each is mis-educative because neither of them applies the principles of a carefully developed philosophy of experience. Many pages of the present volume illustrate the meaning of experience and its relation to education.

杜威博士反对那些表达和延长分裂的标签，他认为教育是人类研究世界、积累意义和价值知识的科学方法，而这些成果是批判性研究和智慧生活的数据。科学探究的趋势是朝着知识体系发展，需要将其理解为指导进一步探究的手段。因此，科学家不会将自己的研究局限于发现的问题，而是继续研究问题的性质、它们的年代、条件和意义。为此，他可能需要回顾相关的知识储备。因此，教育必须采用渐进式的主题组织方式，以便对主题的理解可以阐明问题的意义和重要性。科学研究​​可以带来并扩大经验，但这种经验只有在它依赖于重要知识的连续性以及这种知识改变或“调节”学习者的观点、态度和技能的程度上才具有教育意义。因此，真正的学习情况有纵向和横向两个维度。它既是历史的，又是社会的；它既有序，又充满活力。

Frowning upon labels that express and prolong schism, Dr. Dewey interprets education as the scientific method by means of which man studies the world, acquires cumulatively knowledge of meanings and values, these outcomes, however, being data for critical study and intelligent living. The tendency of scientific inquiry is toward a body of knowledge which needs to be understood as the means whereby further inquiry may be directed. Hence the scientist, instead of confining his investigation to problems as they are discovered, proceeds to study the nature of problems, their age, conditions, significance. To this end he may need to review related stores of knowledge. Consequently, education must employ progressive organization of subject-matter in order that the understanding of this subject-matter may illumine the meaning and significance of the problems. Scientific study leads to and enlarges experience, but this experience is educative only to the degree that it rests upon a continuity of significant knowledge and to the degree that this knowledge modifies or “modulates” the learner’s outlook, attitude, and skill. The true learning situation, then, has longitudinal and lateral dimensions. It is both historical and social. It is orderly and dynamic.

此处引人入胜的页面正等待着众多正在热切寻求可靠指导的教育工作者和教师。《经验与教育》提供了坚实的基础，在此基础上，他们可以团结一致地推广一种尊重所有经验来源并建立在积极而非消极的经验与教育哲学基础上的美国教育体系。在这种积极哲学的指导下，美国教育工作者将消除其有争议的标签，并团结一致地为更美好的明天而努力工作。

Arresting pages here await the many educators and teachers who are earnestly seeking reliable guidance at this time. Experience and Education provides a firm foundation upon which they may unitedly promote an American educational system which respects all sources of experience and rests upon a positive—not a negative—philosophy of experience and education. Directed by such a positive philosophy, American educators will erase their contentious labels and in solid ranks labor in behalf of a better tomorrow.

A LFRED L. HALL -Q UEST ， Kappa

第 1 章

Chapter 1

传统教育与进步教育

Traditional vs. Progressive Education

人类喜欢用极端对立的思维方式来思考。它习惯于用非此即彼的方式表述自己的信念，在这两种方式之间，它不承认任何中间可能性。当被迫承认极端是无法采取的，它仍然倾向于认为它们在理论上都是正确的，但当涉及到实际问题时，情况迫使我们妥协。教育哲学也不例外。教育理论的历史以两种观点的对立为标志：教育是内在的发展，而教育是外在的形成；教育是基于自然禀赋的，而教育是克服自然倾向并代之以在外部压力下养成的习惯的过程。

MANKIND likes to think in terms of extreme opposites. It is given to formulating its beliefs in terms of Either-Ors, between which it recognizes no intermediate possibilities. When forced to recognize that the extremes cannot be acted upon, it is still inclined to hold that they are all right in theory but that when it comes to practical matters circumstances compel us to compromise. Educational philosophy is no exception. The history of educational theory is marked by opposition between the idea that education is development from within and that it is formation from without; that it is based upon natural endowments and that education is a process of overcoming natural inclination and substituting in its place habits acquired under external pressure.

目前，就学校的实际事务而言，这种对立往往表现为传统教育与进步教育之间的对比。如果将前者的基本思想广泛地阐述出来，而不加任何准确陈述所需的条件，那么它们大致如下：教育的主题包括过去已经形成的知识和技能；因此，学校的主要任务是将它们传授给新一代。过去，还制定了行为标准和规则；道德训练包括养成符合这些规则和标准的行为习惯。最后，学校组织的一般模式（我指的是学生之间以及学生与教师之间的关系）使学校成为一种与其他社会机构截然不同的机构。想象一下普通教室，它的时间表、分类方案、考试和晋升方案、秩序规则，我想你就会明白“组织模式”的含义。如果你将这一场景与家庭中发生的事情进行对比，你就会理解学校作为一种与任何其他形式的社会组织有着鲜明区别的机构的意义。

At present, the opposition, so far as practical affairs of the school are concerned, tends to take the form of contrast between traditional and progressive education. If the underlying ideas of the former are formulated broadly, without the qualifications required for accurate statement, they are found to be about as follows: The subject-matter of education consists of bodies of information and of skills that have been worked out in the past; therefore, the chief business of the school is to transmit them to the new generation. In the past, there have also been developed standards and rules of conduct; moral training consists in forming habits of action in conformity with these rules and standards. Finally, the general pattern of school organization (by which I mean the relations of pupils to one another and to the teachers) constitutes the school a kind of institution sharply marked off from other social institutions. Call up in imagination the ordinary schoolroom, its time-schedules, schemes of classification, of examination and promotion, of rules of order, and I think you will grasp what is meant by “pattern of organization.” If then you contrast this scene with what goes on in the family, for example, you will appreciate what is meant by the school being a kind of institution sharply marked off from any other form of social organization.

上述三个特点确定了教学和训练的目的和方法。主要目的或目标是通过获取组织有序的信息体系和掌握理解教学内容的技能，为年轻人承担未来的责任和获得成功做好准备。由于教学内容和正确行为的标准都是从过去传承下来的，因此学生的态度总体上必须是温顺、接受和服从的。书籍，尤其是教科书，是过去知识和智慧的主要代表，而教师则是让学生与材料有效联系的机构。教师是传播知识和技能以及执行行为准则的代理人。

The three characteristics just mentioned fix the aims and methods of instruction and discipline. The main purpose or objective is to prepare the young for future responsibilities and for success in life, by means of acquisition of the organized bodies of information and prepared forms of skill which comprehend the material of instruction. Since the subject-matter as well as standards of proper conduct are handed down from the past, the attitude of pupils must, upon the whole, be one of docility, receptivity, and obedience. Books, especially textbooks, are the chief representatives of the lore and wisdom of the past, while teachers are the organs through which pupils are brought into effective connection with the material. Teachers are the agents through which knowledge and skills are communicated and rules of conduct enforced.

我做这个简短的总结，并不是为了批评其背后的哲学。所谓新教育和进步学校的兴起，本身就是对传统教育不满的产物。实际上，这是对传统教育的批评。如果把隐含的批评明确化，其内容大致如下：传统体系本质上是一种自上而下、从外部强加的体系。它将成人的标准、科目和方法强加给那些成长缓慢的年轻人。这种差距是如此之大，以至于所需的科目、学习和行为方法与年轻人现有的能力格格不入。它们超出了年轻学习者已有经验的范围。因此，必须强加这些；尽管优秀的教师会使用艺术手段来掩盖这种强加，以减轻其明显的野蛮特征。

I have not made this brief summary for the purpose of criticizing the underlying philosophy. The rise of what is called new education and progressive schools is of itself a product of discontent with traditional education. In effect it is a criticism of the latter. When the implied criticism is made explicit it reads somewhat as follows: The traditional scheme is, in essence, one of imposition from above and from outside. It imposes adult standards, subject-matter, and methods upon those who are only growing slowly toward maturity. The gap is so great that the required subject-matter, the methods of learning and of behaving are foreign to the existing capacities of the young. They are beyond the reach of the experience the young learners already possess. Consequently, they must be imposed; even though good teachers will use devices of art to cover up the imposition so as to relieve it of obviously brutal features.

但是，成年人的经验和能力与年轻人的差距是如此之大，以至于这种情况本身就禁止学生积极参与所教内容的发展。他们的任务是做和学，就像六百人的任务一样，做完就死。这里的学习意味着掌握书本上和长辈头脑中已经包含的内容。此外，所教的内容被认为基本上是静态的。它被当作成品来教授，很少考虑它最初的构建方式或未来必定会发生的变化。在很大程度上，它是社会的文化产物，这些社会认为未来将与过去大体相同，然而，在一个变化是常态而不是例外的社会中，它却被用作教育的食粮。

But the gulf between the mature or adult products and the experience and abilities of the young is so wide that the very situation forbids much active participation by pupils in the development of what is taught. Theirs is to do—and learn, as it was the part of the six hundred to do and die. Learning here means acquisition of what already is incorporated in books and in the heads of the elders. Moreover, that which is taught is thought of as essentially static. It is taught as a finished product, with little regard either to the ways in which it was originally built up or to changes that will surely occur in the future. It is to a large extent the cultural product of societies that assumed the future would be much like the past, and yet it is used as educational food in a society where change is the rule, not the exception.

如果试图阐明新教育实践中隐含的教育哲学，我想，我们可能会在现存的各种进步学校中发现某些共同的原则。自上而下的强加与个性的表达和培养相对立；外部纪律与自由活动相对立；从课本和教师那里学习，通过经验学习；通过练习获得孤立的技能和技巧与将它们作为达到具有直接生命吸引力的目的的手段获得相对立；为或多或少遥远的未来做准备与充分利用当前生活的机会相对立；静态的目标和材料与对变化的世界的了解相对立。

If one attempts to formulate the philosophy of education implicit in the practices of the new education, we may, I think, discover certain common principles amid the variety of progressive schools now existing. To imposition from above is opposed expression and cultivation of individuality; to external discipline is opposed free activity; to learning from texts and teachers, learning through experience; to acquisition of isolated skills and techniques by drill, is opposed acquisition of them as means of attaining ends which make direct vital appeal; to preparation for a more or less remote future is opposed making the most of the opportunities of present life; to static aims and materials is opposed acquaintance with a changing world.

现在，所有原则本身都是抽象的。它们只有在应用其结果时才会变得具体。正因为所阐述的原则是如此基本和深远，所以一切都取决于在学校和家庭中付诸实践时对它们的解释。正是在这一点上，前面提到的非此即彼的哲学就变得特别中肯。新教育的一般哲学可能是合理的，但抽象原则的差异不会决定所涉及的道德和智力偏好在实践中应如何实现。新运动总是存在这样的危险：在拒绝它所取代的目标和方法时，它可能会消极地而不是积极和建设性地发展其原则。然后它在实践中从被拒绝的东西而不是从其自身哲学的建设性发展中获取线索。

Now, all principles by themselves are abstract. They become concrete only in the consequences which result from their application. Just because the principles set forth are so fundamental and far-reaching, everything depends upon the interpretation given them as they are put into practice in the school and the home. It is at this point that the reference made earlier to Either-Or philosophies becomes peculiarly pertinent. The general philosophy of the new education may be sound, and yet the difference in abstract principles will not decide the way in which the moral and intellectual preference involved shall be worked out in practice. There is always the danger in a new movement that in rejecting the aims and methods of that which it would supplant, it may develop its principles negatively rather than positively and constructively. Then it takes its clew in practice from that which is rejected instead of from the constructive development of its own philosophy.

我认为，新哲学的基本统一性在于，实际经验和教育过程之间存在着密切而必要的关系。如果这是真的，那么，其基本思想的积极和建设性发展取决于对经验的正确认识。以有组织的教材问题为例——稍后将详细讨论。进步教育的问题是：教材和组织在经验中的地位和意义是什么？教材如何发挥作用？经验中是否有任何内在的东西倾向于逐步组织其内容？当经验材料没有逐步组织时，会产生什么结果？一种以拒绝、完全反对为基础的哲学将忽视这些问题。它会倾向于认为，因为旧教育是建立在现成的组织基础上的，所以它足以完全拒绝组织原则，而不是努力发现它的含义以及如何在经验的基础上实现它。我们可以仔细研究新旧教育之间的所有不同点，并得出类似的结论。当拒绝外部控制时，问题就变成了寻找经验中固有的控制因素。当拒绝外部权威时，并不意味着应该拒绝所有权威，而是需要寻找更有效的权威来源。由于旧式教育将成年人的知识、方法和行为规则强加给年轻人，因此，除非基于极端的“非此即彼”哲学，否则，这并不意味着成年人的知识和技能对未成年人的经验没有指导价值。相反，以个人经验为基础的教育可能意味着成熟和未成年人之间的接触比传统学校中任何时候都多，更密切，因此他人的指导更多，而不是更少。那么，问题是：如何在不违反通过个人经验学习的原则的情况下建立这些联系。解决这个问题需要对个人经验构成中起作用的社会因素有深思熟虑的哲学。

I take it that the fundamental unity of the newer philosophy is found in the idea that there is an intimate and necessary relation between the processes of actual experience and education. If this be true, then a positive and constructive development of its own basic idea depends upon having a correct idea of experience. Take, for example, the question of organized subject-matter—which will be discussed in some detail later. The problem for progressive education is: What is the place and meaning of subject-matter and of organization within experience? How does subject-matter function? Is there anything inherent in experience which tends towards progressive organization of its contents? What results follow when the materials of experience are not progressively organized? A philosophy which proceeds on the basis of rejection, of sheer opposition, will neglect these questions. It will tend to suppose that because the old education was based on ready-made organization, therefore it suffices to reject the principle of organization in toto, instead of striving to discover what it means and how it is to be attained on the basis of experience. We might go through all the points of difference between the new and the old education and reach similar conclusions. When external control is rejected, the problem becomes that of finding the factors of control that are inherent within experience. When external authority is rejected, it does not follow that all authority should be rejected, but rather that there is need to search for a more effective source of authority. Because the older education imposed the knowledge, methods, and the rules of conduct of the mature person upon the young, it does not follow, except upon the basis of the extreme Either-Or philosophy, that the knowledge and skill of the mature person has no directive value for the experience of the immature. On the contrary, basing education upon personal experience may mean more multiplied and more intimate contacts between the mature and the immature than ever existed in the traditional school, and consequently more, rather than less, guidance by others. The problem, then, is: how these contacts can be established without violating the principle of learning through personal experience. The solution of this problem requires a well thought-out philosophy of the social factors that operate in the constitution of individual experience.

上述评论表明，新教育的一般原则本身并不能解决进步学校实际或实际经营和管理的任何问题。相反，它们提出了新的问题，这些问题必须基于新的经验哲学来解决。当人们认为只要拒绝旧教育的思想和做法，然后走向另一个极端就足够了时，这些问题甚至没有被认识到，更不用说解决了。然而，我相信你会理解我说的许多新学校往往很少或根本不重视有组织的学习科目；他们的做法好像成人的任何形式的指导和引导都是对个人自由的侵犯，好像教育应该关注现在和未来的想法意味着了解过去在教育中几乎没有或根本没有作用。不夸大这些缺陷，它们至少说明了什么是消极的教育理论和实践，或者对教育现状的反应，而不是在经验理论及其教育潜力的基础上积极和建设性地发展目的、方法和主题。

What is indicated in the foregoing remarks is that the general principles of the new education do not of themselves solve any of the problems of the actual or practical conduct and management of progressive schools. Rather, they set new problems which have to be worked out on the basis of a new philosophy of experience. The problems are not even recognized, to say nothing of being solved, when it is assumed that it suffices to reject the ideas and practices of the old education and then go to the opposite extreme. Yet I am sure that you will appreciate what is meant when I say that many of the newer schools tend to make little or nothing of organized subject-matter of study; to proceed as if any form of direction and guidance by adults were an invasion of individual freedom, and as if the idea that education should be concerned with the present and future meant that acquaintance with the past has little or no role to play in education. Without pressing these defects to the point of exaggeration, they at least illustrate what is meant by a theory and practice of education which proceeds negatively or by reaction against what has been current in education rather than by a positive and constructive development of purposes, methods, and subject-matter on the foundation of a theory of experience and its educational potentialities.

毫不夸张地说，一种自称以自由思想为基础的教育哲学可能会变得像受到反对的传统教育一样教条。因为任何理论和实践都是教条的，如果不以对其基本原则的批判性审视为基础。假设新教育强调学习者的自由。很好。现在出现了一个问题。自由意味着什么？在什么条件下才能实现自由？假设传统学校中常见的那种外部强加限制了而不是促进了年轻人的智力和道德发展。同样，很好。认识到这一严重缺陷提出了一个问题。教师和书籍在促进未成年人的教育发展方面究竟起着什么作用？承认传统教育以与过去紧密相连的事实和思想作为研究主题，对处理现在和未来的问题几乎没有帮助。很好。现在我们面临的问题是发现过去的成就与现在的问题之间在经验中实际存在的联系。我们面临的问题是，如何将对过去的了解转化为有效应对未来的有力工具。我们可能会拒绝将对过去的了解作为教育的目的，从而只强调其作为一种手段的重要性。当我们这样做时，我们面临教育史上的一个新问题：年轻人如何才能熟悉过去，使这种熟悉成为欣赏活生生的当下的有力媒介？

It is not too much to say that an educational philosophy which professes to be based on the idea of freedom may become as dogmatic as ever was the traditional education which is reacted against. For any theory and set of practices is dogmatic which is not based upon critical examination of its own underlying principles. Let us say that the new education emphasizes the freedom of the learner. Very well. A problem is now set. What does freedom mean and what are the conditions under which it is capable of realization? Let us say that the kind of external imposition which was so common in the traditional school limited rather than promoted the intellectual and moral development of the young. Again, very well. Recognition of this serious defect sets a problem. Just what is the role of the teacher and of books in promoting the educational development of the immature? Admit that traditional education employed as the subject-matter for study facts and ideas so bound up with the past as to give little help in dealing with the issues of the present and future. Very well. Now we have the problem of discovering the connection which actually exists within experience between the achievements of the past and the issues of the present. We have the problem of ascertaining how acquaintance with the past may be translated into a potent instrumentality for dealing effectively with the future. We may reject knowledge of the past as the end of education and thereby only emphasize its importance as a means. When we do that we have a problem that is new in the story of education: How shall the young become acquainted with the past in such a way that the acquaintance is a potent agent in appreciation of the living present?

第 2 章

Chapter 2

经验理论的必要性

The Need of a Theory of Experience

简而言之，我要说的是，拒绝传统教育的理念和实践，会给那些相信新教育的人带来一种新的困难教育问题。在我们认识到这一事实之前，在我们彻底认识到背离旧教育并不能解决任何问题之前，我们将盲目而混乱地行事。因此，下文旨在指出新教育面临的一些主要问题，并提出寻求解决这些问题的主要思路。我认为，在所有不确定性中，有一个永久的参考框架：即教育与个人经验之间的有机联系；或者，新教育哲学致力于某种经验主义和实验主义哲学。但经验和实验并不是不言自明的思想。相反，它们的意义是需要探讨的问题的一部分。要了解经验主义的意义，我们需要了解经验是什么。

IN SHORT, the point I am making is that rejection of the philosophy and practice of traditional education sets a new type of difficult educational problem for those who believe in the new type of education. We shall operate blindly and in confusion until we recognize this fact; until we thoroughly appreciate that departure from the old solves no problems. What is said in the following pages is, accordingly, intended to indicate some of the main problems with which the newer education is confronted and to suggest the main lines along which their solution is to be sought. I assume that amid all uncertainties there is one permanent frame of reference: namely, the organic connection between education and personal experience; or, that the new philosophy of education is committed to some kind of empirical and experimental philosophy. But experience and experiment are not self-explanatory ideas. Rather, their meaning is part of the problem to be explored. To know the meaning of empiricism we need to understand what experience is.

认为所有真正的教育都来自经验的信念并不意味着所有的经验都具有真正的或同等的教育意义。经验和教育不能直接等同起来。因为有些经验是错误的教育。任何会阻碍或扭曲进一步经验增长的经验都是错误的教育。经验可能会让人变得麻木不仁；它可能会导致缺乏敏感性和反应能力。然后，未来获得更丰富经验的可能性就会受到限制。同样，给定的经验可能会增加一个人在某个特定方向上的自动技能，但却倾向于让他陷入困境；其效果再次是缩小进一步经验的范围。一种体验可能立即令人愉快，但却会促进形成一种懒散和粗心的态度；这种态度会改变后续体验的质量，从而阻止一个人从中获得他们必须付出的东西。同样，经验可能彼此脱节，尽管每一种体验本身都令人愉快甚至令人兴奋，但它们并没有相互联系。精力会随之消散，人会变得心不在焉。每一次经历可能都生动、生动、“有趣”，但它们的脱节可能会人为地产生分散、分裂、离心的习惯。养成这种习惯的后果是无法控制未来的经历。然后，它们会以享受或不满和反抗的方式被带走。在这种情况下，谈论自我控制是毫无意义的。

The belief that all genuine education comes about through experience does not mean that all experiences are genuinely or equally educative. Experience and education cannot be directly equated to each other. For some experiences are mis-educative. Any experience is mis-educative that has the effect of arresting or distorting the growth of further experience. An experience may be such as to engender callousness; it may produce lack of sensitivity and of responsiveness. Then the possibilities of having richer experience in the future are restricted. Again, a given experience may increase a person’s automatic skill in a particular direction and yet tend to land him in a groove or rut; the effect again is to narrow the field of further experience. An experience may be immediately enjoyable and yet promote the formation of a slack and careless attitude; this attitude then operates to modify the quality of subsequent experiences so as to prevent a person from getting out of them what they have to give. Again, experiences may be so disconnected from one another that, while each is agreeable or even exciting in itself, they are not linked cumulatively to one another. Energy is then dissipated and a person becomes scatterbrained. Each experience may be lively, vivid, and “interesting,” and yet their disconnectedness may artificially generate dispersive, disintegrated, centrifugal habits. The consequence of formation of such habits is inability to control future experiences. They are then taken, either by way of enjoyment or of discontent and revolt, just as they come. Under such circumstances, it is idle to talk of self-control.

传统教育提供了大量上述类型的经验例子。即使心照不宣地认为传统教室不是学生获得经验的地方，也是大错特错。然而，当进步教育作为一种通过经验学习的计划与旧教育形成鲜明对比时，人们就心照不宣地认为这一点。正确的攻击路线是，学生和教师所获得的经验大多是错误的。例如，有多少学生对思想变得麻木不仁，有多少学生因为他们的学习方式而失去了学习的动力？有多少学生通过自动训练获得了特殊技能，以至于他们的判断力和在新情况下明智行事的能力受到限制？有多少人将学习过程与无聊和乏味联系起来？有多少人发现他们所学的东西与学校以外的生活环境格格不入，以至于他们无法控制后者？有多少人将书本与枯燥乏味的苦差事联系起来，以至于他们“习惯”于除了华而不实的读物之外的所有读物？

Traditional education offers a plethora of examples of experiences of the kinds just mentioned. It is a great mistake to suppose, even tacitly, that the traditional schoolroom was not a place in which pupils had experiences. Yet this is tacitly assumed when progressive education as a plan of learning by experience is placed in sharp opposition to the old. The proper line of attack is that the experiences which were had, by pupils and teachers alike, were largely of a wrong kind. How many students, for example, were rendered callous to ideas, and how many lost the impetus to learn because of the way in which learning was experienced by them? How many acquired special skills by means of automatic drill so that their power of judgment and capacity to act intelligently in new situations was limited? How many came to associate the learning process with ennui and boredom? How many found what they did learn so foreign to the situations of life outside the school as to give them no power of control over the latter? How many came to associate books with dull drudgery, so that they were “conditioned” to all but flashy reading matter?

我提出这些问题，并非是为了全盘否定旧教育，而是为了另一个目的。首先，是为了强调这样一个事实：传统学校的年轻人确实有经验；其次，问题不在于缺乏经验，而在于经验的缺陷和错误——从与进一步经验的联系的角度来看，这些经验是错误的和有缺陷的。这一点的积极一面对于进步教育来说更为重要。仅仅强调经验的必要性，甚至强调经验中的活动是不够的。一切都取决于所获得经验的质量。任何经验的质量都有两个方面。一方面是直接的令人愉快或不愉快的方面，另一方面是它对以后的经验的影响。第一个方面是显而易见的，很容易判断。经验的影响不是从表面上看出来的。它给教育者提出了一个问题。他的职责是安排这样一种经验，这种经验虽然不会让学生反感，而是让他参与活动，但不仅仅是直接令人愉快，因为它们促进了未来理想的经验。正如没有人为自己而生或死，也没有经验为自己而生或死。完全独立于欲望或意图，每一种经验都会在进一步的经验中继续存在。因此，基于经验的教育的核心问题是选择在后续经验中富有成效和创造性地存在的当前经验。

If I ask these questions, it is not for the sake of wholesale condemnation of the old education. It is for quite another purpose. It is to emphasize the fact, first, that young people in traditional schools do have experiences; and, secondly, that the trouble is not the absence of experiences, but their defective and wrong character—wrong and defective from the standpoint of connection with further experience. The positive side of this point is even more important in connection with progressive education. It is not enough to insist upon the necessity of experience, nor even of activity in experience. Everything depends upon the quality of the experience which is had. The quality of any experience has two aspects. There is an immediate aspect of agreeableness or disagreeableness, and there is its influence upon later experiences. The first is obvious and easy to judge. The effect of an experience is not borne on its face. It sets a problem to the educator. It is his business to arrange for the kind of experiences which, while they do not repel the student, but rather engage his activities are, nevertheless, more than immediately enjoyable since they promote having desirable future experiences. Just as no man lives or dies to himself, so no experience lives and dies to itself. Wholly independent of desire or intent, every experience lives on in further experiences. Hence the central problem of an education based upon experience is to select the kind of present experiences that live fruitfully and creatively in subsequent experiences.

稍后，我将更详细地讨论经验连续性的原则，或者可以称为经验连续性的原则。在这里，我只想强调这一原则对于教育经验哲学的重要性。教育哲学，像任何理论一样，必须用文字、用符号来表达。但只要它不仅仅是文字，它就是一个实施教育的计划。像任何计划一样，它必须参考要做什么和如何做来制定。人们越是明确和真诚地认为教育是在经验之内、通过经验、为经验而发展，就越需要清楚地了解经验是什么。除非经验被设想为决定科目、教学和纪律方法以及学校的物质设备和社会组织的计划，否则它就完全是空谈。它被简化为一种词语形式，这种词语形式可能令人激动，但任何其他词语组合都可以同样代替它，除非它们表示要发起和执行的操作。虽然传统教育是一种例行公事，其中的计划和方案是从过去流传下来的，但这并不意味着进步教育就是无计划的即兴发挥。

Later, I shall discuss in more detail the principle of the continuity of experience or what may be called the experiential continuum. Here I wish simply to emphasize the importance of this principle for the philosophy of educative experience. A philosophy of education, like any theory, has to be stated in words, in symbols. But so far as it is more than verbal it is a plan for conducting education. Like any plan, it must be framed with reference to what is to be done and how it is to be done. The more definitely and sincerely it is held that education is a development within, by, and for experience, the more important it is that there shall be clear conceptions of what experience is. Unless experience is so conceived that the result is a plan for deciding upon subject-matter, upon methods of instruction and discipline, and upon material equipment and social organization of the school, it is wholly in the air. It is reduced to a form of words which may be emotionally stirring but for which any other set of words might equally well be substituted unless they indicate operations to be initiated and executed. Just because traditional education was a matter of routine in which the plans and programs were handed down from the past, it does not follow that progressive education is a matter of planless improvisation.

传统学校不需要任何始终如一的教育哲学，它所需要的只是一套抽象的词汇，如文化、纪律、我们伟大的文化遗产等，实际的指导不是从这些词汇中得出的，而是从习俗和既定的惯例中得出的。正因为进步的学校不能依赖既定的传统和制度习惯，它们就必须或多或少地杂乱无章地进行，或者由思想来指导，当这些思想清晰而连贯时，就形成了一种教育哲学。反抗传统学校特有的组织方式，就要求建立一种基于思想的组织方式。我认为，只要对教育史稍有了解，就可以证明只有教育改革者和创新者才感到需要一种教育哲学。那些坚持既定制度的人只需要几句好听的话来证明现有的做法是合理的。真正的工作是由固定下来的、制度化的习惯来完成的。进步教育的教训是，它迫切需要一种基于经验哲学的教育哲学，这种教育哲学比以前的创新者所承担的更为紧迫。

The traditional school could get along without any consistently developed philosophy of education. About all it required in that line was a set of abstract words like culture, discipline, our great cultural heritage, etc., actual guidance being derived not from them but from custom and established routines. Just because progressive schools cannot rely upon established traditions and institutional habits, they must either proceed more or less haphazardly or be directed by ideas which, when they are made articulate and coherent, form a philosophy of education. Revolt against the kind of organization characteristic of the traditional school constitutes a demand for a kind of organization based upon ideas. I think that only slight acquaintance with the history of education is needed to prove that educational reformers and innovators alone have felt the need for a philosophy of education. Those who adhered to the established system needed merely a few fine-sounding words to justify existing practices. The real work was done by habits which were so fixed as to be institutional. The lesson for progressive education is that it requires in an urgent degree, a degree more pressing than was incumbent upon former innovators, a philosophy of education based upon a philosophy of experience.

我顺便提到，这里所说的哲学，套用林肯关于民主的名言，是一种教育，一种教育，一种教育的实践，一种教育的体验。这些词，教育的实践，一种教育的体验，一种教育的体验。这些词，教育的实践，一种教育的体验，一种教育的体验，没有一个词是自明的。每一个词都是一个挑战，去发现和实施一种秩序和组织的原则，这种原则源于对教育经验意义的理解。

I remarked incidentally that the philosophy in question is, to paraphrase the saying of Lincoln about democracy, one of education of, by, and for experience. No one of these words, of, by, or for, names anything which is self-evident. Each of them is a challenge to discover and put into operation a principle of order and organization which follows from understanding what educative experience signifies.

因此，与传统教育相比，制定适合新教育的材料、方法和社会关系是一项更为困难的任务。我认为，在管理进步学校时遇到的许多困难和对它们的许多批评都源于此。当人们认为新教育比旧教育更容易时，困难就会加剧，批评也会增多。我想，这种信念或多或少是流行的。也许它再次体现了非此即彼的哲学，这种哲学源于这样一种观点：几乎所有需要做的就是不要做传统学校所做的事情。

It is, accordingly, a much more difficult task to work out the kinds of materials, of methods, and of social relationships that are appropriate to the new education than is the case with traditional education. I think many of the difficulties experienced in the conduct of progressive schools and many of the criticisms leveled against them arise from this source. The difficulties are aggravated and the criticisms are increased when it is supposed that the new education is somehow easier than the old. This belief is, I imagine, more or less current. Perhaps it illustrates again the Either-Or philosophy, springing from the idea that about all which is required is not to do what is done in traditional schools.

我欣然承认，新教育在原则上比旧教育简单。它与成长的原则相一致，而旧教育在选择和安排科目和方法时，有许多人为的东西，而人为的东西总是导致不必要的复杂性。但是简单和简单并不相同。发现真正简单的东西并根据发现采取行动是一项极其困难的任务。一旦人为和复杂的东西在制度上确立下来并根植于习俗和惯例中，那么走上人们走过的道路要比采取新的观点后，弄清楚新观点实际上涉及什么要容易得多。旧的托勒密天文学体系比哥白尼体系更复杂，因为它有周期和本轮。但是，在根据后一种原则组织实际天文现象之前，最简单的做法是遵循旧知识习惯提供的阻力最小的路线。因此，我们回到这一观点：要为学校工作指明新的方向，就需要一种连贯的经验理论，为选择和组织适当的教育方法和材料提供积极的指导。这个过程是缓慢而艰巨的。这是一个成长的问题，有许多障碍往往会阻碍成长，并使它走上错误的路线。

I admit gladly that the new education is simpler in principle than the old. It is in harmony with principles of growth, while there is very much which is artificial in the old selection and arrangement of subjects and methods, and artificiality always leads to unnecessary complexity. But the easy and the simple are not identical. To discover what is really simple and to act upon the discovery is an exceedingly difficult task. After the artificial and complex is once institutionally established and ingrained in custom and routine, it is easier to walk in the paths that have been beaten than it is, after taking a new point of view, to work out what is practically involved in the new point of view. The old Ptolemaic astronomical system was more complicated with its cycles and epicycles than the Copernican system. But until organization of actual astronomical phenomena on the ground of the latter principle had been effected the easiest course was to follow the line of least resistance provided by the old intellectual habit. So we come back to the idea that a coherent theory of experience, affording positive direction to selection and organization of appropriate educational methods and materials, is required by the attempt to give new direction to the work of the schools. The process is a slow and arduous one. It is a matter of growth, and there are many obstacles which tend to obstruct growth and to deflect it into wrong lines.

我稍后会谈及组织。也许，此时此刻，我们只需要说，我们必须摆脱那种以传统教育的组织形式来思考组织的倾向，无论是内容（或主题），还是方法和社会关系。我认为，当前对组织概念的反对很大程度上是由于很难摆脱旧学校研究的图景。一提到“组织”，我们的想象几乎会自动转向我们熟悉的组织形式，在反抗这种组织形式时，我们就会回避任何组织的概念。另一方面，教育反动派正在集结力量，他们利用新型学校缺乏足够的智力和道德组织来证明组织不仅需要组织，而且将任何组织都与实验科学兴起之前建立的组织联系起来。未能在经验和实验的基础上发展组织概念，让反动派轻而易举地获胜。但事实是，经验科学如今提供了在任何领域中都能找到的最好的知识组织类型，这一事实表明，我们这些自称为经验主义者的人没有理由在秩序和组织问题上成为“软弱者”。

I shall have something to say later about organization. All that is needed, perhaps, at this point is to say that we must escape from the tendency to think of organization in terms of the kind of organization, whether of content (or subject-matter), or of methods and social relations, that mark traditional education. I think that a good deal of the current opposition to the idea of organization is due to the fact that it is so hard to get away from the picture of the studies of the old school. The moment “organization” is mentioned imagination goes almost automatically to the kind of organization that is familiar, and in revolting against that we are led to shrink from the very idea of any organization. On the other hand, educational reactionaries, who are now gathering force, use the absence of adequate intellectual and moral organization in the newer type of school as proof not only of the need of organization, but to identify any and every kind of organization with that instituted before the rise of experimental science. Failure to develop a conception of organization upon the empirical and experimental basis gives reactionaries a too easy victory. But the fact that the empirical sciences now offer the best type of intellectual organization which can be found in any field shows that there is no reason why we, who call ourselves emipiricists, should be “pushovers” in the matter of order and organization.

Chapter 3

经验标准

Criteria of Experience

如果关于需要形成经验理论以便教育能够在经验的基础上明智地进行的说法是真的，那么显然，在本次讨论中，下一步要做的就是提出构建这一理论最重要的原则。因此，我不会为进行一定程度的哲学分析而道歉，否则这些分析可能不合时宜。然而，我可以在某种程度上向你保证，这种分析本身并不是目的，而是为了获得标准，以便以后在讨论一些具体的、对大多数人来说更有趣的问题时应用这些标准。

IF THERE IS any truth in what has been said about the need of forming a theory of experience in order that education may be intelligently conducted upon the basis of experience, it is clear that the next thing in order in this discussion is to present the principles that are most significant in framing this theory. I shall not, therefore, apologize for engaging in a certain amount of philosophical analysis, which otherwise might be out of place. I may, however, reassure you to some degree by saying that this analysis is not an end in itself but is engaged in for the sake of obtaining criteria to be applied later in discussion of a number of concrete and, to most persons, more interesting issues.

我已经提到过我所谓的连续性范畴，或经验连续性。正如我所指出的，这一原则涉及每一次试图区分具有教育价值的经验和不具有教育价值的经验。认为这种区分不仅在批评传统教育类型时是必要的，而且在发起和实施不同类型的教育时也是必要的，这似乎是多余的。然而，最好暂时坚持这种区分是必要的这一观点。我想，人们可以放心地认为，进步运动受到推崇的一个原因是，它似乎比传统学校的程序更符合我们人民所信奉的民主理想，因为传统学校的程序带有太多的专制色彩。它受到欢迎的另一个原因是，与传统学校经常出现的严厉政策相比，它的方法更人道。

I have already mentioned what I called the category of continuity, or the experiential continuum. This principle is involved, as I pointed out, in every attempt to discriminate between experiences that are worth while educationally and those that are not. It may seem superfluous to argue that this discrimination is necessary not only in criticizing the traditional type of education but also in initiating and conducting a different type. Nevertheless, it is advisable to pursue for a little while the idea that it is necessary. One may safely assume, I suppose, that one thing which has recommended the progressive movement is that it seems more in accord with the democratic ideal to which our people is committed than do the procedures of the traditional school, since the latter have so much of the autocratic about them. Another thing which has contributed to its favorable reception is that its methods are humane in comparison with the harshness so often attending the policies of the traditional school.

我要提出的问题是，为什么我们更喜欢民主和人道的制度，而不是专制和严酷的制度。我说的“为什么”是指我们喜欢民主和人道制度的理由，而不仅仅是导致我们喜欢民主和人道制度的原因。原因之一可能是，我们不仅在学校里，而且在媒体、讲坛、讲台、法律和立法机构中也都学到，民主是所有社会制度中最好的。我们可能已经从周围环境中吸收了这种观念，以至于它已经成为我们精神和道德构成的习惯性组成部分。但类似的原因也导致不同环境中的其他人得出截然不同的结论——例如，更喜欢法西斯主义。我们喜欢民主和人道制度的原因与我们为什么应该喜欢民主和人道制度的原因不是一回事。

The question I would raise concerns why we prefer democratic and humane arrangements to those which are autocratic and harsh. And by “why,” I mean the reason for preferring them, not just the causes which lead us to the preference. One cause may be that we have been taught not only in the schools but by the press, the pulpit, the platform, and our laws and law-making bodies that democracy is the best of all social institutions. We may have so assimilated this idea from our surroundings that it has become an habitual part of our mental and moral make-up. But similar causes have led other persons in different surroundings to widely varying conclusions—to prefer fascism, for example. The cause for our preference is not the same thing as the reason why we should prefer it.

我的目的不是详细阐述原因。但我要问一个问题：我们能找到任何理由，不归结为这样的信念吗：民主社会安排比非民主和反民主的社会生活形式更能促进人类体验的更高质量，更广泛地获得和享受？尊重个人自由和人类关系的体面和友善的原则最终不是归结为这样的信念吗：与压制、胁迫或武力手段相比，这些东西有助于大多数人获得更高质量的体验？我们偏爱的原因不就是我们相信通过说服达成的相互协商和信念，能够提供比其他方式更高质量的体验吗？

It is not my purpose here to go in detail into the reason. But I would ask a single question: Can we find any reason that does not ultimately come down to the belief that democratic social arrangements promote a better quality of human experience, one which is more widely accessible and enjoyed, than do non-democratic and anti-democratic forms of social life? Does not the principle of regard for individual freedom and for decency and kindliness of human relations come back in the end to the conviction that these things are tributary to a higher quality of experience on the part of a greater number than are methods of repression and coercion or force? Is it not the reason for our preference that we believe that mutual consultation and convictions reached through persuasion, make possible a better quality of experience than can otherwise be provided on any wide scale?

如果这些问题的答案是肯定的（我个人认为，除了这些之外，我们无法证明我们偏爱民主和人道。）那么，我们之所以欢迎进步教育，是因为进步教育依赖和使用人道方法，与民主密切相关，这可以追溯到这样一个事实：人们会区分不同经验的内在价值。因此，我回到以经验连续性原则作为区分标准。

If the answer to these questions is in the affirmative (and personally I do not see how we can justify our preference for democracy and humanity on any other ground), the ultimate reason for hospitality to progressive education, because of its reliance upon and use of humane methods and its kinship to democracy, goes back to the fact that discrimination is made between the inherent values of different experiences. So I come back to the principle of continuity of experience as a criterion of discrimination.

从根本上讲，当习惯被从生物学角度解释时，这一原则建立在习惯这一事实之上。习惯的基本特征是，每一次经历都会改变行为者和经历者，而这种改变，无论我们是否愿意，都会影响后续经历的质量。因为进入这些经历的人是略有不同的。如此理解的习惯原则显然比习惯作为一种或多或少固定的做事方式的普通概念更为深刻，尽管后者是习惯的一个特殊情况。它涵盖了态度的形成，这些态度既是情感的，也是智力的；它涵盖了我们的基本敏感性以及应对和应对生活中遇到的所有条件的方式。从这个角度来看，经验连续性的原则意味着，每一次经历都会从之前的经历中吸取一些东西，并以某种方式改变之后经历的质量。正如诗人所说，

At bottom, this principle rests upon the fact of habit, when habit is interpreted biologically. The basic characteristic of habit is that every experience enacted and undergone modifies the one who acts and undergoes, while this modification affects, whether we wish it or not, the quality of subsequent experiences. For it is a somewhat different person who enters into them. The principle of habit so understood obviously goes deeper than the ordinary conception of a habit as a more or less fixed way of doing things, although it includes the latter as one of its special cases. It covers the formation of attitudes, attitudes that are emotional and intellectual; it covers our basic sensitivities and ways of meeting and responding to all the conditions which we meet in living. From this point of view, the principle of continuity of experience means that every experience both takes up something from those which have gone before and modifies in some way the quality of those which come after. As the poet states it,

…所有的经验都是一座拱门，

… all experience is an arch wherethro’

照亮那未曾涉足的世界，它的边缘渐渐消失

Gleams that untraveled world, whose margin fades

当我移动时，它将永远存在。

For ever and for ever when I move.

然而，到目前为止，我们还没有理由对经验进行区分。因为这一原则具有普遍适用性。在每种情况下都存在某种连续性。只有当我们注意到经验连续性运作的不同形式时，我们才能获得对经验进行区分的基础。我可以说明一下有人对我曾经提出的一个观点提出异议的含义——即，当用主动分词“成长”来理解时，教育过程可以等同于成长。

So far, however, we have no ground for discrimination among experiences. For the principle is of universal application. There is some kind of continuity in every case. It is when we note the different forms in which continuity of experience operates that we get the basis of discriminating among experiences. I may illustrate what is meant by an objection which has been brought against an idea which I once put forth—namely, that the educative process can be identified with growth when that is understood in terms of the active participle, growing.

成长，或者说成长为不仅在身体上而且在智力和道德上的发展，是连续性原则的一个例证。反对意见认为，成长可能朝许多不同的方向发展：例如，一个以盗窃为职业的人可能会朝那个方向发展，通过实践，可能会成长为一个非常熟练的盗窃者。因此，有人认为“成长”是不够的；我们还必须明确成长的方向，以及它趋向的最终结果。然而，在确定反对意见具有决定性之前，我们必须进一步分析一下这个案例。

Growth, or growing as developing, not only physically but intellectually and morally, is one exemplification of the principle of continuity. The objection made is that growth might take many different directions: a man, for example, who starts out on a career of burglary may grow in that direction, and by practice may grow into a highly expert burglar. Hence it is argued that “growth” is not enough; we must also specify the direction in which growth takes place, the end towards which it tends. Before, however, we decide that the objection is conclusive we must analyze the case a little further.

毫无疑问，一个人可以像强盗、匪徒或腐败政客一样提高效率。但是从教育成长和教育成长的角度来看，问题在于，这种成长方式是促进还是阻碍了总体成长。这种成长方式是否为进一步成长创造了条件，还是会设置条件，使已经在这个特定方向上成长的人无法获得继续在新方向上成长的机会、刺激和机遇？在特定方向上成长对态度和习惯有何影响，而这些态度和习惯正是其他方面发展的唯一途径？我将留给你们来回答这些问题，只是说，当且仅当某一特定方面的发展有助于持续成长时，它才符合教育成长的标准。因为这个概念必须得到普遍的而不是专门的有限应用。

That a man may grow in efficiency as a burglar, as a gangster, or as a corrupt politician, cannot be doubted. But from the standpoint of growth as education and education as growth the question is whether growth in this direction promotes or retards growth in general. Does this form of growth create conditions for further growth, or does it set up conditions that shut off the person who has grown in this particular direction from the occasions, stimuli, and opportunities for continuing growth in new directions? What is the effect of growth in a special direction upon the attitudes and habits which alone open up avenues for development in other lines? I shall leave you to answer these questions, saving simply that when and only when development in a particular line conduces to continuing growth does it answer to the criterion of education as growing. For the conception is one that must find universal and not specialized limited application.

现在我回到连续性问题，连续性是区分具有教育意义的经验和具有错误教育意义的经验的标准。正如我们所看到的，无论如何都存在某种连续性，因为每一次经历都会或好或坏地影响有助于决定进一步经历质量的态度，通过建立某些偏好和厌恶，使为这个或那个目的而采取行动变得更容易或更难。此外，每一次经历都会在某种程度上影响进一步经历的客观条件。例如，一个学会说话的孩子有了新的能力和新的愿望。但他也扩大了后续学习的外部条件。当他学会阅读时，他同样开辟了一个新的环境。如果一个人决定成为一名教师、律师、医生或股票经纪人，当他实现他的意图时，他必然会在某种程度上决定他未来将在其中采取行动的环境。他使自己对某些条件更加敏感和敏感，并且相对不受那些如果他做出另一种选择会成为刺激的东西的影响。

I return now to the question of continuity as a criterion by which to discriminate between experiences which are educative and those which are mis-educative. As we have seen, there is some kind of continuity in any case since every experience affects for better or worse the attitudes which help decide the quality of further experiences, by setting up certain preference and aversion, and making it easier or harder to act for this or that end. Moreover, every experience influences in some degree the objective conditions under which further experiences are had. For example, a child who learns to speak has a new facility and new desire. But he has also widened the external conditions of subsequent learning. When he learns to read, he similarly opens up a new environment. If a person decides to become a teacher, lawyer, physician, or stockbroker, when he executes his intention he thereby necessarily determines to some extent the environment in which he will act in the future. He has rendered himself more sensitive and responsive to certain conditions, and relatively immune to those things about him that would have been stimuli if he had made another choice.

但是，虽然连续性原则在某种程度上适用于每种情况，但当前经验的质量会影响该原则的应用方式。我们谈到宠坏孩子和被宠坏的孩子。过度溺爱孩子的影响是持续的。它建立了一种态度，这种态度自动要求人们和事物迎合他未来的欲望和任性。它使他寻求那种能够让他做他当时想做的事情的情况。这使他厌恶和相对无能为力地应对需要努力和坚持不懈克服障碍的情况。经验连续性的原则可能会使一个人的发展停留在较低的水平，从而限制他以后的成长能力，这并不矛盾。

But, while the principle of continuity applies in some way in every case, the quality of the present experience influences the way in which the principle applies. We speak of spoiling a child and of the spoilt child. The effect of over-indulging a child is a continuing one. It sets up an attitude which operates as an automatic demand that persons and objects cater to his desires and caprices in the future. It makes him seek the kind of situation that will enable him to do what he feels like doing at the time. It renders him averse to and comparatively incompetent in situations which require effort and perseverance in overcoming obstacles. There is no paradox in the fact that the principle of the continuity of experience may operate so as to leave a person arrested on a low plane of development, in a way which limits later capacity for growth.

另一方面，如果经验激发好奇心、增强主动性，并建立起足够强烈的欲望和目标，使一个人超越未来的死胡同，那么连续性的作用方式就大不相同了。每一种经验都是一股推动力。它的价值只能根据它走向何方和进入何方来判断。作为教育者，成年人应该拥有更成熟的经验，这使他能够以经验不太成熟的人无法做到的方式评价年轻人的每一次经验。那么，教育者的任务就是看经验的发展方向。如果他不利用自己更深刻的洞察力来帮助组织不成熟者的经验条件，而是抛弃自己的洞察力，那么他更成熟就毫无意义了。不考虑经验的推动力，不根据经验的发展方向来判断和引导它，就意味着背叛了经验本身的原则。这种背叛有两个方向。教育者没有认识到他应该从自己过去的经历中获得的理解。他也没有认识到所有人类经验最终都是社会性的：它涉及接触和交流。从道德的角度来说，成熟的人没有权利在特定场合不让年轻人获得他自己经历所赋予的同情理解能力。

On the other hand, if an experience arouses curiosity, strengthens initiative, and sets up desires and purposes that are sufficiently intense to carry a person over-dead places in the future, continuity works in a very different way. Every experience is a moving force. Its value can be judged only on the ground of what it moves toward and into. The greater maturity of experience which should belong to the adult as educator puts him in a position to evaluate each experience of the young in a way in which the one having the less mature experience cannot do. It is then the business of the educator to see in what direction an experience is heading. There is no point in his being more mature if, instead of using his greater insight to help organize the conditions of the experience of the immature, he throws away his insight. Failure to take the moving force of an experience into account so as to judge and direct it on the ground of what it is moving into means disloyalty to the principle of experience itself. The disloyalty operates in two directions. The educator is false to the understanding that he should have obtained from his own past experience. He is also unfaithful to the fact that all human experience is ultimately social: that it involves contact and communication. The mature person, to put it in moral terms, has no right to withhold from the young on given occasions whatever capacity for sympathetic understanding his own experience has given him.

然而，一旦说出这些话，人们就会倾向于走向另一个极端，认为这些话是外界某种伪装的强加的借口。因此，值得一提的是，成年人如何运用自己更广泛的经验所赋予他的智慧，而不施加单纯的外部控制。一方面，他的职责是保持警惕，观察正在形成什么态度和习惯倾向。在这方面，如果他是一名教育工作者，他必须能够判断哪些态度实际上有利于继续成长，哪些态度有害。此外，他还必须对个人有同情的理解，这使他了解正在学习的人的头脑中到底在想什么。除其他外，正是父母和老师需要这些能力，使得基于生活经验的教育体系比遵循传统教育模式更难以成功实施。

No sooner, however, are such things said than there is a tendency to react to the other extreme and take what has been said as a plea for some sort of disguised imposition from outside. It is worth while, accordingly, to say something about the way in which the adult can exercise the wisdom his own wider experience gives him without imposing a merely external control. On one side, it is his business to be on the alert to see what attitudes and habitual tendencies are being created. In this direction he must, if he is an educator, be able to judge what attitudes are actually conducive to continued growth and what are detrimental. He must, in addition, have that sympathetic understanding of individuals as individuals which gives him an idea of what is actually going on in the minds of those who are learning. It is, among other things, the need for these abilities on the part of the parent and teacher which makes a system of education based upon living experience a more difficult affair to conduct successfully than it is to follow the patterns of traditional education.

但事情还有另一个方面。经验并不只是在人的内部进行。经验确实在人的内部进行，因为它影响着欲望和目的态度的形成。但这并不是故事的全部。每一种真正的经验都有一种积极的一面，它在某种程度上改变了经验发生的客观条件。举一个大的例子，文明与野蛮的区别在于先前的经验在多大程度上改变了随后的经验发生的客观条件。道路、快速移动和运输工具、工具、器具、家具、电灯和电力的存在就是例证。破坏了目前文明经验的外部条件，我们的经验就会在一段时间内重新回到野蛮民族的经验。

But there is another aspect of the matter. Experience does not go on simply inside a person. It does go on there, for it influences the formation of attitudes of desire and purpose. But this is not the whole of the story. Every genuine experience has an active side which changes in some degree the objective conditions under which experiences are had. The difference between civilization and savagery, to take an example on a large scale, is found in the degree in which previous experiences have changed the objective conditions under which subsequent experiences take place. The existence of roads, of means of rapid movement and transportation, tools, implements, furniture, electric light and power, are illustrations. Destroy the external conditions of present civilized experience, and for a time our experience would relapse into that of barbaric peoples.

总之，我们从生到死生活在一个由人和事物组成的世界里，这个世界之所以如此，很大程度上是因为人类的活动所造成的。如果忽略这一事实，经验就会被当作只发生在个人身体和心灵内部的事情。经验不是凭空产生的，这一点不必说。个人之外还有经验的来源。经验不断从这些源泉中汲取养分。没有人会质疑贫民窟里的孩子和有文化家庭的孩子有不同的经验；没有人会质疑乡村男孩和城市男孩有不同的经验；没有人会质疑海滨男孩和在内陆草原长大的男孩有不同的经验。通常我们认为这些事实太平常了，不值得记录。但是，当认识到它们的教育意义时，它们就表明了教育者可以引导年轻人经验的第二种方式，而不是强加于人。教育工作者的首要职责是，他们不仅要意识到环境条件塑造实际经验的一般原则，还要具体认识到哪些环境有利于获得导致成长的经验。最重要的是，他们应该知道如何利用现有的物质和社会环境，以便从中汲取他们所能贡献的一切，以建立有价值的经验。

In a word, we live from birth to death in a world of persons and things which in large measure is what it is because of what has been done and transmitted from previous human activities. When this fact is ignored, experience is treated as if it were something which goes on exclusively inside an individual’s body and mind. It ought not to be necessary to say that experience does not occur in a vacuum. There are sources outside an individual which give rise to experience. It is constantly fed from these springs. No one would question that a child in a slum tenement has a different experience from that of a child in a cultured home; that the country lad has a different kind of experience from the city boy, or a boy on the seashore one different from the lad who is brought up on inland prairies. Ordinarily we take such facts for granted as too commonplace to record. But when their educational import is recognized, they indicate the second way in which the educator can direct the experience of the young without engaging in imposition. A primary responsibility of educators is that they not only be aware of the general principle of the shaping of actual experience by environing conditions, but that they also recognize in the concrete what surroundings are conducive to having experiences that lead to growth. Above all, they should know how to utilize the surroundings, physical and social, that exist so as to extract from them all that they have to contribute to building up experiences that are worth while.

传统教育不必面对这个问题；它可以系统地逃避这一责任。学校环境，包括课桌、黑板和小校园，就足够了。教师不必熟悉当地社区的物质、历史、经济、职业等条件，以便将其用作教育资源。相反，一种以教育与经验的必要联系为基础的教育体系，如果忠于其原则，就必须不断考虑到这些因素。教育者所承受的这种负担是进步教育比传统体系更难进行的另一个原因。

Traditional education did not have to face this problem; it could systematically dodge this responsibility. The school environment of desks, blackboards, a small school yard, was supposed to suffice. There was no demand that the teacher should become intimately acquainted with the conditions of the local community, physical, historical, economic, occupational, etc., in order to utilize them as educational resources. A system of education based upon the necessary connection of education with experience must, on the contrary, if faithful to its principle, take these things constantly into account. This tax upon the educator is another reason why progressive education is more difficult to carry on than was ever the traditional system.

可以制定教育方案，使客观条件相当系统地服从于受教育者自身的条件。只要教师、书籍、仪器和设备以及代表老年人更成熟经验的一切事物的地位和职能都系统地服从于年轻人的直接倾向和感受，就会发生这种情况。任何一种理论都认为，只有以施加外部控制和限制个人自由为代价才能重视这些客观因素，最终都建立在这样的观念上：只有当客观条件服从于拥有经验的个人的内心活动时，经验才是真正的经验。

It is possible to frame schemes of education that pretty systematically subordinate objective conditions to those which reside in the individuals being educated. This happens whenever the place and function of the teacher, of books, of apparatus and equipment, of everything which represents the products of the more mature experience of elders, is systematically subordinated to the immediate inclinations and feelings of the young. Every theory which assumes that importance can be attached to these objective factors only at the expense of imposing external control and of limiting the freedom of individuals rests finally upon the notion that experience is truly experience only when objective conditions are subordinated to what goes on within the individuals having the experience.

我的意思并不是说可以排除客观条件。人们认识到客观条件必须发挥作用：人们对于我们生活在一个由物质和人组成的世界这一不可避免的事实做出了如此多的让步。但我认为，观察一些家庭和学校的情况会发现，一些家长和一些教师正在采取将客观条件置于内部条件之下的想法。在这种情况下，人们不仅认为内部条件是主要的（从某种意义上说确实如此），而且认为由于内部条件暂时存在，它们决定了整个教育过程。

I do not mean that it is supposed that objective conditions can be shut out. It is recognized that they must enter in: so much concession is made to the inescapable fact that we live in a world of things and persons. But I think that observation of what goes on in some families and some schools would disclose that some parents and some teachers are acting upon the idea of subordinating objective conditions to internal ones. In that case, it is assumed not only that the latter are primary, which in one sense they are, but that just as they temporarily exist they fix the whole educational process.

让我以婴儿为例来说明。婴儿对食物、休息和活动的需求在某一方面无疑是首要的和决定性的。必须提供营养；必须提供舒适的睡眠，等等。但这些事实并不意味着父母可以在婴儿生气或烦躁时随时喂奶，也不意味着不能有规律的喂奶和睡眠时间计划，等等。明智的母亲会考虑婴儿的需求，但不会以不履行自己调节满足需求的客观条件的责任的方式考​​虑婴儿的需求。如果她在这方面是一位明智的母亲，她会借鉴专家和她自己的过去经验，从中了解哪些经验通常最有利于婴儿的正常发育。这些条件并非从属于婴儿的直接内部条件，而是明确地安排好，以便与这些直接内部状态产生某种特定的互动。

Let me illustrate from the case of an infant. The needs of a baby for food, rest, and activity are certainly primary and decisive in one respect. Nourishment must be provided; provision must be made for comfortable sleep, and so on. But these facts do not mean that a parent shall feed the baby at any time when the baby is cross or irritable, that there shall not be a program of regular hours of feeding and sleeping, etc. The wise mother takes account of the needs of the infant but not in a way which dispenses with her own responsibility for regulating the objective conditions under which the needs are satisfied. And if she is a wise mother in this respect, she draws upon past experiences of experts as well as her own for the light that these shed upon what experiences are in general most conducive to the normal development of infants. Instead of these conditions being subordinated to the immediate internal condition of the baby, they are definitely ordered so that a particular kind of interaction with these immediate internal states may be brought, about.

刚才使用的“互动”一词表达了解释经验的教育功能和力量的第二个主要原则。它赋予经验中的两个因素——客观条件和内部条件——同等的权利。任何正常的经验都是这两组条件的相互作用。它们结合在一起，或者在相互作用中，形成了我们所说的情境。传统教育的问题不在于它强调控制经验的外部条件，而在于它很少关注同样决定获得何种经验的内部因素。它从一方面违反了互动原则。但这种违反并不是新教育从另一方面违反原则的理由——除非是基于上述极端的非此即彼的教育哲学。

The word “interaction,” which has just been used, expresses the second chief principle for interpreting an experience in its educational function and force. It assigns equal rights to both factors in experience—objective and internal conditions. Any normal experience is an interplay of these two sets of conditions. Taken together, or in their interaction, they form what we call a situation. The trouble with traditional education was not that it emphasized the external conditions that enter into the control of the experiences but that it paid so little attention to the internal factors which also decide what kind of experience is had. It violated the principle of interaction from one side. But this violation is no reason why the new education should violate the principle from the other side—except upon the basis of the extreme Either-Or educational philosophy which has been mentioned.

从调节婴儿发育的客观条件的需要中得出的例子表明，首先，父母有责任安排婴儿进食、睡眠等的条件；其次，父母要利用过去的经验来履行责任，例如，专业医生和其他对正常身体发育进行过专门研究的人的建议。当母亲利用由此获得的知识来调节营养和睡眠的客观条件时，这是否限制了母亲的自由？还是说，在履行父母职责时，她的智力得到了提高，从而扩大了她的自由？毫无疑问，如果对建议和指示过于迷信，以至于它们成为在任何可能情况下都必须遵循的不可动摇的命令，那么父母和孩子的自由都会受到限制。但这种限制也会限制个人判断所运用的智力。

The illustration drawn from the need for regulation of the objective conditions of a baby’s development indicates, first, that the parent has responsibility for arranging the conditions under which an infant’s experience of food, sleep, etc., occurs, and, secondly, that the responsibility is fulfilled by utilizing the funded experience of the past, as this is represented, say, by the advice of competent physicians and others who have made a special study of normal physical growth. Does it limit the freedom of the mother when she uses the body of knowledge thus provided to regulate the objective conditions of nourishment and sleep? Or does the enlargement of her intelligence in fulfilling her parental function widen her freedom? Doubtless if a fetish were made of the advice and directions so that they came to be inflexible dictates to be followed under every possible condition, then restriction of freedom of both parent and child would occur. But this restriction would also be a limitation of the intelligence that is exercised in personal judgment.

客观条件的调节在哪些方面限制了婴儿的自由？当婴儿被放在婴儿床里，当婴儿想继续玩耍时，或者当婴儿想吃东西时没有得到食物，或者当婴儿哭闹时没有人抱起和抚摸，婴儿的即时动作和倾向肯定会受到一些限制。当母亲或保姆把婴儿从即将掉入的明火中抢走时，也会发生限制。我以后会更多地谈论自由。这里只需问一问，自由是否应该根据相对短暂的事件来思考和判断，还是其意义存在于不断发展的经验中。

In what respect does regulation of objective conditions limit the freedom of the baby? Some limitation is certainly placed upon its immediate movements and inclinations when it is put in its crib, at a time when it wants to continue playing, or does not get food at the moment it would like it, or when it isn’t picked up and dandled when it cries for attention. Restriction also occurs when mother or nurse snatches a child away from an open fire into which it is about to fall. I shall have more to say later about freedom. Here it is enough to ask whether freedom is to be thought of and adjudged on the basis of relatively momentary incidents or whether its meaning is found in the continuity of developing experience.

个人生活在世界中这一说法具体意味着他们生活在一系列情境中。当人们说他们生活在这些情境中时，“在”这个词的含义与说硬币在口袋里或油漆在罐子里时的含义不同。它再次意味着个人与物体和其他人之间正在发生互动。情境和互动的概念是不可分割的。体验总是因为个人与当时构成其环境的事物之间发生了互动而成为体验，无论后者是由与他谈论某个话题或事件的人组成，谈论的主题也是情境的一部分；还是他正在玩的玩具；他正在阅读的书（当时他的环境条件可能是英国、古希腊或一个想象中的地区）；还是他正在进行的实验的材料。换句话说，环境是与个人需求、愿望、目的和能力互动以创造体验的任何条件。即使当一个人建造空中楼阁时，他也在与自己想象中构建的物体进行互动。

The statement that individuals live in a world means, in the concrete, that they live in a series of situations. And when it is said that they live in these situations, the meaning of the word “in” is different from its meaning when it is said that pennies are “in” a pocket or paint is “in” a can. It means, once more, that interaction is going on between an individual and objects and other persons. The conceptions of situation and of interaction are inseparable from each other. An experience is always what it is because of a transaction taking place between an individual and what, at the time, constitutes his environment, whether the latter consists of persons with whom he is talking about some topic or event, the subject talked about being also a part of the situation; or the toys with which he is playing; the book he is reading (in which his environing conditions at the time may be England or ancient Greece or an imaginary region) ; or the materials of an experiment he is performing. The environment, in other words, is whatever conditions interact with personal needs, desires, purposes, and capacities to create the experience which is had. Even when a person builds a castle in the air he is interacting with the objects which he constructs in fancy.

连续性和相互作用这两个原则并不是彼此分离的。它们相互交织，又相互统一。可以说，它们是经验的纵向和横向方面。不同的情况接踵而至。但由于连续性原则，一些事情会从较早的情况延续到较晚的情况。当一个人从一种情况过渡到另一种情况时，他的世界、他的环境就会扩大或缩小。他并不是生活在另一个世界，而是生活在同一个世界的不同部分或方面。他在一种情况下学到的知识和技能成为理解和有效处理后续情况的工具。只要生活和学习还在继续，这个过程就会继续下去。否则，经验的过程就会变得混乱，因为形成经验的个人因素是分裂的。一个分裂的世界，一个各部分和各方面都不连贯的世界，既是分裂人格的标志，也是分裂人格的原因。当分裂达到一定程度时，我们称这个人为疯子。另一方面，只有当连续的经验相互融合时，才会存在完全完整的人格。它只有在构建相关对象的世界时才能被建立起来。

The two principles of continuity and interaction are not separate from each other. They intercept and unite. They are, so to speak, the longitudinal and lateral aspects of experience. Different situations succeed one another. But because of the principle of continuity something is carried over from the earlier to the later ones. As an individual passes from one situation to another, his world, his environment, expands or contracts. He does not find himself living in another world but in a different part or aspect of one and the same world. What he has learned in the way of knowledge and skill in one situation becomes an instrument of understanding and dealing effectively with the situations which follow. The process goes on as long as life and learning continue. Otherwise the course of experience is disorderly, since the individual factor that enters into making an experience is split. A divided world, a world whose parts and aspects do not hang together, is at once a sign and a cause of a divided personality. When the splitting-up reaches a certain point we call the person insane. A fully integrated personality, on the other hand, exists only when successive experiences are integrated with one another. It can be built up only as a world of related objects is constructed.

连续性和互动性在它们相互的积极结合中提供了经验的教育意义和价值的衡量标准。教育者最直接关心的是互动发生的情境。个人作为其中的一个因素，是他在特定时间的状态。另一个因素，即客观条件，在某种程度上属于教育者可以控制的范围。正如已经指出的，“客观条件”一词涵盖的范围很广。它包括教育者所做的事情和做事的方式，不仅包括所说的话，还包括说话的语气。它包括设备、书籍、器械、玩具、游戏。它包括个人互动的材料，最重要的是，一个人所处情境的整个社会设置。

Continuity and interaction in their active union with each other provide the measure of the educative significance and value of an experience. The immediate and direct concern of an educator is then with the situations in which interaction takes place. The individual, who enters as a factor into it, is what he is at a given time. It is the other factor, that of objective conditions, which lies to some extent within the possibility of regulation by the educator. As has already been noted, the phrase “objective conditions” covers a wide range. It includes what is done by the educator and the way in which it is done, not only words spoken but the tone of voice in which they are spoken. It includes equipment, books, apparatus, toys, games played. It includes the materials with which an individual interacts, and, most important of all, the total social set-up of the situations in which a person is engaged.

当人们说客观条件是教育者可以控制的条件时，当然，这意味着他能够直接影响他人的经验，从而影响他们所接受的教育，这就要求他有责任确定一个环境，该环境将与受教者的现有能力和需求相互作用，以创造有价值的经验。传统教育的问题不在于教育者承担了提供环境的责任。问题在于他们没有考虑创造经验的另一个因素，即受教者的能力和目的。人们认为，除了能够引起个人的某种反应之外，某些条件本身就是可取的。这种相互适应的缺乏使得教学和学习过程成为偶然。那些适合所提供条件的人设法学习。其他人则尽其所能。因此，选择客观条件的责任意味着了解在特定时间学习的个人的需求和能力。某些材料和方法在其他时间对其他人有效是不够的。我们必须有理由相信，它们将在特定时间为特定个体产生具有教育意义的体验。

When it is said that the objective conditions are those which are within the power of the educator to regulate, it is meant, of course, that his ability to influence directly the experience of others and thereby the education they obtain places upon him the duty of determining that environment which will interact with the existing capacities and needs of those taught to create a worth-while experience. The trouble with traditional education was not that educators took upon themselves the responsibility for providing an environment. The trouble was that they did not consider the other factor in creating an experience; namely, the powers and purposes of those taught. It was assumed that a certain set of conditions was intrinsically desirable, apart from its ability to evoke a certain quality of response in individuals. This lack of mutual adaptation made the process of teaching and learning accidental. Those to whom the provided conditions were suitable managed to learn. Others got on as best they could. Responsibility for selecting objective conditions carries with it, then, the responsibility for understanding the needs and capacities of the individuals who are learning at a given time. It is not enough that certain materials and methods have proved effective with other individuals at other times. There must be a reason for thinking that they will function in generating an experience that has educative quality with particular individuals at a particular time.

不给婴儿吃牛排，并不反映牛排的营养价值。我们不在一年级或五年级教授三角学，也并不反映三角学的不良影响。学科本身并不具有教育意义或有助于成长。没有一门学科本身，或者与学习者所处的成长阶段无关，可以赋予其内在的教育价值。没有考虑到适应个人的需求和能力，这是某些学科和某些方法本质上是文化的或本质上有利于智力训练的想法的根源。抽象的教育价值是不存在的。某些学科和方法以及对某些事实和真理的了解本身就具有教育价值，这种观念是传统教育将教育内容大量简化为预先消化的材料的原因。根据这一观点，只需在定量评分方案中，逐月、逐年地调节所提供材料的数量和难度就足够了。否则，学生就应该按照外部规定的剂量学习。如果学生不学习而放弃学习，如果他逃避学习，或者逃避学习，心不在焉，最终对学习科目产生了情绪上的厌恶，那么他就应该承担责任。没有人质疑问题是否出在学习科目本身或提供学习科目的方式上。互动原则清楚地表明，学习材料未能适应个人的需求和能力，与个人未能适应学习材料一样，都可能导致体验缺乏教育意义。

It is no reflection upon the nutritive quality of beefsteak that it is not fed to infants. It is not an invidious reflection upon trigonometry that we do not teach it in the first or fifth grade of school. It is not the subject per se that is educative or that is conducive to growth. There is no subject that is in and of itself, or without regard to the stage of growth attained by the learner, such that inherent educational value can be attributed to it. Failure to take into account adaptation to the needs and capacities of individuals was the source of the idea that certain subjects and certain methods are intrinsically cultural or intrinsically good for mental discipline. There is no such thing as educational value in the abstract. The notion that some subjects and methods and that acquaintance with certain facts and truths possess educational value in and of themselves is the reason why traditional education reduced the material of education so largely to a diet of predigested materials. According to this notion, it was enough to regulate the quantity and difficulty of the material provided, in a scheme of quantitative grading, from month to month and from year to year. Otherwise a pupil was expected to take it in the doses that were prescribed from without. If the pupil left it instead of taking it, if he engaged in physical truancy, or in the mental truancy of mind-wandering and finally built up an emotional revulsion against the subject, he was held to be at fault. No question was raised as to whether the trouble might not lie in the subject-matter or in the way in which it was offered. The principle of interaction makes it clear that failure of adaptation of material to needs and capacities of individuals may cause an experience to be non-educative quite as much as failure of an individual to adapt himself to the material.

然而，在教育应用中，连续性原则意味着在教育过程的每个阶段都必须考虑到未来。这一理念很容易被误解，在传统教育中被严重扭曲。传统教育的假设是，通过掌握某些技能和学习以后（也许是在大学或成年生活中）需要的某些科目，学生理所当然地为未来的需要和环境做好了准备。现在，“准备”是一个危险的想法。从某种意义上说，每一次经历都应该为一个人以后更深刻、更广阔的经历做好准备。这就是成长、连续性和经验重建的真正意义。但是，如果认为仅仅掌握一定数量的算术、地理、历史等知识（这些知识在未来某个时候可能会有用）就能产生这种效果，如果认为掌握阅读和计算技能会自动为在与当初获得这些技能截然不同的条件下正确和有效地使用这些技能做好准备，那也是错误的。

The principle of continuity in its educational application means, nevertheless, that the future has to be taken into account at every stage of the educational process. This idea is easily misunderstood and is badly distorted in traditional education. Its assumption is, that by acquiring certain skills and by learning certain subjects which would be needed later (perhaps in college or perhaps in adult life) pupils are as a matter of course made ready for the needs and circumstances of the future. Now “preparation” is a treacherous idea. In a certain sense every experience should do something to prepare a person for later experiences of a deeper and more expansive quality. That is the very meaning of growth, continuity, reconstruction of experience. But it is a mistake to suppose that the mere acquisition of a certain amount of arithmetic, geography, history, etc., which is taught and studied because it may be useful at some time in the future, has this effect, and it is a mistake to suppose that acquisition of skills in reading and figuring will automatically constitute preparation for their right and effective use under conditions very unlike those in which they were acquired.

几乎每个人都曾回顾过自己的学生时代，想知道自己在学校学习期间积累的知识都到哪里去了，为什么他所掌握的技术技能必须以不同的形式重新学习才能对他大有裨益。事实上，如果一个人没有发现为了取得进步，为了在智力上取得进步，他不必忘记他在学校学到的大部分知识，那他就很幸运了。这些问题不能通过说这些科目实际上没有被学习来解决，因为这些科目至少已经足够让学生通过考试了。一个问题是，所讨论的科目是孤立地学习的；它被放在一个防水的隔间里。那么，当人们问起它都到哪里去了，正确的答案是它仍然在原来存放它的特殊隔间里。如果与获得它时完全相同的条件再次出现，它也会再次出现并可用。但它在获得时就被隔离了，因此与其他经验脱节，无法在实际生活条件下获得。这种学习，无论当时根深蒂固，都应该提供真正的准备，这违背了经验法则。

Almost everyone has had occasion to look back upon his school days and wonder what has become of the knowledge he was supposed to have amassed during his years of schooling, and why it is that the technical skills he acquired have to be learned over again in changed form in order to stand him in good stead. Indeed, he is lucky who does not find that in order to make progress, in order to go ahead intellectually, he does not have to unlearn much of what he learned in school. These questions cannot be disposed of by saying that the subjects were not actually learned, for they were learned at least sufficiently to enable a pupil to pass examinations in them. One trouble is that the subject-matter in question was learned in isolation; it was put, as it were, in a water-tight compartment. When the question is asked, then, what has become of it, where has it gone to, the right answer is that it is still there in the special compartment in which it was originally stowed away. If exactly the same conditions recurred as those under which it was acquired, it would also recur and be available. But it was segregated when it was acquired and hence is so disconnected from the rest of experience that it is not available under the actual conditions of life. It is contrary to the laws of experience that learning of this kind, no matter how thoroughly engrained at the time, should give genuine preparation.

准备上的失败还不止于此。也许最大的教学谬误就是认为一个人只学习他当时正在学习的特定内容。在形成持久态度、好恶方面，附带学习可能而且往往比学到的拼写课、地理课或历史课更重要。因为这些态度才是未来最重要的。可以形成的最重要的态度是继续学习的愿望。如果这方面的动力被削弱而不是被加强，那么就会发生比单纯缺乏准备更严重的事情。学生实际上被剥夺了天生的能力，否则这些能力会使他能够应对生活中遇到的情况。我们经常看到一些几乎没有受过学校教育的人，而没有接受过固定的学校教育对他们来说是一种积极的资产。他们至少保留了天生的常识和判断力，在实际生活条件下的运用赋予了他们从经验中学习的宝贵能力。如果一个人在获得一定数量的地理和历史信息、获得读写能力的过程中丧失了自己的灵魂：丧失了对有价值的事物的欣赏能力、丧失了与这些事物相关的价值的认识能力；丧失了运用所学知识的愿望，尤其是丧失了从未来经历中提取意义的能力，那么，如果他获得了一定数量的地理和历史信息、获得了读写能力，那还有什么意义呢？

Nor does failure in preparation end at this point. Perhaps the greatest of all pedagogical fallacies is the notion that a person learns only the particular thing he is studying at the time. Collateral learning in the way of formation of enduring attitudes, of likes and dislikes, may be and often is much more important than the spelling lesson or lesson in geography or history that is learned. For these attitudes are fundamentally what count in the future. The most important attitude that can be formed is that of desire to go on learning. If impetus in this direction is weakened instead of being intensified, something much more than mere lack of preparation takes place. The pupil is actually robbed of native capacities which otherwise would enable him to cope with the circumstances that he meets in the course of his life. We often see persons who have had little schooling and in whose case the absence of set schooling proves to be a positive asset. They have at least retained their native common sense and power of judgment, and its exercise in the actual conditions of living has given them the precious gift of ability to learn from the experiences they have. What avail is it to win prescribed amounts of information about geography and history, to win ability to read and write, if in the process the individual loses his own soul: loses his appreciation of things worth while, of the values to which these things are relative; if he loses desire to apply what he has learned and, above all, loses the ability to extract meaning from his future experiences as they occur?

那么，教育计划中准备的真正意义是什么呢？首先，它意味着一个人，无论年轻还是年老，都能从他目前的经历中获得当时对他来说所有的东西。当准备成为控制目的时，现在的潜力就会被牺牲给一个假定的未来。当这种情况发生时，对未来的实际准备就会被忽略或扭曲。仅仅利用现在来为未来做准备的理想是自相矛盾的。它忽略了，甚至排除了一个人为未来做准备的条件。我们总是活在当下，而不是其他时间，只有在每一个当下时刻提取每一个当下经历的全部意义，我们才能为将来做同样的事情做好准备。这是唯一从长远来看有意义的准备。

What, then, is the true meaning of preparation in the educational scheme? In the first place, it means that a person, young or old, gets out of his present experience all that there is in it for him at the time in which he has it. When preparation is made the controlling end, then the potentialities of the present are sacrificed to a suppositious future. When this happens, the actual preparation for the future is missed or distorted. The ideal of using the present simply to get ready for the future contradicts itself. It omits, and even shuts out, the very conditions by which a person can be prepared for his future. We always live at the time we live and not at some other time, and only by extracting at each present time the full meaning of each present experience are we prepared for doing the same thing in the future. This is the only preparation which in the long run amounts to anything.

所有这些都意味着必须细心注意赋予每个当下体验以价值意义的条件。我们不应该推断说，只要享受当下的体验，当下的体验是什么并不重要，而应该得出完全相反的结论。这是另一个容易从一个极端走向另一个极端的问题。由于传统学校倾向于将当下牺牲给遥远的、或多或少未知的未来，因此人们开始认为，教育者对年轻人经历的当下体验几乎没有责任。但现在和未来的关系并不是非此即彼的问题。现在无论如何都会影响未来。应该对两者之间的联系有所了解的人是那些已经成熟的人。因此，他们有责任为对未来产生有利影响的当下体验创造条件。教育作为成长或成熟应该是一个永远存在的过程。

All this means that attentive care must be devoted to the conditions which give each present experience a worthwhile meaning. Instead of inferring that it doesn’t make much difference what the present experience is as long as it is enjoyed, the conclusion is the exact opposite. Here is another matter where it is easy to react from one extreme to the other. Because traditional schools tended to sacrifice the present to a remote and more or less unknown future, therefore it comes to be believed that the educator has little responsibility for the kind of present experiences the young undergo. But the relation of the present and the future is not an Either-Or affair. The present affects the future anyway. The persons who should have some idea of the connection between the two are those who have achieved maturity. Accordingly, upon them devolves the responsibility for instituting the conditions for the kind of present experience which has a favorable effect upon the future. Education as growth or maturity should be an ever-present process.

第四章

Chapter 4

社会控制

Social Control

我曾说过，教育计划和项目从生活经验的角度看待教育，因此致力于制定和采用一种明智的理论，或者说经验哲学。否则，它们将任由吹来的每一阵智力之风摆布。我试图通过提请大家注意经验构成中的两个基本原则来说明这种理论的必要性：相互作用原则和连续性原则。如果有人问我为什么花了这么多时间来阐述一种相当抽象的哲学，那是因为，在教育存在于生活经验这一理念的基础上建立学校的实际尝试必然会出现不一致和混乱，除非它们受到某种关于经验是什么以及什么将教育经验与非教育经验和错误教育经验区分开来的概念的指导。现在我要谈一组实际的教育问题，我希望对这些问题的讨论将提供比到目前为止的讨论更具体的主题和材料。

I HAVE said that educational plans and projects, seeing education in terms of life-experience, are thereby committed to framing and adopting an intelligent theory or, if you please, philosophy of experience. Otherwise they are at the mercy of every intellectual breeze that happens to blow. I have tried to illustrate the need for such a theory by calling attention to two principles which are fundamental in the constitution of experience: the principles of interaction and of continuity. If, then, I am asked why I have spent so much time on expounding a rather abstract philosophy, it is because practical attempts to develop schools based upon the idea that education is found in life-experience are bound to exhibit inconsistencies and confusions unless they are guided by some conception of what experience is, and what marks off educative experience from non-educative and mis-educative experience. I now come to a group of actual educational questions the discussion of which will, I hope, provide topics and material that are more concrete than the discussion up to this point.

作为经验价值标准的连续性和互动性这两个原则是如此紧密地联系在一起，以至于很难说清楚首先要解决什么特殊的教育问题。即使方便地将问题分为学科或研究问题和教学方法问题，也可能使我们在选择和组织要讨论的主题时失败。因此，主题的开始和顺序有些随意。不过，我将从个人自由和社会控制这个老问题开始，然后再讨论由此自然产生的问题。

The two principles of continuity and interaction as criteria of the value of experience are so intimately connected that it is not easy to tell just what special educational problem to take up first. Even the convenient division into problems of subject-matter or studies and of methods of teaching and learning is likely to fail us in selection and organization of topics to discuss. Consequently, the beginning and sequence of topics is somewhat arbitrary. I shall commence, however, with the old question of individual freedom and social control and pass on to the questions that grow naturally out of it.

在考虑教育问题时，最好先暂时忽略学校，考虑其他人的情况。我认为没有人会否认，普通好公民实际上受到大量社会控制，而且这种控制的很大一部分并不涉及限制个人自由。即使是理论上的无政府主义者，其哲学认为国家或政府控制是一种十足的邪恶，也相信随着政治国家的废除，其他形式的社会控制也会发挥作用：事实上，他反对政府管制，是因为他相信，随着国家废除，其他对他来说更正常的控制方式也会发挥作用。

It is often well in considering educational problems to get a start by temporarily ignoring the school and thinking of other human situations. I take it that no one would deny that the ordinary good citizen is as a matter of fact subject to a great deal of social control and that a considerable part of this control is not felt to involve restriction of personal freedom. Even the theoretical anarchist, whose philosophy commits him to the idea that state or government control is an unmitigated evil, believes that with abolition of the political state other forms of social control would operate: indeed, his opposition to governmental regulation springs from his belief that other and to him more normal modes of control would operate with abolition of the state.

我们不采取这种极端立场，让我们注意一些在日常生活中运作的社会控制的例子，然后寻找其背后的原则。让我们从年轻人本身开始。孩子们在课间休息或放学后玩游戏，从捉人游戏和老猫游戏到棒球和足球。游戏有规则，这些规则规定了他们的行为。游戏不是杂乱无章地或一连串的即兴发挥进行的。没有规则就没有游戏。如果发生争议，可以向裁判上诉，或者通过讨论和某种仲裁来做出决定；否则游戏就会中断并结束。

Without taking up this extreme position, let us note some examples of social control that operate in everyday life, and then look for the principle underlying them. Let us begin with the young people themselves. Children at recess or after school play games, from tag and one-old-cat to baseball and football. The games involve rules, and these rules order their conduct. The games do not go on haphazardly or by a succession of improvisations. Without rules there is no game. If disputes arise there is an umpire to appeal to, or discussion and a kind of arbitration are means to a decision; otherwise the game is broken up and comes to an end.

我想提请大家注意这种情形的一些相当明显的控制特征。首先，规则是游戏的一部分。它们并不在游戏之外。没有规则，就没有游戏；不同的规则，则是不同的游戏。只要游戏进行得相当顺利，玩家就不会觉得自己是在服从外部强加，而是在玩游戏。其次，个人有时可能会觉得某个决定不公平，甚至会生气。但他反对的不是规则，而是他声称违反规则的行为，是某种片面和不公平的行为。第三，规则，以及游戏的进行，都是相当标准化的。有公认的计分方式、选择边线的方式，以及采取的位置、做出的动作等。这些规则受到传统和先例的认可。玩游戏的人可能看过职业比赛，他们想效仿他们的长辈。传统元素相当强大。通常，只有当他们所效仿的成年人群体自己也对规则进行了改变时，一群年轻人才会改变他们的游戏规则，而长者所做的改变至少应该有助于使游戏更具技巧性或更吸引观众。

There are certain fairly obvious controlling features of such situations to which I want to call attention. The first is that the rules are a part of the game. They are not outside of it. No rules, then no game; different rules, then a different game. As long as the game goes on with a reasonable smoothness, the players do not feel that they are submitting to external imposition but that they are playing the game. In the second place an individual may at times feel that a decision isn’t fair and he may even get angry. But he is not objecting to a rule but to what he claims is a violation of it, to some one-sided and unfair action. In the third place, the rules, and hence the conduct of the game, are fairly standardized. There are recognized ways of counting out, of selection of sides, as well as for positions to be taken, movements to be made, etc. These rules have the sanction of tradition and precedent. Those playing the game have seen, perhaps, professional matches and they want to emulate their elders. An element that is conventional is pretty strong. Usually, a group of youngsters change the rules by which they play only when the adult group to which they look for models have themselves made a change in the rules, while the change made by the elders is at least supposed to conduce to making the game more skillful or more interesting to spectators.

现在，我要得出的一般结论是，对个人行为的控制受到个人所参与的整个环境的影响，他们参与其中，相互合作或相互作用。因为即使在竞争性游戏中，也存在某种参与，分享共同的经历。反过来说，参与者并不觉得自己受到某个人的指挥或受到某个外部上级的意志的支配。当发生激烈的争端时，通常是因为所谓的理由是裁判或另一方的某个人不公平；换句话说，在这种情况下，某个人试图将自己的个人意志强加给别人。

Now, the general conclusion I would draw is that control of individual actions is effected by the whole situation in which individuals are involved, in which they share and of which they are co-operative or interacting parts. For even in a competitive game there is a certain kind of participation, of sharing in a common experience. Stated the other way around, those who take part do not feel that they are bossed by an individual person or are being subjected to the will of some outside superior person. When violent disputes do arise, it is usually on the alleged ground that the umpire or some person on the other side is being unfair; in other words, that in such cases some individual is trying to impose his individual will on someone else.

似乎把一个案例看得太重了，认为这个案例说明了社会对个人进行控制的一般原则，而不侵犯个人的自由。但如果通过一系列案例来说明这个问题，我认为这个特定案例确实说明了一般原则的结论是合理的。游戏通常是竞争性的。如果我们以一个群体所有成员都参与的合作活动为例，例如在相互信任的井然有序的家庭生活中，这一点会更加清楚。在所有这些情况下，建立秩序的不是任何一个人的意愿或愿望，而是整个群体的推动精神。控制是社会性的，但个人是社区的一部分，而不是社区之外的人。

It may seem to be putting too heavy a load upon a single case to argue that this instance illustrates the general principle of social control of individuals without the violation of freedom. But if the matter were followed out through a number of cases, I think the conclusion that this particular instance does illustrate a general principle would be justified. Games are generally competitive. If we took instances of co-operative activities in which all members of a group take part, as for example in well-ordered family life in which there is mutual confidence, the point would be even clearer. In all such cases it is not the will or desire of any one person which establishes order but the moving spirit of the whole group. The control is social, but individuals are parts of a community, not outside of it.

我这样说并不是说，父母等权威在任何场合都不必干预和行使相当直接的控制。但我确实要说，首先，与所有参与的场合相比，这种场合的数量微不足道。更重要的是，当在一个管理良好的家庭或其他社区团体中行使权威时，这种权威不仅仅是个人意志的表现；父母或教师作为整个团体利益的代表和代理人行使权威。关于第一点，在一所秩序井然的学校里，对个人的控制主要依赖于所进行的活动和维持这些活动的环境。教师将他或她必须以个人方式行使权威的场合减少到最低限度。其次，当有必要坚定地说话和行动时，这是代表团体的利益，而不是为了展示个人权力。这就造成了武断行为与公正公平行为之间的区别。

I do not mean by this that there are no occasions upon which the authority of, say, the parent does not have to intervene and exercise fairly direct control. But I do say that, in the first place, the number of these occasions is slight in comparison with the number of those in which the control is exercised by situations in which all take part. And what is even more important, the authority in question when exercised in a well-regulated household or other community group is not a manifestation of merely personal will; the parent or teacher exercises it as the representative and agent of the interests of the group as a whole. With respect to the first point, in a well-ordered school the main reliance for control of this and that individual is upon the activities carried on and upon the situations in which these activities are maintained. The teacher reduces to a minimum the occasions in which he or she has to exercise authority in a personal way. When it is necessary, in the second place, to speak and act firmly, it is done in behalf of the interest of the group, not as an exhibition of personal power. This makes the difference between action which is arbitrary and that which is just and fair.

此外，教师或孩子无需用语言来阐述差异，就能在经验中感受到。很少有孩子感觉不到这种差异（即使他们无法清楚地表达出来，并将其归结为一种理智原则），这种差异是出于个人权力和指挥欲望的行动，与出于所有人利益的公平行动。我甚至可以说，总的来说，孩子们比成年人对这种差异的迹象和症状更敏感。孩子们在互相玩耍时学会了这种差异。他们愿意，往往太愿意了，接受一个孩子的建议，让他当领导，如果他的行为增加了他们所做事情的经验价值，而他们讨厌指挥的企图。然后他们经常退缩，当被问到原因时，他们会说这是因为某某“太专横了”。

Moreover, it is not necessary that the difference should be formulated in words, by either teacher or the young, in order to be felt in experience. The number of children who do not feel the difference (even if they cannot articulate it and reduce it to an intellectual principle) between action that is motivated by personal power and desire to dictate and action that is fair, because in the interest of all, is small. I should even be willing to say that upon the whole children are more sensitive to the signs and symptoms of this difference than are adults. Children learn the difference when playing with one another. They are willing, often too willing if anything, to take suggestions from one child and let him be a leader if his conduct adds to the experienced value of what they are doing, while they resent the attempt at dictation. Then they often withdraw and when asked why, say that it is because so-and-so “is too bossy.”

我不想用漫画代替图画的方式来描述传统学校。但我认为可以公平地说，教师的个人命令之所以经常发挥不应有的作用，以及存在的秩序在很大程度上是对成人意愿的绝对服从，是因为情况几乎迫使教师这样做。学校不是一个通过参与共同活动而团结在一起的团体或社区。因此，缺乏正常、适当的控制条件。这些条件的缺失由教师的直接干预来弥补，而且在很大程度上必须由教师的直接干预来弥补，正如俗话所说，教师“维持秩序”。他维持秩序是因为秩序由教师来维持，而不是存在于共同完成的工作中。

I do not wish to refer to the traditional school in ways which set up a caricature in lieu of a picture. But I think it is fair to say that one reason the personal commands of the teacher so often played an undue role and a reason why the order which existed was so much a matter of sheer obedience to the will of an adult was because the situation almost forced it upon the teacher. The school was not a group or community held together by participation in common activities. Consequently, the normal, proper conditions of control were lacking. Their absence was made up for, and to a considerable extent had to be made up for, by the direct intervention of the teacher, who, as the saying went, “kept order.” He kept it because order was in the teacher’s keeping, instead of residing in the shared work being done.

结论是，在所谓的新学校中，社会控制的主要来源在于工作本身，即作为一项社会事业，每个人都有机会做出贡献，每个人都感到有责任。大多数孩子天生就“善于社交”。孤立对他们来说比对成年人更令人厌烦。真正的社区生活以这种自然的社交为基础。但社区生活并不是纯粹自发地以持久的方式组织起来的。它需要提前思考和规划。教育者有责任了解个人和主题，以便选择适合社会组织的活动，在这个组织中，每个人都有机会做出贡献，所有人参与的活动是控制的主要载体。

The conclusion is that in what are called the new schools, the primary source of social control resides in the very nature of the work done as a social enterprise in which all individuals have an opportunity to contribute and to which all feel a responsibility. Most children are naturally “sociable.” Isolation is even more irksome to them than to adults. A genuine community life has its ground in this natural sociability. But community life does not organize itself in an enduring way purely spontaneously. It requires thought and planning ahead. The educator is responsible for a knowledge of individuals and for a knowledge of subject-matter that will enable activities to be selected which lend themselves to social organization, an organization in which all individuals have an opportunity to contribute something, and in which the activities in which all participate are the chief carrier of control.

我对年轻人的浪漫主义还不够，无法想象每个学生都会做出反应，或者任何通常冲动强烈的孩子​​在任何场合都会做出反应。有些人来学校时可能已经是校外有害环境的受害者，他们变得如此被动和过分温顺，以至于无法做出贡献。还有一些人，由于以前的经历，变得傲慢、不守规矩，甚至可能完全叛逆。但可以肯定的是，社会控制的一般原则不能以此类情况为依据。同样，没有一般规则可以处理此类情况。教师必须单独处理他们。他们属于一般类别，但没有两个完全相同。教育者必须尽可能地发现顽固态度的原因。如果教育过程要继续下去，他或她就不能把一个意志与另一个意志对立起来，看谁的意志更强大，也不能让不守规矩、不参与的学生永远阻碍其他人的教育活动。排除也许是特定时刻唯一可用的措施，但它不是解决办法。因为它可能会强化导致不良反社会态度的根本原因，比如渴望关注或炫耀。

I am not romantic enough about the young to suppose that every pupil will respond or that any child of normally strong impulses will respond on every occasion. There are likely to be some who, when they come to school, are already victims of injurious conditions outside of the school and who have become so passive and unduly docile that they fail to contribute. There will be others who, because of previous experience, are bumptious and unruly and perhaps downright rebellious. But it is certain that the general principle of social control cannot be predicated upon such cases. It is also true that no general rule can be laid down for dealing with such cases. The teacher has to deal with them individually. They fall into general classes, but no two are exactly alike. The educator has to discover as best he or she can the causes for the recalcitrant attitudes. He or she cannot, if the educational process is to go on, make it a question of pitting one will against another in order to see which is strongest, nor yet allow the unruly and non-participating pupils to stand permanently in the way of the educative activities of others. Exclusion perhaps is the only available measure at a given juncture, but it is no solution. For it may strengthen the very causes which have brought about the undesirable anti-social attitude, such as desire for attention or to show off.

例外很少能证明规则，或为规则应该是什么提供线索。因此，我不会过分重视这些例外情况，尽管目前进步学校确实经常会遇到比应有的份额更多的例外情况，因为父母可能会把孩子送到这样的学校作为最后的手段。我认为，进步学校控制薄弱的情况无论如何都不是源于这些例外情况。更有可能的是，未能提前安排好工作（我指的是从事的所有活动），这些工作会创造一种情况，这种情况本身倾向于控制这个、那个和其他学生做什么以及如何做。这种失败最常见的原因是缺乏足够周到的事先规划。造成这种缺乏的原因多种多样。在这方面特别值得一提的是，这种提前规划是不必要的，甚至本质上是对受教育者合法自由的敌意。

Exceptions rarely prove a rule or give a clew to what the rule should be. I would not, therefore, attach too much importance to these exceptional cases, although it is true at present that progressive schools are likely often to have more than their fair share of these cases, since parents may send children to such schools as a last resort. I do not think weakness in control when it is found in progressive schools arises in any event from these exceptional cases. It is much more likely to arise from failure to arrange in advance for the kind of work (by which I mean all kinds of activities engaged in) which will create situations that of themselves tend to exercise control over what this, that, and the other pupil does and how he does it. This failure most often goes back to lack of sufficiently thoughtful planning in advance. The causes for such lack are varied. The one which is peculiarly important to mention in this connection is the idea that such advance planning is unnecessary and even that it is inherently hostile to the legitimate freedom of those being instructed.

当然，教师完全有可能以一种僵硬、缺乏智力灵活性的方式进行准备计划，从而导致成人的强加，但这种强加仍然是外在的，因为执行得非常巧妙，表面上尊重个人自由。但这种计划并非从所涉及的原则中自然而然地得出的。我不知道教师的成熟度和对世界、学科和个人的更多了解有什么用，除非教师能够安排有利于社区活动和组织的条件，通过让所有人都参与公共项目来控制个人冲动。因为迄今为止所从事的提前计划已经非常常规，几乎没有给个人自由思考或因独特的个人经验而做出的贡献留下空间，所以这并不意味着必须拒绝所有的计划。相反，教育者有责任制定一种更聪明、因而更困难的计划。他必须调查他所面对的特定群体的能力和需求，同时必须安排好条件，为满足这些需求和发展这些能力的经验提供主题或内容。规划必须足够灵活，以允许经验的个性自由发挥，但又必须足够坚定，以指明不断发展力量的方向。

Now, of course, it is quite possible to have preparatory planning by the teacher done in such a rigid and intellectually inflexible fashion that it does result in adult imposition, which is none the less external because executed with tact and the semblance of respect for individual freedom. But this kind of planning does not follow inherently from the principle involved. I do not know what the greater maturity of the teacher and the teacher’s greater knowledge of the world, of subject-matters and of individuals, is for unless the teacher can arrange conditions that are conducive to community activity and to organization which exercises control over individual impulses by the mere fact that all are engaged in communal projects. Because the kind of advance planning heretofore engaged in has been so routine as to leave little room for the free play of individual thinking or for contributions due to distinctive individual experience, it does not follow that all planning must be rejected. On the contrary, there is incumbent upon the educator the duty of instituting a much more intelligent, and consequently more difficult, kind of planning. He must survey the capacities and needs of the particular set of individuals with whom he is dealing and must at the same time arrange the conditions which provide the subject-matter or content for experiences that satisfy these needs and develop these capacities. The planning must be flexible enough to permit free play for individuality of experience and yet firm enough to give direction towards continuous development of power.

现在正是时候谈谈教师的职责和职责。经验的发展是通过互动而产生的，这一原则意味着教育本质上是一个社会过程。这一性质体现在个人形成社区群体的程度上。将教师排除在群体之外是荒谬的。作为群体中最成熟的成员，他对互动和交流负有特殊的责任，而互动和交流是群体作为一个社区的生命。认为儿童是个人，他们的自由应该受到尊重，而较成熟的人作为个人则不应有自由，这种想法太荒谬了，无须反驳。将教师排除在社区活动的积极和领导之外的倾向是从一个极端转向另一个极端的另一个例子。当学生是一个阶级而不是一个社会群体时，教师必然主要从外部采取行动，而不是作为所有人都参与的交换过程的指导者。当教育以经验为基础，教育经验被视为一个社会过程时，情况就会发生根本变化。教师不再是外部老板或独裁者的地位，而是承担起集体活动领导者的角色。

The present occasion is a suitable one to say something about the province and office of the teacher. The principle that development of experience comes about through interaction means that education is essentially a social process. This quality is realized in the degree in which individuals form a community group. It is absurd to exclude the teacher from membership in the group. As the most mature member of the group he has a peculiar responsibility for the conduct of the interactions and intercommunications which are the very life of the group as a community. That children are individuals whose freedom should be respected while the more mature person should have no freedom as an individual is an idea too absurd to require refutation. The tendency to exclude the teacher from a positive and leading share in the direction of the activities of the community of which he is a member is another instance of reaction from one extreme to another. When pupils were a class rather than a social group, the teacher necessarily acted largely from the outside, not as a director of processes of exchange in which all had a share. When education is based upon experience and educative experience is seen to be a social process, the situation changes radically. The teacher loses the position of external boss or dictator but takes on that of leader of group activities.

在讨论游戏行为作为正常社会控制的一个例子时，提到了标准化惯例因素的存在。在学校生活中，这一因素的对应物是礼仪问题，尤其是礼貌和礼节表现中的良好礼仪问题。我们对人类历史不同时期世界各地风俗的了解越多，我们就越能了解礼仪在不同地点和不同时间之间的差异有多大。这一事实证明，其中存在很大的惯例因素。但是，任何时间或地点的任何群体都有一定的礼仪准则，例如，关于问候他人的适当方式。惯例的具体形式并不固定和绝对。但某种形式的惯例的存在本身并不是一种惯例。它是所有社会关系的统一伴随物。至少，它是防止或减少摩擦的润滑剂。

In discussing the conduct of games as an example of normal social control, reference was made to the presence of a standardized conventional factor. The counterpart of this factor in school life is found in the question of manners, especially of good manners in the manifestations of politeness and courtesy. The more we know about customs in different parts of the world at different times in the history of mankind, the more we learn how much manners differ from place to place and time to time. This fact proves that there is a large conventional factor involved. But there is no group at any time or place which does not have some code of manners as, for example, with respect to proper ways of greeting other persons. The particular form a convention takes has nothing fixed and absolute about it. But the existence of some form of convention is not itself a convention. It is a uniform attendant of all social relationships. At the very least, it is the oil which prevents or reduces friction.

当然，这些社交形式有可能变成我们所说的“纯粹的形式”。它们可能只是外在的表演，没有任何意义。但是，避免空洞的礼节性社交形式并不意味着拒绝所有形式要素。相反，它表明需要发展适合社交场合的社交形式。一些进步学校的访客对他们遇到的缺乏礼貌感到震惊。更了解情况的人知道，在某种程度上，他们的缺席是由于孩子们渴望继续他们正在做的事情。例如，在他们渴望的情况下，他们可能会撞到对方和访客，却没有道歉的话。人们可能会说，这种情况比仅仅表现出外在的拘谨，伴随着对学校工作的智力和情感缺乏兴趣要好。但它也代表着教育的失败，未能学到人生中最重要的一课，即相互适应和适应。教育正在以片面的方式进行，因为正在形成的态度和习惯阻碍了未来通过与他人轻松、便捷的接触和交流而产生的学习。

It is possible, of course, for these social forms to become, as we say, “mere formalities.” They may become merely outward show with no meaning behind them. But the avoidance of empty ritualistic forms of social intercourse does not mean the rejection of every formal element. It rather indicates the need for development of forms of intercourse that are inherently appropriate to social situations. Visitors to some progressive schools are shocked by the lack of manners they come across. One who knows the situation better is aware that to some extent their absence is due to the eager interest of children to go on with what they are doing. In their eagerness they may, for example, bump into each other and into visitors with no word of apology. One might say that this condition is better than a display of merely external punctilio accompanying intellectual and emotional lack of interest in school work. But it also represents a failure in education, a failure to learn one of the most important lessons of life, that of mutual accommodation and adaptation. Education is going on in a one-sided way, for attitudes and habits are in process of formation that stand in the way of the future learning that springs from easy and ready contact and communication with others.

第五章

Chapter 5

自由的本质

The Nature of Freedom

冒着重复我经常说的话的风险，我想谈谈社会控制问题的另一面，即自由的本质。唯一具有持久重要性的自由是智力自由，即为了内在有价值的目的而进行的观察和判断的自由。我认为，关于自由最常见的错误是把它等同于行动自由，或等同于活动的外部或身体方面。现在，活动的这种外部和身体方面不能与活动的内在方面分开；与思想、欲望和目的的自由。典型的传统教室的固定安排对外部行动施加了限制，教室里有固定的桌子排，学生的军事化制度，只有在某些固定信号下才被允许移动，这极大地限制了智力和道德自由。如果要让个人有机会在自由的智力源泉中成长，就必须废除束缚和锁链程序，没有这些程序就无法保证真正的和持续的正常成长。

AT THE RISK of repeating what has been often said by me I want to say something about the other side of the problem of social control, namely, the nature of freedom. The only freedom that is of enduring importance is freedom of intelligence, that is to say, freedom of observation and of judgment exercised in behalf of purposes that are intrinsically worth while. The commonest mistake made about freedom is, I think, to identify it with freedom of movement, or with the external or physical side of activity. Now, this external and physical side of activity cannot be separated from the internal side of activity; from freedom of thought, desire, and purpose. The limitation that was put upon outward action by the fixed arrangements of the typical traditional schoolroom, with its fixed rows of desks and its military regimen of pupils who were permitted to move only at certain fixed signals, put a great restriction upon intellectual and moral freedom. Straitjacket and chain-gang procedures had to be done away with if there was to be a chance for growth of individuals in the intellectual springs of freedom without which there is no assurance of genuine and continued normal growth.

但事实仍然是，增加外部行动的自由只是手段，而不是目的。获得这种自由并不意味着教育问题就解决了。就教育而言，一切都取决于如何利用这种增加的自由。它服务于什么目的？它会产生什么结果？首先，让我谈谈增加外部自由可能带来的好处。首先，没有这种自由，教师几乎不可能了解他所关心的个人。强迫学生保持安静和默许，阻止他们透露自己的真实本性。它们强制学生保持人为的统一。它们把外表置于本质之前。它们重视保持注意力、礼仪和服从的外表。熟悉这种制度盛行的学校的人都知道，思想、想象、欲望和狡猾的活动在这种假象的背后肆无忌惮地发展。只有当一些不当行为导致它们被发现时，它们才会向老师透露。只要将这种高度人为的情况与教室外的正常人际关系（比如在一个管理良好的家庭中）进行对比，就能体会到，教师与受教育者的熟悉和理解是多么致命。然而，如果没有这种洞察力，学习材料和教学方法就只能偶然地进入学生的内心，从而指导其心智和性格的发展。这是一个恶性循环。学习和方法的机械统一性造成了一种统一的僵化，这种僵化又使学习和背诵的统一性永久化，而在这种强制统一性的背后，个人倾向以不规则的、或多或少被禁止的方式运作。

But the fact still remains that an increased measure of freedom of outer movement is a means, not an end. The educational problem is not solved when this aspect of freedom is obtained. Everything then depends, so far as education is concerned, upon what is done with this added liberty. What end does it serve? What consequences flow from it? Let me speak first of the advantages which reside potentially in increase of outward freedom. In the first place, without its existence it is practically impossible for a teacher to gain knowledge of the individuals with whom he is concerned. Enforced quiet and acquiescence prevent pupils from disclosing their real natures. They enforce artificial uniformity. They put seeming before being. They place a premium upon preserving the outward appearance of attention, decorum, and obedience. And everyone who is acquainted with schools in which this system prevailed well knows that thoughts, imaginations, desires, and sly activities ran their own unchecked course behind this façade. They were disclosed to the teacher only when some untoward act led to their detection. One has only to contrast this highly artificial situation with normal human relations outside the schoolroom, say in a well-conducted home, to appreciate how fatal it is to the teacher’s acquaintance with and understanding of the individuals who are, supposedly, being educated. Yet without this insight there is only an accidental chance that the material of study and the methods used in instruction will so come home to an individual that his development of mind and character is actually directed. There is a vicious circle. Mechanical uniformity of studies and methods creates a kind of uniform immobility and this reacts to perpetuate uniformity of studies and of recitations, while behind this enforced uniformity individual tendencies operate in irregular and more or less forbidden ways.

增加外在自由的另一个重要优势在于学习过程的本质。有人指出，旧方法重视被动性和接受性。身体的静止极大地重视这些特征。在标准化学校中，唯一能摆脱这些特征的办法是进行不规律的、也许是不听话的活动。实验室或车间不可能完全安静。传统学校的非社会性体现在它将沉默作为其主要优点之一。当然，没有明显的身体活动，也存在着激烈的智力活动。但是，如果这种智力活动持续很长时间，那么这种智力活动的能力就意味着相对较晚的成就。即使是年轻人，也应该有短暂的安静思考时间。但只有在这些时间紧随更明显的行动之后，并用于组织在活动期间获得的东西时，这些时间才是真正的反思，在这些活动中，手和大脑以外的其他身体部位都会得到使用。行动自由也是保持正常身心健康的重要手段。我们仍要从希腊人的例子学习，他们清楚地看到了健全的身体和健全的心智之间的关系。但在上述所有方面，外在行动的自由是实现判断自由和实现精心选择的目标的手段。每个人所需的外在自由程度各不相同。随着成熟度的提高，这种自由自然会减少，但完全没有这种自由，即使是一个成熟的人也无法获得新的材料，使他的智力得以发挥。这种自由活动作为成长的手段，其数量和质量是教育者在发展的每个阶段都必须考虑的问题。

The other important advantage of increased outward freedom is found in the very nature of the learning process. That the older methods set a premium upon passivity and receptivity has been pointed out. Physical quiescence puts a tremendous premium upon these traits. The only escape from them in the standardized school is an activity which is irregular and perhaps disobedient. There cannot be complete quietude in a laboratory or workshop. The non-social character of the traditional school is seen in the fact that it erected silence into one of its prime virtues. There is, of course, such a thing as intense intellectual activity without overt bodily activity. But capacity for such intellectual activity marks a comparatively late achievement when it is continued for a long period. There should be brief intervals of time for quiet reflection provided for even the young. But they are periods of genuine reflection only when they follow after times of more overt action and are used to organize what has been gained in periods of activity in which the hands and other parts of the body beside the brain are used. Freedom of movement is also important as a means of maintaining normal physical and mental health. We have still to learn from the example of the Greeks who saw clearly the relation between a sound body and a sound mind. But in all the respects mentioned freedom of outward action is a means to freedom of judgment and of power to carry deliberately chosen ends into execution. The amount of external freedom which is needed varies from individual to individual. It naturally tends to decrease with increasing maturity, though its complete absence prevents even a mature individual from having the contacts which will provide him with new materials upon which his intelligence may exercise itself. The amount and the quality of this kind of free activity as a means of growth is a problem that must engage the thought of the educator at every stage of development.

然而，最大的错误莫过于将这种自由视为目的本身。它往往会破坏作为秩序正常来源的共享合作活动。但另一方面，它把本应是积极的自由变成了消极的东西。因为不受限制的自由，也就是消极的一面，只能作为实现权力的手段来珍视：制定目的、明智判断、根据行动的后果来评估欲望的权力；选择和命令的权力意味着将选定的目的付诸实施。

There can be no greater mistake, however, than to treat such freedom as an end in itself. It then tends to be destructive of the shared cooperative activities which are the normal source of order. But, on the other hand, it turns freedom which should be positive into something negative. For freedom from restriction, the negative side, is to be prized only as a means to a freedom which is power: power to frame purposes, to judge wisely, to evaluate desires by the consequences which will result from acting upon them; power to select and order means to carry chosen ends into operation.

无论如何，自然的冲动和欲望构成了起点。但是，如果不对冲动和欲望进行某种重建、改造，使其恢复到它们最初出现的形式，就不会有智力的增长。这种改造涉及对冲动的最初状态的抑制。外部强加的抑制的替代方法是通过个人自己的反思和判断进行抑制。老话“停下来思考”是合理的心理学。因为思考就是停止冲动的直接表现，直到这种冲动与其他可能的行动倾向联系起来，从而形成一个更全面、更连贯的活动计划。其他一些行动倾向导致使用眼睛、耳朵和手来观察客观情况；其他一些导致回忆过去发生的事情。因此，思考是对立即行动的推迟，同时它通过观察和记忆的结合对冲动进行内部控制，这种结合是反思的核心。上述内容解释了老生常谈的“自我控制”一词的含义。教育的理想目标是创造自我控制的力量。但仅仅取消外部控制并不能保证产生自我控制。跳出油锅又跳进火坑是很容易的。换句话说，很容易摆脱一种外部控制，却发现自己处于另一种更危险的外部控制之下。不受智力控制的冲动和欲望受偶然情况的控制。摆脱他人的控制，却发现自己的行为受一时的心血来潮和反复无常的支配，这可能是一种损失而不是收获；也就是说，受冲动的摆布，而这种冲动的形成并没有经过智力判断。以这种方式控制行为的人最多只能拥有自由的幻觉。实际上，他受到他无法控制的力量的指挥。

Natural impulses and desires constitute in any case the starting point. But there is no intellectual growth without some reconstruction, some remaking, of impulses and desires in the form in which they first show themselves. This remaking involves inhibition of impulse in its first estate. The alternative to externally imposed inhibition is inhibition through an individual’s own reflection and judgment. The old phrase “stop and think” is sound psychology. For thinking is stoppage of the immediate manifestation of impulse until that impulse has been brought into connection with other possible tendencies to action so that a more comprehensive and coherent plan of activity is formed. Some of the other tendencies to action lead to use of eye, ear, and hand to observe objective conditions; others result in recall of what has happened in the past. Thinking is thus a postponement of immediate action, while it effects internal control of impulse through a union of observation and memory, this union being the heart of reflection. What has been said explains the meaning of the well-worn phrase “self-control.” The ideal aim of education is creation of power of self-control. But the mere removal of external control is no guarantee for the production of self-control. It is easy to jump out of the frying-pan into the fire. It is easy, in other words, to escape one form of external control only to find oneself in another and more dangerous form of external control. Impulses and desires that are not ordered by intelligence are under the control of accidental circumstances. It may be a loss rather than a gain to escape from the control of another person only to find one’s conduct dictated by immediate whim and caprice; that is, at the mercy of impulses into whose formation intelligent judgment has not entered. A person whose conduct is controlled in this way has at most only the illusion of freedom. Actually he is directed by forces over which he has no command.

第六章

Chapter 6

目的的意义

The Meaning of Purpose

因此，这是一种健全的本能，它将自由等同于制定目的和执行或实现所制定目的的权力。这种自由反过来又等同于自我控制；因为制定目的和组织执行目的的手段是智慧的工作。柏拉图曾经将奴隶定义为执行他人目的的人，正如刚才所说，被自己盲目欲望所奴役的人也是奴隶。我认为，进步教育哲学中没有比强调学习者参与制定指导其学习过程活动的目的的重要性更健全的了，正如传统教育中没有比未能确保学生积极合作构建学习目的更大的缺陷一样。但目的和目标的意义并不是不言而喻和不言自明的。它们在教育中的重要性被强调得越多，理解目的是什么、目的如何产生以及目的在经验中如何发挥作用就越重要。

IT IS, then, a sound instinct which identifies freedom with power to frame purposes and to execute or carry into effect purposes so framed. Such freedom is in turn identical with self-control; for the formation of purposes and the organization of means to execute them are the work of intelligence. Plato once defined a slave as the person who executes the purposes of another, and, as has just been said, a person is also a slave who is enslaved to his own blind desires. There is, I think, no point in the philosophy of progressive education which is sounder than its emphasis upon the importance of the participation of the learner in the formation of the purposes which direct his activities in the learning process, just as there is no defect in traditional education greater than its failure to secure the active co-operation of the pupil in construction of the purposes involved in his studying. But the meaning of purposes and ends is not self-evident and self-explanatory. The more their educational importance is emphasized, the more important it is to understand what a purpose is; how it arises and how it functions in experience.

真正的目的总是始于冲动。阻碍冲动的立即执行会将其转化为欲望。然而，冲动和欲望本身都不是目的。目的是一种最终目的。也就是说，它涉及预见冲动所导致的后果。预见后果需要智慧的运作。它首先要求观察客观条件和环境。因为冲动和欲望不仅会单独产生后果，还会通过它们与周围条件的相互作用或合作产生后果。像走路这样简单动作的冲动只有在与所站的地面积极结合的情况下才会产生。在一般情况下，我们不必太注意地面。在棘手的情况下，我们必须非常仔细地观察条件，就像爬一座陡峭崎岖、没有小径的山一样。因此，观察练习是将冲动转化为目的的一个条件。就像在铁路道口的标志处一样，我们必须停下来，看，听。

A genuine purpose always starts with an impulse. Obstruction of the immediate execution of an impulse converts it into a desire. Nevertheless neither impulse nor desire is itself a purpose. A purpose is an end-view. That is, it involves foresight of the consequences which will result from acting upon impulse. Foresight of consequences involves the operation of intelligence. It demands, in the first place, observation of objective conditions and circumstances. For impulse and desire produce consequences not by themselves alone but through their interaction or cooperation with surrounding conditions. The impulse for such a simple action as walking is executed only in active conjunction with the ground on which one stands. Under ordinary circumstances, we do not have to pay much attention to the ground. In a ticklish situation we have to observe very carefully just what the conditions are, as in climbing a steep and rough mountain where no trail has been laid out. Exercise of observation is, then, one condition of transformation of impulse into a purpose. As in the sign by a railway crossing, we have to stop, look, listen.

但仅靠观察是不够的。我们必须理解我们所看到、听到和触摸到的事物的意义。这种意义包括将所看到的东西付诸行动时将产生的后果。婴儿可能会看到火焰的亮度，并因此被吸引去伸手去拿它。那么，火焰的意义就不在于它的亮度，而在于它的燃烧能力，也就是触摸它所产生的后果。我们只能通过以前的经验来意识到后果。在那些由于许多以前的经验而熟悉的情况下，我们不必停下来去回忆那些经验到底是什么。火焰象征着光和热，而我们不必明确地去思考以前热和燃烧的经历。但在不熟悉的情况下，除非我们在脑海中回顾过去的经历，除非我们反思它们，并通过看到它们与现在的情况有什么相似之处，继续对当前情况下可能发生的事情做出判断，否则我们无法说出观察到的情况将会产生什么后果。

But observation alone is not enough. We have to understand the significance of what we see, hear, and touch. This significance consists of the consequences that will result when what is seen is acted upon. A baby may see the brightness of a flame and be attracted thereby to reach for it. The significance of the flame is then not its brightness but its power to burn, as the consequence that will result from touching it. We can be aware of consequences only because of previous experiences. In cases that are familiar because of many prior experiences we do not have to stop to remember just what those experiences were. A flame comes to signify light and heat without our having expressly to think of previous experiences of heat and burning. But in unfamiliar cases, we cannot tell just what the consequences of observed conditions will be unless we go over past experiences in our mind, unless we reflect upon them and by seeing what is similar in them to those now present, go on to form a judgment of what may be expected in the present situation.

因此，目的的形成是一种相当复杂的智力活动。它涉及（1）观察周围环境；（2）了解过去类似情况下发生的事情，这种知识部分来自回忆，部分来自那些有更广泛经验的人的信息、建议和警告；（3）判断，将观察到的内容和回忆的内容结合起来，看看它们意味着什么。目的不同于最初的冲动和愿望，因为它转化为一种行动计划和方法，这种计划和方法基于对在给定观察条件下以某种方式行动的后果的预见。“如果愿望是马，乞丐会骑。”对某事的渴望可能很强烈。这种渴望可能强烈到超越了对行动后果的估计。这种情况不能为教育提供模式。关键的教育问题是推迟对愿望的直接行动，直到观察和判断介入。如果我没有弄错的话，这一点肯定与进步学校的管理有关。过分强调活动是目的，而不是智力活动，导致将自由等同于立即执行冲动和欲望。这种等同是通过将冲动与目的相混淆而成立的；尽管如前所述，除非将明显的行动推迟到预见到将冲动付诸实施的后果，否则就没有目的——没有观察、信息和判断就不可能有预见。当然，仅仅有预见，即使它表现为准确的预测，也是不够的。理智的预期，即后果的观念，必须与欲望和冲动相结合才能获得动力。然后，它为盲目的事物指明了方向，而欲望则为观念提供了动力和动量。然后，一个想法就变成了一个计划，一个活动的计划。假设一个人想获得一个新家，比如说建一所房子。不管他的愿望有多强烈，都不能直接实现。这个人必须形成一个想法，他想要什么样的房子，包括房间的数量和布置等。他必须画出一个计划，并制作蓝图和规格说明。除非他也盘点一下自己的资源，否则这一切可能只是闲暇时的无聊消遣。他必须考虑资金和可用信贷与计划执行之间的关系。他必须调查可用的地点、价格、与他的营业地点的距离、与宜人的社区的距离、与学校设施的距离等等。所有考虑的因素：他的支付能力、家庭规模和需求、可能的地点等都是客观事实。它们不是原始愿望的一部分。但必须对它们进行查看和判断，以便将愿望转化为目的，将目的转化为行动计划。

The formation of purposes is, then, a rather complex intellectual operation. It involves (1) observation of surrounding conditions; (2) knowledge of what has happened in similar situations in the past, a knowledge obtained partly by recollection and partly from the information, advice, and warning of those who have had a wider experience; and (3) judgment which puts together what is observed and what is recalled to see what they signify. A purpose differs from an original impulse and desire through its translation into a plan and method of action based upon foresight of the consequences of acting under given observed conditions in a certain way. “If wishes were horses, beggars would ride.” Desire for something may be intense. It may be so strong as to override estimation of the consequences that will follow acting upon it. Such occurrences do not provide the model for education. The crucial educational problem is that of procuring the postponement of immediate action upon desire until observation and judgment have intervened. Unless I am mistaken, this point is definitely relevant to the conduct of progressive schools. Overemphasis upon activity as an end, instead of upon intelligent activity, leads to identification of freedom with immediate execution of impulses and desires. This identification is justified by a confusion of impulse with purpose; although, as has just been said, there is no purpose unless overt action is postponed until there is foresight of the consequences of carrying the impulse into execution—a foresight that is impossible without observation, information, and judgment. Mere foresight, even if it takes the form of accurate prediction, is not, of course, enough. The intellectual anticipation, the idea of consequences, must blend with desire and impulse to acquire moving force. It then gives direction to what otherwise is blind, while desire gives ideas impetus and momentum. An idea then becomes a plan in and for an activity to be carried out. Suppose a man has a desire to secure a new home, say by building a house. No matter how strong his desire, it cannot be directly executed. The man must form an idea of what kind of house he wants, including the number and arrangement of rooms, etc. He has to draw a plan, and have blue prints and specifications made. All this might be an idle amusement for spare time unless he also took stock of his resources. He must consider the relation of his funds and available credit to the execution of the plan. He has to investigate available sites, their price, their nearness to his place of business, to a congenial neighborhood, to school facilities, and so on and so on. All of the things reckoned with: his ability to pay, size and needs of family, possible locations, etc., etc., are objective facts. They are no part of the original desire. But they have to be viewed and judged in order that a desire may be converted into a purpose and a purpose into a plan of action.

我们所有人都有欲望，至少那些还没有病态到完全麻木不仁的人都有欲望。这些欲望是行动的最终推动力。一个职业商人希望事业有成；一个将军希望打赢这场战争；一个父母希望有一个舒适的家，让家人受教育，等等。欲望的强度衡量了将要付出的努力的强度。但是，除非把这些愿望转化为可以实现的手段，否则它们就是空中楼阁。手段多快或多快的问题取代了预期的想象结果，而且，由于手段是客观的，所以如果要形成真正的目的，就必须研究和理解它们。

All of us have desires, all at least who have not become so pathological that they are completely apathetic. These desires are the ultimate moving springs of action. A professional businessman wishes to succeed in his career; a general wishes to win the battle; a parent to have a comfortable home for his family, and to educate his children, and so on indefinitely. The intensity of the desire measures the strength of the efforts that will be put forth. But the wishes are empty castles in the air unless they are translated into the means by which they may be realized. The question of how soon or of means takes the place of a projected imaginative end, and, since means are objective, they have to be studied and understood if a genuine purpose is to be formed.

传统教育往往忽视个人冲动和欲望作为推动力的重要性。但这并不是进步教育将冲动和欲望等同于目的的理由，从而忽视了学生参与形成激发他们的目的时需要仔细观察、广泛了解和判断的必要性。在教育计划中，欲望和冲动的出现并不是最终目的。它是形成活动计划和方法的机会和要求。重申一下，只有通过研究条件和获取所有相关信息才能形成这样的计划。

Traditional education tended to ignore the importance of personal impulse and desire as moving springs. But this is no reason why progressive education should identify impulse and desire with purpose and thereby pass lightly over the need for careful observation, for wide range of information, and for judgment if students are to share in the formation of the purposes which activate them. In an educational scheme, the occurrence of a desire and impulse is not the final end. It is an occasion and a demand for the formation of a plan and method of activity. Such a plan, to repeat, can be formed only by study of conditions and by securing all relevant information.

教师的任务是确保学生能充分利用这个机会。由于自由存在于发展目标的明智观察和判断中，因此教师对学生智力运用的指导是对自由的帮助，而不是对自由的限制。有时教师似乎甚至不敢向小组成员提出建议，告诉他们应该做什么。我听说过这样的情况：孩子们被物品和材料包围着，然后完全被放任不管，教师甚至不愿意建议如何处理这些材料，以免侵犯自由。那么，既然材料是某种建议的来源，为什么还要提供材料呢？但更重要的是，学生采取行动的建议无论如何都必须来自某个地方。很难理解，为什么经验更丰富、视野更开阔的人的建议，竟然不如来自某种或多或少偶然来源的建议有效。

The teacher’s business is to see that the occasion is taken advantage of. Since freedom resides in the operations of intelligent observation and judgment by which a purpose is developed, guidance given by the teacher to the exercise of the pupils’ intelligence is an aid to freedom, not a restriction upon it. Sometimes teachers seem to be afraid even to make suggestions to the members of a group as to what they should do. I have heard of cases in which children are surrounded with objects and materials and then left entirely to themselves, the teacher being loath to suggest even what might be done with the materials lest freedom be infringed upon. Why, then, even supply materials, since they are a source of some suggestion or other? But what is more important is that the suggestion upon which pupils act must in any case come from somewhere. It is impossible to understand why a suggestion from one who has a larger experience and a wider horizon should not be at least as valid as a suggestion arising from some more or less accidental source.

当然，滥用职权是有可能的，会迫使年轻人的活动按照教师的目的而不是学生的目的进行。但避免这种危险的方法并不是让成年人完全退出。首先，教师要明智地了解受教学生的能力、需要和过去的经验；其次，让所提建议通过小组成员进一步提出并组织成一个整体的建议发展成一个计划和项目。换句话说，这个计划是一个合作项目，而不是命令。教师的建议不是一成不变的结果的模子，而是一个起点，通过所有参与学习过程的人的经验贡献，可以发展成一个计划。发展是通过相互的给予和接受而发生的，教师接受但不害怕给予。关键是，目的是通过社会智慧的过程发展和形成的。

It is possible of course to abuse the office, and to force the activity of the young into channels which express the teacher’s purpose rather than that of the pupils. But the way to avoid this danger is not for the adult to withdraw entirely. The way is, first, for the teacher to be intelligently aware of the capacities, needs, and past experiences of those under instruction, and, secondly, to allow the suggestion made to develop into a plan and project by means of the further suggestions contributed and organized into a whole by the members of the group. The plan, in other words, is a co-operative enterprise, not a dictation. The teacher’s suggestion is not a mold for a cast-iron result but is a starting point to be developed into a plan through contributions from the experience of all engaged in the learning process. The development occurs through reciprocal give-and-take, the teacher taking but not being afraid also to give. The essential point is that the purpose grow and take shape through the process of social intelligence.

Chapter 7



主题的渐进组织

Progressive Organization of

Subject-Matter

我们已经多次提到经验中涉及的客观条件，以及它们在促进或未能促进进一步经验丰富增长方面的作用。从某种意义上说，这些客观条件，无论是观察、记忆、从他人那里获得的信息，还是想象，都与学习和学习的主题有关；或者更笼统地说，与学习课程的内容有关。然而，到目前为止，还没有明确提到主题本身。现在将讨论这个话题。当教育以经验的形式来构思时，一个考虑因素很明显。任何可以称为学习的东西，无论是算术、历史、地理还是自然科学之一，都必须从最初属于普通生活经验范围的材料中得出。在这方面，新教育与从超出受教者经验范围的事实和真理开始的程序形成了鲜明的对比，因此，这些程序存在着发现将它们纳入经验的方法和手段的问题。毫无疑问，早期基础教育的新方法取得巨大成功的一个主要原因是它遵循了相反的原则。

ALLUSION HAS been made in passing a number of times to objective conditions involved in experience and to their function in promoting or failing to promote the enriched growth of further experience. By implication, these objective conditions, whether those of observation, of memory, of information procured from others, or of imagination, have been identified with the subject-matter of study and learning; or, speaking more generally, with the stuff of the course of study. Nothing, however, has been said explicitly so far about subject-matter as such. That topic will now be discussed. One consideration stands out clearly when education is conceived in terms of experience. Anything which can be called a study, whether arithmetic, history, geography, or one of the natural sciences, must be derived from materials which at the outset fall within the scope of ordinary life-experience. In this respect the newer education contrasts sharply with procedures which start with facts and truths that are outside the range of the experience of those taught, and which, therefore, have the problem of discovering ways and means of bringing them within experience. Undoubtedly one chief cause for the great success of newer methods in early elementary education has been its observance of the contrary principle.

但是，在经验中找到学习材料只是第一步。下一步是将已经体验到的内容逐渐发展成更充分、更丰富、更有组织的形式，这种形式逐渐接近向熟练的成熟人士呈现主题的形式。这种变化在不脱离教育与经验的有机联系的情况下是可能的，这一点可以从以下事实中看出：这种变化发生在学校之外，与正规教育无关。例如，婴儿从一个在空间和时间上非常有限的物体环境开始。在没有学术指导的帮助下，这种环境凭借经验本身固有的动力稳步扩大。随着婴儿学会伸手、爬、走路和说话，其经验的内在主题不断扩大和加深。它与新的物体和事件联系在一起，这些物体和事件呼唤新的力量，而这些力量的运用则完善和扩大了其经验的内容。生活空间和生命持续时间得到了扩展。环境，即经验世界，不断变得更大，可以说，变得更厚重。在这个时期结束时接收孩子的教育者必须找到有意识地、刻意地完成“自然”在早年所完成的事情的方法。

But finding the material for learning within experience is only the first step. The next step is the progressive development of what is already experienced into a fuller and richer and also more organized form, a form that gradually approximates that in which subject-matter is presented to the skilled, mature person. That this change is possible without departing from the organic connection of education with experience is shown by the fact that this change takes place outside of the school and apart from formal education. The infant, for example, begins with an environment of objects that is very restricted in space and time. That environment steadily expands by the momentum inherent in experience itself without aid from scholastic instruction. As the infant learns to reach, creep, walk, and talk, the intrinsic subject-matter of its experience widens and deepens. It comes into connection with new objects and events which call out new powers, while the exercise of these powers refines and enlarges the content of its experience. Life-space and life-durations are expanded. The environment, the world of experience, constantly grows larger and, so to speak, thicker. The educator who receives the child at the end of this period has to find ways for doing consciously and deliberately what “nature” accomplishes in the earlier years.

认为将经验引向不同事物的原则只要给予学生一些新的经验就足够了，只要确保他们能够更熟练、更轻松地处理他们已经熟悉的事物就足够了，这是错误的。同样重要的是，新的事物和事件必须与先前的经验在智力上联系起来，这意味着在有意识地表达事实和思想方面取得了一些进步。因此，教育者的职责就是在现有经验范围内选择那些有希望和潜力提出新问题的事物，通过激发新的观察和判断方式，扩大进一步经验的领域。他必须始终把已经获得的东西视为一种机构和手段，而不是固定的财产，以开辟新的领域，这些领域对现有的观察能力和明智地使用记忆提出了新的要求。成长中的联系必须是他不变的口号。

It is a mistake to suppose that the principle of the leading on of experience to something different is adequately satisfied simply by giving pupils some new experiences any more than it is by seeing to it that they have greater skill and ease in dealing with things with which they are already familiar. It is also essential that the new objects and events be related intellectually to those of earlier experiences, and this means that there be some advance made in conscious articulation of facts and ideas. It thus becomes the office of the educator to select those things within the range of existing experience that have the promise and potentiality of presenting new problems which by stimulating new ways of observation and judgment will expand the area of further experience. He must constantly regard what is already won not as a fixed possession but as an agency and instrumentality for opening new fields which make new demands upon existing powers of observation and of intelligent use of memory. Connectedness in growth must be his constant watchword.

教育者比任何其他职业的成员都更关心长远的未来。当医生使病人恢复健康时，他可能会觉得他的工作已经完成了。毫无疑问，他有义务建议病人如何生活，以避免将来再遇到类似的麻烦。但毕竟，他的生活是他自己的事，而不是医生的事；对于目前的观点来说，更重要的是，只要医生确实致力于指导和建议病人的未来，他就承担了教育者的职责。律师忙于为他的客户打赢官司，或帮助后者摆脱他自己陷入的某种困境。如果超出了他所面临的案件，他也会成为教育者。教育者根据其工作的性质，有义务从它为未来取得的成就或未能取得的成就来看待他目前的工作，而未来的目标是与现在的目标相联系的。

The educator more than the member of any other profession is concerned to have a long look ahead. The physician may feel his job done when he has restored a patient to health. He has undoubtedly the obligation of advising him how to live so as to avoid similar troubles in the future. But, after all, the conduct of his life is his own affair, not the physician’s; and what is more important for the present point is that as far as the physician does occupy himself with instruction and advice as to the future of his patient he takes upon himself the function of an educator. The lawyer is occupied with winning a suit for his client or getting the latter out of some complication into which he has got himself. If it goes beyond the case presented to him he too becomes an educator. The educator by the very nature of his work is obliged to see his present work in terms of what it accomplishes, or fails to accomplish, for a future whose objects are linked with those of the present.

在这里，进步教育者面临的问题比传统学校的教师更困难。后者确实必须向前看。但除非他的个性和热情能使他超越传统学校的限制，否则他只能满足于考虑下一个考试期或升入下一个班级。他可以根据传统学校制度要求的因素来设想未来。将教育与实际经验结合起来的教师有责任做一件更严肃、更艰巨的工作。他必须意识到将学生带入属于已有经验的新领域的潜力，并且必须利用这些知识作为选择和安排影响他们当前经验的条件的标准。

Here, again, the problem for the progressive educator is more difficult than for the teacher in the traditional school. The latter had indeed to look ahead. But unless his personality and enthusiasm took him beyond the limits that hedged in the traditional school, he could content himself with thinking of the next examination period or the promotion to the next class. He could envisage the future in terms of factors that lay within the requirements of the school system as that conventionally existed. There is incumbent upon the teacher who links education and actual experience together a more serious and a harder business. He must be aware of the potentialities for leading students into new fields which belong to experiences already had, and must use this knowledge as his criterion for selection and arrangement of the conditions that influence their present experience.

由于传统学校的学习内容是根据成年人对未来某个时候年轻人有用的判断来选择和安排的，因此要学习的内容是在学习者当前生活经验之外确定的。因此，它与过去有关；它与过去时代对人们有用的东西有关。由于对相反极端的反应，尽管在这种情况下这很不幸，但教育应该从当前经验中获取材料，应该使学习者能够应对当前和未来的问题，这一正确观念常常被转化为进步学校可以在很大程度上忽视过去的观念。如果现在能与过去区分开来，这个结论是正确的。但过去的成就是理解现在的唯一手段。正如个人必须借助自己的过去来理解自己所处的状况一样，当今社会生活中的问题与过去有着如此密切而直接的联系，以至于学生如果不深入探究这些问题的过去根源，就无法理解这些问题或找到解决这些问题的最佳方法。换句话说，学习的目标在于未来，而学习的直接材料在于当前的经验这一合理原则只有在当前经验被追溯到过去的程度时才能发挥作用。只有当它被扩大到吸收过去时，它才能扩展到未来。

Because the studies of the traditional school consisted of subject-matter that was selected and arranged on the basis of the judgment of adults as to what would be useful for the young sometime in the future, the material to be learned was settled upon outside the present life-experience of the learner. In consequence, it had to do with the past; it was such as had proved useful to men in past ages. By reaction to an opposite extreme, as unfortunate as it was probably natural under the circumstances, the sound idea that education should derive its materials from present experience and should enable the learner to cope with the problems of the present and future has often been converted into the idea that progressive schools can to a very large extent ignore the past. If the present could be cut off from the past, this conclusion would be sound. But the achievements of the past provide the only means at command for understanding the present. Just as the individual has to draw in memory upon his own past to understand the conditions in which he individually finds himself, so the issues and problems of present social life are in such intimate and direct connection with the past that students cannot be prepared to understand either these problems or the best way of dealing with them without delving into their roots in the past. In other words, the sound principle that the objectives of learning are in the future and its immediate materials are in present experience can be carried into effect only in the degree that present experience is stretched, as it were, backward. It can expand into the future only as it is also enlarged to take in the past.

如果时间允许，讨论当代人将来将不得不面对的政治和经济问题将使这一一般性陈述更加明确和具体。除非我们知道这些问题是如何产生的，否则我们无法理解这些问题的性质。目前存在的、导致当前社会弊病和混乱的制度和习俗并非一夜之间形成的。它们背后有着悠久的历史。仅仅根据目前显而易见的情况来处理这些问题，必然会导致采取肤浅的措施，最终只会使现有问题更加严重，更难解决。仅仅基于对现在的了解而脱离过去而制定的政策，与个人行为的粗心大意是相对应的。摆脱将过去作为目的本身的学术体系的方法是让了解过去成为理解现在的手段。在解决这个问题之前，目前教育理念和实践的冲突将继续下去。一方面，有些反动派声称教育的主要（如果不是唯一的话）任务是传承文化遗产。另一方面，有些人认为我们应该忽视过去，只关注现在和未来。

If time permitted, discussion of the political and economic issues which the present generation will be compelled to face in the future would render this general statement definite and concrete. The nature of the issues cannot be understood save as we know how they came about. The institutions and customs that exist in the present and that give rise to present social ills and dislocations did not arise overnight. They have a long history behind them. Attempt to deal with them simply on the basis of what is obvious in the present is bound to result in adoption of superficial measures which in the end will only render existing problems more acute and more difficult to solve. Policies framed simply upon the ground of knowledge of the present cut off from the past is the counterpart of heedless carelessness in individual conduct. The way out of scholastic systems that made the past an end in itself is to make acquaintance with the past a means of understanding the present. Until this problem is worked out, the present clash of educational ideas and practices will continue. On the one hand, there will be reactionaries that claim that the main, if not the sole, business of education is transmission of the cultural heritage. On the other hand, there will be those who hold that we should ignore the past and deal only with the present and future.

我认为，到目前为止，进步学校最薄弱的一点是知识科目的选择和组织，在这种情况下，这是不可避免的。它们必须摆脱旧教育中固定不变的材料，这是不可避免的，也是理所当然的。此外，经验领域非常广泛，其内容因地而异，因时而异。为所有进步学校开设一门课程是不可能的；这意味着放弃与生活经验联系的基本原则。此外，进步学校是新生事物。它们的发展时间还不到一代人。因此，在选择和组织科目方面存在一定程度的不确定性和松懈是意料之中的。这不是根本批评或抱怨的理由。

That up to the present time the weakest point in progressive schools is in the matter of selection and organization of intellectual subject-matter is, I think, inevitable under the circumstances. It is as inevitable as it is right and proper that they should break loose from the cut and dried material which formed the staple of the old education. In addition, the field of experience is very wide and it varies in its contents from place to place and from time to time. A single course of studies for all progressive schools is out of the question; it would mean abandoning the fundamental principle of connection with life-experiences. Moreover, progressive schools are new. They have had hardly more than a generation in which to develop. A certain amount of uncertainty and of laxity in choice and organization of subject-matter is, therefore, what was to be expected. It is no ground for fundamental criticism or complaint.

然而，当正在进行的进步教育运动未能认识到选择和组织学习主题的问题是根本问题时，这是受到合理批评的理由。利用特殊场合的即兴发挥可以防止教学和学习被定型和死板。但学习的基本材料不能草率地捡起来。只要有思想自由，就必然会出现不可预见的场合。应该利用它们。但在发展持续的活动路线中使用它们与信任它们提供主要的学习材料之间存在着明显的区别。

It is a ground for legitimate criticism, however, when the ongoing movement of progressive education fails to recognize that the problem of selection and organization of subject-matter for study and learning is fundamental. Improvisation that takes advantage of special occasions prevents teaching and learning from being stereotyped and dead. But the basic material of study cannot be picked up in a cursory manner. Occasions which are not and cannot be foreseen are bound to arise wherever there is intellectual freedom. They should be utilized. But there is a decided difference between using them in the development of a continuing line of activity and trusting to them to provide the chief material of learning.

除非给定的经验引领学生进入一个以前不熟悉的领域，否则就不会出现问题，而问题则是思考的刺激因素。应将当前经验中发现的条件用作问题的来源，这是基于经验的教育与传统教育的区别之一。因为在传统教育中，问题是由外部设定的。尽管如此，成长取决于是否存在需要通过运用智力来克服的困难。再一次，教育者有责任平等地看待两件事：首先，问题源于当前经验的条件，并且是在学生能力范围内；其次，问题激发学习者积极寻求信息和产生新想法。这样获得的新事实和新想法成为进一步经验的基础，新问题由此产生。这个过程是一个连续的螺旋。现在与过去不可避免地联系在一起，这一原则的应用不仅限于历史研究。以自然科学为例。当代社会生活之所以如此，很大程度上得益于物理科学的应用成果。乡村和城市中每个儿童和青年的经历之所以如此，是因为利用了电、热和化学过程的器具。儿童不会吃不涉及化学和生理原理的饭菜。他不会在人造光下看书，也不会坐汽车或火车，而不会接触到科学产生的操作和过程。

Unless a given experience leads out into a field previously unfamiliar no problems arise, while problems are the stimulus to thinking. That the conditions found in present experience should be used as sources of problems is a characteristic which differentiates education based upon experience from traditional education. For in the latter, problems were set from outside. Nonetheless, growth depends upon the presence of difficulty to be overcome by the exercise of intelligence. Once more, it is part of the educator’s responsibility to see equally to two things: First, that the problem grows out of the conditions of the experience being had in the present, and that it is within the range of the capacity of students; and, secondly, that it is such that it arouses in the learner an active quest for information and for production of new ideas. The new facts and new ideas thus obtained become the ground for further experiences in which new problems are presented. The process is a continuous spiral. The inescapable linkage of the present with the past is a principle whose application is not restricted to a study of history. Take natural science, for example. Contemporary social life is what it is in very large measure because of the results of application of physical science. The experience of every child and youth, in the country and the city, is what it is in its present actuality because of appliances which utilize electricity, heat, and chemical processes. A child does not eat a meal that does not involve in its preparation and assimilation chemical and physiological principles. He does not read by artificial light or take a ride in a motor car or on a train without coming into contact with operations and processes which science has engendered.

正确的教育原则是，学生应该通过日常社会应用接触科学主题，并了解其事实和规律。坚持这种方法不仅是了解科学本身的最直接途径，而且随着学生的成熟，这也是了解当今社会经济和工业问题的最可靠途径。因为它们在很大程度上是科学在商品和服务的生产和分配中的应用的产物，而后者的过程是决定人类和社会群体之间当前关系的最重要因素。因此，认为类似于实验室和研究机构中研究的过程不是年轻人日常生活经验的一部分，因此不属于基于经验的教育范围，这是荒谬的。不成熟的人不能像成熟的专家那样研究科学事实和原理，这是不言而喻的。但这一事实并没有免除教育者利用现有经验的责任，从而使学习者能够通过提取事实和规律逐渐获得科学秩序的体验，这成为了教育者面临的主要问题之一。

It is a sound educational principle that students should be introduced to scientific subject-matter and be initiated into its facts and laws through acquaintance with everyday social applications. Adherence to this method is not only the most direct avenue to understanding of science itself but as the pupils grow more mature it is also the surest road to the understanding of the economic and industrial problems of present society. For they are the products to a very large extent of the application of science in production and distribution of commodities and services, while the latter processes are the most important factor in determining the present relations of human beings and social groups to one another. It is absurd, then, to argue that processes similar to those studied in laboratories and institutes of research are not a part of the daily life-experience of the young and hence do not come within the scope of education based upon experience. That the immature cannot study scientific facts and principles in the way in which mature experts study them goes without saying. But this fact, instead of exempting the educator from responsibility for using present experiences so that learners may gradually be led, through extraction of facts and laws, to experience of a scientific order, sets one of his main problems.

因为，如果说现有的经验之所以能够详细而广泛地存在，是因为科学首先应用于商品和服务的生产和分配过程，然后应用于人类维持的社会关系，那么，如果不通过教育引导学习者了解科学的最终构成要素，就不可能理解当前的社会力量（没有科学，就无法掌握和引导这些力量）。引导学习者熟悉科学主题这一原则的重要性，不会随着由此获得的对当前社会问题的洞察而终止。科学方法还指明了可以建立更好社会秩序的措施和政策。科学的应用在很大程度上产生了现在的社会条件，但其应用范围还远不止这些。因为迄今为止，科学的应用或多或少是随意的，而且受到诸如私人利益和权力等目的的影响，而这些目的都是前科学时代的制度的遗产。

For if it is true that existing experience in detail and also on a wide scale is what it is because of the application of science, first, to processes of production and distribution of goods and services, and then to the relations which human beings sustain socially to one another, it is impossible to obtain an understanding of present social forces (without which they cannot be mastered and directed) apart from an education which leads learners into knowledge of the very same facts and principles which in their final organization constitute the sciences. Nor does the importance of the principle that learners should be led to acquaintance with scientific subject-matter cease with the insight thereby given into present social issues. The methods of science also point the way to the measures and policies by means of which a better social order can be brought into existence. The applications of science which have produced in large measure the social conditions which now exist do not exhaust the possible field of their application. For so far science has been applied more or less casually and under the influence of ends, such as private advantage and power, which are a heritage from the institutions of a prescientific age.

我们几乎每天都会从许多渠道得知，人类不可能明智地指导他们的共同生活。一方面，我们被告知，人类关系（国内和国际）的复杂性，另一方面，人类在很大程度上是情感和习惯的产物，这使得通过智力进行大规模的社会规划和指导成为不可能。如果曾经进行过任何系统性的努力，从早期教育开始，通过年轻人的不断学习和学习，以期使以科学为代表的智力方法成为教育的至高无上的方法，那么这种观点将更加可信。习惯的固有性质中没有任何东西可以阻止智能方法本身成为习惯；情感的本质中也没有任何东西可以阻止对该方法产生强烈的情感忠诚。

We are told almost daily and from many sources that it is impossible for human beings to direct their common life intelligently. We are told, on one hand, that the complexity of human relations, domestic and international, and on the other hand, the fact that human beings are so largely creatures of emotion and habit, make impossible large-scale social planning and direction by intelligence. This view would be more credible if any systematic effort, beginning with early education and carried on through the continuous study and learning of the young, had ever been undertaken with a view to making the method of intelligence, exemplified in science, supreme in education. There is nothing in the inherent nature of habit that prevents intelligent method from becoming itself habitual; and there is nothing in the nature of emotion to prevent the development of intense emotional allegiance to the method.

这里以科学为例，说明对当前经验中存在的主题进行逐步选择以进行组织：这种组织是自由的，不是外部强加的，因为它与经验本身的增长相一致。学习者将当前生活经验中发现的主题用于科学，也许是对基本原则的最好说明，即利用现有经验作为手段，将学习者带入一个比教育增长所从经验中发现的更广阔、更精致、更有条理的环境世界，无论是物质世界还是人类世界。霍格本最近的著作《百万数学》表明，如果将数学视为文明的一面镜子，并将其视为文明进步的主要推动力，那么它就能像物理科学一样，为实现预期目标做出贡献。无论如何，其根本理想是知识的逐步组织。在知识组织方面，我们很可能发现非此即彼的哲学最为活跃。在实践中，即使没有用太多的言辞，人们通常认为，由于传统教育建立在一种几乎完全蔑视现实生活经验的知识组织概念之上，因此基于生活经验的教育应该蔑视事实和观念的组织。

The case of science is here employed as an illustration of progressive selection of subject-matter resident in present experience towards organization: an organization which is free, not externally imposed, because it is in accord with the growth of experience itself. The utilization of subject-matter found in the present life-experience of the learner towards science is perhaps the best illustration that can be found of the basic principle of using existing experience as the means of carrying learners on to a wider, more refined, and better organized environing world, physical and human, than is found in the experiences from which educative growth sets out. Hogben’s recent work, Mathematics for the Million, shows how mathematics, if it is treated as a mirror of civilization and as a main agency in its progress, can contribute to the desired goal as surely as can the physical sciences. The underlying ideal in any case is that of progressive organization of knowledge. It is with reference to organization of knowledge that we are likely to find Either-Or philosophies most acutely active. In practice, if not in so many words, it is often held that since traditional education rested upon a conception of organization of knowledge that was almost completely contemptuous of living present experience, therefore education based upon living experience should be contemptuous of the organization of facts and ideas.

刚才我称这种组织为理想，其负面含义是，教育者不能从已经组织好的知识开始，然后一点一点地灌输。但作为一种理想，组织事实和思想的积极过程是一个始终存在的教育过程。任何经验，如果不能既有助于了解更多事实，又有助于产生更多思想，并更好地、更有秩序地安排它们，就不具有教育意义。组织是一种与经验无关的原则，这种说法是不正确的。否则，经验就会如此分散，以至于混乱不堪。幼儿的经验以人和家庭为中心。精神病学家现在知道，家庭关系正常秩序的紊乱是日后精神和情绪问题的根源——这一事实证明了这种组织方式的现实性。早期学校教育（幼儿园和低年级）的一大进步是，它保留了经验组织的社会和人文中心，而不是过去那种剧烈的重心转移。但教育和音乐的一个突出问题是调节。就教育而言，调节意味着从社会和人类中心转向更客观的智力组织体系，但始终要牢记，智力组织本身并不是目的，而是理解和更明智地安排社会关系（特别是人类关系和纽带）的手段。

When a moment ago I called this organization an ideal, I meant, on the negative side, that the educator cannot start with knowledge already organized and proceed to ladle it out in doses. But as an ideal the active process of organizing facts and ideas is an ever-present educational process. No experience is educative that does not tend both to knowledge of more facts and entertaining of more ideas and to a better, a more orderly, arrangement of them. It is not true that organization is a principle foreign to experience. Otherwise experience would be so dispersive as to be chaotic. The experience of young children centers about persons and the home. Disturbance of the normal order of relationships in the family is now known by psychiatrists to be a fertile source of later mental and emotional troubles—a fact which testifies to the reality of this kind of organization. One of the great advances in early school education, in the kindergarten and early grades, is that it preserves the social and human center of the organization of experience, instead of the older violent shift of the center of gravity. But one of the outstanding problems of education, as of music, is modulation. In the case of education, modulation means movement from a social and human center toward a more objective intellectual scheme of organization, always bearing in mind, however, that intellectual organization is not an end in itself but is the means by which social relations, distinctively human ties and bonds, may be understood and more intelligently ordered.

当教育在理论和实践上以经验为基础时，成人和专家所组织的科目显然不能提供起点。然而，它代表了教育应不断努力的目标。几乎无需多言，科学知识组织的最基本原则之一是因果原则。科学专家掌握和表述这一原则的方式与年轻人的经验方式截然不同。但这种关系及其意义的理解甚至对幼儿的经验来说都并不陌生。当一个两三岁的孩子学会不要太靠近火焰，但又要靠近炉子取暖时，他就掌握并运用了因果关系。没有任何智力活动不符合因果关系的要求，而且，智力的程度不仅在于它符合因果关系，还在于它被有意识地铭记在心。

When education is based in theory and practice upon experience, it goes without saying that the organized subject-matter of the adult and the specialist cannot provide the starting point. Nevertheless, it represents the goal toward which education should continuously move. It is hardly necessary to say that one of the most fundamental principles of the scientific organization of knowledge is the principle of cause-and-effect. The way in which this principle is grasped and formulated by the scientific specialist is certainly very different from the way in which it can be approached in the experience of the young. But neither the relation nor grasp of its meaning is foreign to the experience of even the young child. When a child two or three years of age learns not to approach a flame too closely and yet to draw near enough a stove to get its warmth he is grasping and using the causal relation. There is no intelligent activity that does not conform to the requirements of the relation, and it is intelligent in the degree in which it is not only conformed to but consciously borne in mind.

在早期的经验形式中，因果关系不是抽象地呈现的，而是以所用手段与所达到目的的关系、手段与结果的关系的形式呈现的。判断力和理解力的增长本质上是形成目的以及选择和安排实现目的的手段的能力的增长。年轻人最基本的经验充满了手段-结果关系的例子。没有一顿饭或一个照明光源不体现这种关系。教育的问题不在于缺乏体现因果关系的手段与结果关系的情况。然而，未能利用这些情况引导学习者掌握给定经验案例中的关系却很常见。逻辑学家将选择和组织手段以达到目的的操作称为“分析和综合”。

In the earlier forms of experience the causal relation does not offer itself in the abstract but in the form of the relation of means employed to ends attained; of the relation of means and consequences. Growth in judgment and understanding is essentially growth in ability to form purposes and to select and arrange means for their realization. The most elementary experiences of the young are filled with cases of the means-consequence relation. There is not a meal cooked nor a source of illumination employed that does not exemplify this relation. The trouble with education is not the absence of situations in which the causal relation is exemplified in the relation of means and consequences. Failure to utilize the situations so as to lead the learner on to grasp the relation in the given cases of experience is, however, only too common. The logician gives the names “analysis and synthesis” to the operations by which means are selected and organized in relation to a purpose.

这一原则决定了学校活动利用的最终基础。在教育方面，再没有比一边呼吁学校开展各种积极的活动，一边又谴责需要逐步组织信息和思想更荒谬的事情了。有理智的活动与无目的的活动的区别在于，前者涉及从各种现有条件中选择手段（分析），并对这些手段进行安排（综合），以达到预期的目标或目的。学习者越不成熟，所着眼的目的就越简单，所采用的手段就越初级，这是显而易见的。但是，从某种程度上理解结果与手段的关系这一活动组织原则甚至适用于非常年幼的孩子。否则，一项活动就不再具有教育意义，因为它是盲目的。随着年龄的增长，手段相互关系的问题变得更加紧迫。随着理智观察从手段与目的的关系转移到手段相互关系这一更复杂的问题，因果关系的概念变得突出和明确。学校中商店、厨房等场所的最终合理性不仅在于它们提供了活动的机会，还在于它们提供了进行某种活动或获得机械技能的机会，这些活动或技能使学生关注手段和目的的关系，然后考虑事物相互作用以产生明确效果的方式。从原则上讲，这与科学研究中实验室的场地相同。

This principle determines the ultimate foundation for the utilization of activities in school. Nothing can be more absurd educationally than to make a plea for a variety of active occupations in the school while decrying the need for progressive organization of information and ideas. Intelligent activity is distinguished from aimless activity by the fact that it involves selection of means—analysis—out of the variety of conditions that are present, and their arrangement—synthesis—to reach an intended aim or purpose. That the more immature the learner is, the simpler must be the ends held in view and the more rudimentary the means employed, is obvious. But the principle of organization of activity in terms of some perception of the relation of consequences to means applies even with the very young. Otherwise an activity ceases to be educative because it is blind. With increased maturity, the problem of interrelation of means becomes more urgent. In the degree in which intelligent observation is transferred from the relation of means to ends to the more complex question of the relation of means to one another, the idea of cause and effect becomes prominent and explicit. The final justification of shops, kitchens, and so on in the school is not just that they afford opportunity for activity, but that they provide opportunity for the kind of activity or for the acquisition of mechanical skills which leads students to attend to the relation of means and ends, and then to consideration of the way things interact with one another to produce definite effects. It is the same in principle as the ground for laboratories in scientific research.

除非能够根据经验解决智力组织问题，否则肯定会对外部强加的组织方法产生反应。这种反应的迹象已经很明显。我们被告知，我们的新旧学校在主要任务上都失败了。据说，它们没有培养批判性辨别能力和推理能力。我们被告知，由于积累了各种未消化的信息，并且试图获得在商业和商业世界中立即有用的技能，思考能力被扼杀了。我们被告知，这些弊端源于科学的影响，源于以牺牲过去经过检验的文化遗产为代价来扩大当前的需求。有人认为，科学及其方法必须服从；我们必须回到亚里士多德和圣托马斯逻辑中表达的终极第一原理的逻辑，以便年轻人能够在智力和道德生活中获得可靠的支撑，而不是任由吹过的微风摆布。

Unless the problem of intellectual organization can be worked out on the ground of experience, reaction is sure to occur toward externally imposed methods of organization. There are signs of this reaction already in evidence. We are told that our schools, old and new, are failing in the main task. They do not develop, it is said, the capacity for critical discrimination and the ability to reason. The ability to think is smothered, we are told, by accumulation of miscellaneous ill-digested information, and by the attempt to acquire forms of skill which will be immediately useful in the business and commercial world. We are told that these evils spring from the influence of science and from the magnification of present requirements at the expense of the tested cultural heritage from the past. It is argued that science and its method must be subordinated; that we must return to the logic of ultimate first principles expressed in the logic of Aristotle and St. Thomas, in order that the young may have sure anchorage in their intellectual and moral life, and not be at the mercy of every passing breeze that blows.

如果科学方法在学校所有科目的日常工作中始终如一地、持续不断地应用，这种情感诉求会给我留下比现在更深刻的印象。我认为，教育若要避免漫无目的地漂泊，就必须在两种选择之间做出选择。其中一种是试图引导教育者回归科学方法发展之前几个世纪的智力方法和理想。在普遍存在情感、智力和经济不安全感的时期，这种诉求可能会暂时奏效。因为在这种情况下，依赖固定权威的愿望很强烈。然而，它与现代生活的所有条件如此脱节，我认为朝这个方向寻求救赎是愚蠢的。另一种选择是系统地利用科学方法作为明智探索和利用经验固有潜力的模式和理想。

If the method of science had ever been consistently and continuously applied throughout the day-by-day work of the school in all subjects, I should be more impressed by this emotional appeal than I am. I see at bottom but two alternatives between which education must choose if it is not to drift aimlessly. One of them is expressed by the attempt to induce educators to return to the intellectual methods and ideals that arose centuries before scientific method was developed. The appeal may be temporarily successful in a period when general insecurity, emotional and intellectual as well as economic, is rife. For under these conditions the desire to lean on fixed authority is active. Nevertheless, it is so out of touch with all the conditions of modern life that I believe it is folly to seek salvation in this direction. The other alternative is systematic utilization of scientific method as the pattern and ideal of intelligent exploration and exploitation of the potentialities inherent in experience.

所涉及的问题对进步学校来说具有特别强烈的影响。不经常关注经验的知识内容的发展，不不断获得事实和思想的组织，最终可能只会加强回归知识和道德权威主义的反动倾向。现在不是讨论科学方法的时候和地方。但它的某些特征与任何基于经验的教育方案密切相关，因此应该注意。

The problem involved comes home with peculiar force to progressive schools. Failure to give constant attention to development of the intellectual content of experiences and to obtain ever-increasing organization of facts and ideas may in the end merely strengthen the tendency toward a reactionary return to intellectual and moral authoritarianism. The present is not the time nor place for a disquisition upon scientific method. But certain features of it are so closely connected with any educational scheme based upon experience that they should be noted.

首先，与其他方法相比，科学的实验方法更重视思想本身，而不是更少重视思想本身。除非行动受到某种主导思想的指导，否则科学意义上的实验就不存在。所采用的思想是假设，而不是最终真理，这就是为什么在科学中，思想比其他任何地方都受到更严格的保护和检验。一旦它们被视为第一真理，就不再有任何理由对它们进行严格审查。作为固定的真理，它们必须被接受，这就是事情的结局。但作为假设，它们必须不断地被检验和修订，这一要求要求它们被准确地表述出来。

In the first place, the experimental method of science attaches more importance, not less, to ideas as ideas than do other methods. There is no such thing as experiment in the scientific sense unless action is directed by some leading idea. The fact that the ideas employed are hypotheses, not final truths, is the reason why ideas are more jealously guarded and tested in science than anywhere else. The moment they are taken to be first truths in themselves there ceases to be any reason for scrupulous examination of them. As fixed truths they must be accepted and that is the end of the matter. But as hypotheses, they must be continuously tested and revised, a requirement that demands they be accurately formulated.

其次，思想或假设是通过付诸行动时产生的后果来检验的。这一事实意味着必须仔细、有辨别地观察行动的后果。不通过观察其后果来检验的活动可能会暂时令人愉悦。但从理智上讲，它毫无意义。它既不提供有关行动发生情况的知识，也不会导致思想的澄清和扩展。

In the second place, ideas or hypotheses are tested by the consequences which they produce when they are acted upon. This fact means that the consequences of action must be carefully and discriminatingly observed. Activity that is not checked by observation of what follows from it may be temporarily enjoyed. But intellectually it leads nowhere. It does not provide knowledge about the situations in which action occurs nor does it lead to clarification and expansion of ideas.

第三，实验方法所体现的智慧方法要求记录想法、活动和观察到的后果。记录是一种反思性回顾和总结，其中既有对发展中的经验的重要特征的辨别，也有对重要特征的记录。反思就是回顾已经做过的事情，以便提取净意义，这些意义是明智地处理进一步经验的资本。它是智力组织和训练有素的头脑的核心。

In the third place, the method of intelligence manifested in the experimental method demands keeping track of ideas, activities, and observed consequences. Keeping track is a matter of reflective review and summarizing, in which there is both discrimination and record of the significant features of a developing experience. To reflect is to look back over what has been done so as to extract the net meanings which are the capital stock for intelligent dealing with further experiences. It is the heart of intellectual organization and of the disciplined mind.

我不得不用一般的、常常是抽象的语言来讲话。但是，我所说的内容与这样的要求有机地联系在一起：经验要想具有教育意义，就必须引导学生进入一个不断扩展的主题世界，一个事实或信息和思想的主题世界。只有当教育者将教学和学习视为一个不断重建经验的过程时，这一条件才能得到满足。反过来，只有当教育者有长远的眼光，将每一次当前的经验视为影响未来经验的推动力时，这一条件才能得到满足。我知道，我强调科学方法可能会产生误导，因为它可能只会导致人们想起由专家进行的实验室研究的特殊技术。但强调科学方法的意义与专门技术几乎没有关系。这意味着科学方法是我们掌握的了解我们生活的世界中日常经验意义的唯一真实手段。这意味着科学方法提供了一种工作模式，即经验如何以及在何种条件下被用于不断向前和向外引导。让教学方法适应不同成熟程度的个体是教育者面临的一个问题，而问题中不变的因素是形成观念、根据观念采取行动、观察由此产生的条件以及整理事实和观念以备将来使用。六岁儿童的观念、活动、观察和组织与十二岁或十八岁的儿童不同，更不用说成年科学家了。但如果经验具有教育意义，那么在每一个层面上，经验都会不断发展。因此，无论经验水平如何，我们别无选择，要么按照它提供的模式行事，要么忽视智力在发展和控制生动活泼的经验中的作用。

I have been forced to speak in general and often abstract language. But what has been said is organically connected with the requirement that experiences in order to be educative must lead out into an expanding world of subject-matter, a subject-matter of facts or information and of ideas. This condition is satisfied only as the educator views teaching and learning as a continuous process of reconstruction of experience. This condition in turn can be satisfied only as the educator has a long look ahead, and views every present experience as a moving force in influencing what future experiences will be. I am aware that the emphasis I have placed upon scientific method may be misleading, for it may result only in calling up the special technique of laboratory research as that is conducted by specialists. But the meaning of the emphasis placed upon scientific method has little to do with specialized techniques. It means that scientific method is the only authentic means at our command for getting at the significance of our everyday experiences of the world in which we live. It means that scientific method provides a working pattern of the way in which and the conditions under which experiences are used to lead ever onward and outward. Adaptation of the method to individuals of various degrees of maturity is a problem for the educator, and the constant factors in the problem are the formation of ideas, acting upon ideas, observation of the conditions which result, and organization of facts and ideas for future use. Neither the ideas, nor the activities, nor the observations, nor the organization are the same for a person six years old as they are for one twelve or eighteen years old, to say nothing of the adult scientist. But at every level there is an expanding development of experience if experience is educative in effect. Consequently, whatever the level of experience, we have no choice but either to operate in accord with the pattern it provides or else to neglect the place of intelligence in the development and control of a living and moving experience.

第八章

Chapter 8

体验——教育的手段和目的

Experience—The Means and Goal of Education

在我所说的内容中，我理所当然地认为，教育要想实现其对个体学习者和社会的目标，必须以经验为基础——而经验总是某个人的实际生活经验。我并没有主张接受这一原则，也没有试图为其辩护。教育界的保守派和激进派都对目前的教育状况深感不满。至少在这两个教育思想流派的聪明人中，有这么多的共识。教育体系必须朝着一个方向发展，要么倒退到前科学时代的智力和道德标准，要么前进到更多地利用科学方法来发展不断增长、不断扩大的经验的可能性。我只是试图指出，如果教育采取后一种方式，必须令人满意地满足一些条件。

IN WHAT I HAVE SAID I have taken for granted the soundness of the principle that education in order to accomplish its ends both for the individual learner and for society must be based upon experience—which is always the actual life-experience of some individual. I have not argued for the acceptance of this principle nor attempted to justify it. Conservatives as well as radicals in education are profoundly discontented with the present educational situation taken as a whole. There is at least this much agreement among intelligent persons of both schools of educational thought. The educational system must move one way or another, either backward to the intellectual and moral standards of a pre-scientific age or forward to ever greater utilization of scientific method in the development of the possibilities of growing, expanding experience. I have but endeavored to point out some of the conditions which must be satisfactorily fulfilled if education takes the latter course.

因为我对教育的潜力非常有信心，当教育被看作是智力指导下发展日常经验中固有的可能性时，我认为没有必要在这里批评另一条道路，也没有必要提出支持走经验道路的论据。我认为，走这条路唯一会失败的理由在于，经验和实验方法将无法得到充分的理解。世界上没有哪门学科比经验学科更严格，它要经受智力发展和指导的考验。因此，我认为，即使是暂时反对新教育的标准、目标和方法，唯一的理由就是那些自称采用这些标准的教育者在实践中没有忠实于这些标准、目标和方法。正如我多次强调的那样，新教育的道路并不比旧道路更容易走，而是更加艰难。在它达到大多数人之前，它将一直如此，而要达到这一目标，需要其追随者多年的认真合作。我认为，它的未来面临的最大危险是，人们认为它是一条容易走的路，很容易走，以至于它的进程可以即兴发挥，即使不是即兴的，至少也是几乎每天或每周。正是出于这个原因，我没有赞扬它的原则，而是只展示了如果它要获得理所当然的成功，就必须满足的某些条件。

For I am so confident of the potentialities of education when it is treated as intelligently directed development of the possibilities inherent in ordinary experience that I do not feel it necessary to criticize here the other route nor to advance arguments in favor of taking the route of experience. The only ground for anticipating failure in taking this path resides to my mind in the danger that experience and the experimental method will not be adequately conceived. There is no discipline in the world so severe as the discipline of experience subjected to the tests of intelligent development and direction. Hence the only ground I can see for even a temporary reaction against the standards, aims, and methods of the newer education is the failure of educators who professedly adopt them to be faithful to them in practice. As I have emphasized more than once, the road of the new education is not an easier one to follow than the old road but a more strenuous and difficult one. It will remain so until it has attained its majority and that attainment will require many years of serious co-operative work on the part of its adherents. The greatest danger that attends its future is, I believe, the idea that it is an easy way to follow, so easy that its course may be improvised, if not in an impromptu fashion, at least almost from day to day or from week to week. It is for this reason that instead of extolling its principles, I have confined myself to showing certain conditions which must be fulfilled if it is to have the successful career which by right belongs to it.

在前面的论述中，我经常使用“进步”和“新”教育这两个词。然而，在结束之前，我还要表明我的坚定信念：根本问题不是新教育与旧教育，也不是进步教育与传统教育之间的对立，而是一个问题，即任何东西必须是什么，才能配得上教育这个名称。我希望并相信，我不会仅仅因为进步这个名称可以适用于任何目的或方法，就支持它们。基本问题涉及教育的性质，没有前缀任何限定的形容词。我们想要和需要的是纯粹而简单的教育，当我们致力于找出教育到底是什么，以及必须满足哪些条件才能使教育成为现实，而不是一个名称或口号时，我们将取得更确定、更快的进步。正是出于这个原因，我才强调需要一种健全的经验哲学。

I have used frequently in what precedes the words “progressive” and “new” education. I do not wish to close, however, without recording my firm belief that the fundamental issue is not of new versus old education nor of progressive against traditional education but a question of what anything whatever must be to be worthy of the name education. I am not, I hope and believe, in favor of any ends or any methods simply because the name progressive may be applied to them. The basic question concerns the nature of education with no qualifying adjectives prefixed. What we want and need is education pure and simple, and we shall make surer and faster progress when we devote ourselves to finding out just what education is and what conditions have to be satisfied in order that education may be a reality and not a name or a slogan. It is for this reason alone that I have emphasized the need for a sound philosophy of experience.
