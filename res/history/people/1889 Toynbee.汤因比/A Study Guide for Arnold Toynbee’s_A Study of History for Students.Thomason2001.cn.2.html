历史研究

A Study of History

阿诺德·J·汤因比

Arnold J. Toynbee

1934–1961

1934–1961

介绍

Introduction

阿诺德·汤因比的多卷本著作《历史研究》是二十世纪出版的主要历史学术著作之一。第一卷于1934年在伦敦出版，之后各卷陆续出版，直至第十二卷也是最后一卷于1961年在伦敦出版。D.C.萨默维尔在汤因比的合作下，编纂了第一至十卷的两卷本节选本，并分别于1947年（第一卷）和1957年（第二卷）在伦敦出版。

Arnold Toynbee's multi-volume A Study of History is one of the major works of historical scholarship published in the twentieth century. The first volume was published in London in 1934, and subsequent volumes appeared periodically until the twelfth and final volume was published in London in 1961. A two-volume abridgement of volumes 1-10 was prepared by D. C. Somervell with Toynbee's cooperation and published in 1947 (volume one) and 1957 (volume two) in London.

《世界史》原著篇幅浩大，前十卷共计六千余页，三百万余字。索默维尔的节略本仅收录原著约六分之一的内容，也已有九百余页。这部著作的规模与其宏大的目标相符，汤因比旨在分析人类历史上所有已知文明的起源、发展和衰落。在汤因比的分析中，这包括五个现存文明、十六个已消亡文明，以及他所定义的几个停滞不前的文明。

A Study of History in its original form is a huge work. The first ten volumes contain over six thousand pages and more than three million words. Somervell's abridgement, containing only about one-sixth of the original, runs to over nine hundred pages. The size of the work is in proportion to the grandeur of Toynbee's purpose, which is to analyze the genesis, growth, and fall of every human civilization ever known. In Toynbee's analysis, this amounts to five living civilizations and sixteen extinct ones, as well as several that Toynbee defines as arrested civilizations.

汤因比在文明的兴衰中发现了一种反复出现的模式，他在《历史研究》中分析的正是这种模式背后的历史规律。

Toynbee detects in the rise and fall of civilizations a recurring pattern, and it is the laws of history behind this pattern that he analyzes in A Study of History.

从一开始，《历史研究》就是一部充满争议的作品。它在普通大众中广受欢迎，尤其是在美国。二战后，汤因比被誉为时代的先知。另一方面，学院派历史学家对他的著作持怀疑态度，许多人认为他的方法不科学，结论不可靠，甚至完全错误。然而，尽管存在这些批评，《历史研究》依然是一部发人深省的作品，它探讨了人类的过去、原因以及未来走向。

From the outset, A Study of History was a controversial work. It won wide readership amongst the general public, especially in the United States, and after World War II Toynbee was hailed as a prophet of his times. On the other hand, his work was viewed with skepticism by academic historians, many of whom argued that his methods were unscientific and his conclusions unreliable or simply untrue. Despite these criticisms, however, A Study of History endures as a provocative vision of where humanity has been, and why, and where it may be headed.

作者简介

Author Biography

阿诺德·J·汤因比于1889年4月14日出生于伦敦，父亲哈里·V·汤因比是一位社会工作者，母亲莎拉·伊迪丝·马歇尔·汤因比是一位历史学家。汤因比自幼展现出学术天赋，1902年至1907年间获得奖学金就读于温彻斯特公学，之后进入牛津大学贝利奥尔学院学习古典学，并于1911年毕业。同年，汤因比前往雅典的英国考古学院深造，进一步研究古希腊历史。1912年，他成为贝利奥尔学院的研究员和导师，并担任此职三年。由于健康原因无法服兵役，第一次世界大战期间，他曾在英国陆军部政治情报部门工作，并于1919年作为英国代表团成员出席了巴黎和会。同年，他还担任伦敦大学科拉伊斯拜占庭和现代希腊研究教席教授。

Arnold J. Toynbee was born in London on April 14, 1889, the son of Harry V. Toynbee, a social worker, and Sarah Edith Marshall Toynbee, a historian. Showing academic promise at a young age, Toynbee won scholarships to attend Winchester School from 1902 to 1907, and then Balliol College, Oxford, where he studied Classics and graduated in 1911. In the same year, Toynbee pursued his interest in ancient Greek history by studying at the British Archeological School in Athens. In 1912, he became a fellow and tutor at Balliol College, a position he held for three years. Unable to perform military service because of his health, during World War I he worked in the Political Intelligence Department of the War Office and was a member of the British delegation at the Paris Peace Conference in 1919. He also held the Koraes Chair of Byzantine and Modern Greek Studies at London University in 1919.

1925年，汤因比开始担任皇家国际事务研究所研究主任和伦敦大学国际史教授，任期长达三十年。他著作颇丰，在1921年至1934年间发表了140多篇文章和书籍，其中包括《希腊和土耳其的西方问题》（1922年）、《希腊历史思想》（1924年）、《希腊文明与性格》（1924年）、年度《国际事务调查》（1923-1927年）以及《中国之旅》（1931年）。他还在撰写《历史研究》，这部著作使他名声大噪。该书探讨了文明的兴衰，前三卷于1934年出版，第四至六卷于1939年出版。

In 1925, Toynbee began a thirty-year tenure as director of studies at the Royal Institute of International Affairs and professor of international history at London University. He was a prolific author, writing more than 140 articles and books between 1921 and 1934, including The Western Question in Greece and Turkey (1922), Greek Historical Thought (1924), Greek Civilisation and Character (1924), the annual Survey of International Affairs (1923-1927), and A Journey to China (1931). He was also at work on A Study of History, for which he is best known. The first three volumes of this investigation into the rise and fall of civilizations were published in 1934; volumes 4-6 followed in 1939.

1943年至1946年，汤因比担任英国外交部研究司司长。他还作为英国代表出席了第二次巴黎和会。1954年，《历史研究》第七至十卷出版。汤因比还参与了由D.C.萨默维尔编写的节略本，该书分两卷出版（分别于1947年和1957年出版）。

From 1943 until 1946, Toynbee directed the Research Department at the Foreign Office. He also attended the second Paris Peace Conference as a British delegate. In 1954, volumes 7-10 of A Study of History were published. An abridged version, prepared by D. C. Somervell with Toynbee's cooperation, appeared in two volumes (1947 and 1957).

汤因比的巨著使他成为当时最著名的历史学家之一，但也引发了争议。最后一卷，即第十二卷《重新思考》（1961年），是他试图回应众多批评者的尝试。

Toynbee's massive work made him one of the best-known historians of his time although it also proved controversial. The final, twelfth volume, Reconsiderations (1961), was an attempt to answer his many critics.

完成《历史研究》之后，汤因比继续保持着高产的写作速度。从1956年到1973年，他撰写了十六本书。其中包括《历史学家的宗教观》（1956年），他在书中倡导回归精神价值观；《变革与习惯：我们时代的挑战》（1966年），他在书中提出中国可能会成为世界事务中的统一力量；以及自传体作品《经验》（1969年）。

After finishing A Study of History, Toynbee continued to publish at a prolific rate. Between 1956 and 1973, he wrote sixteen books. These included An Historian's Approach to Religion (1956), in which he advocated a return to spiritual values, Change and Habit: The Challenge of Our Time (1966), in which he suggested that China might emerge as a unifying influence in world affairs, and the autobiographical Experiences (1969).

汤因比于1912年与罗莎琳德·默里结婚，育有两个孩子。这段婚姻于1945年以离婚告终。1946年，汤因比与研究员兼作家维罗妮卡·玛乔丽·博尔特结婚。他们合作撰写了《国际事务概览》。

Toynbee married Rosalind Murray in 1912, and they had two children. The marriage ended in divorce in 1945. In 1946, Toynbee married Veronica Marjorie Boulter, a research associate and writer. They collaborated in writing the Survey of International Affairs.

汤因比于 1975 年 10 月 22 日在英国约克去世。

Toynbee died in York, England, on October 22, 1975.

概括

Summary

第一章：历史研究单元

Chapter 1: The Unit of Historical Study

在《历史研究》一书中，汤因比首先明确了历史学家研究的对象。这个对象并非单个国家，而是一个完整的文明。他列举了五大现存文明：西方基督教文明、东正教文明、伊斯兰文明、印度教文明和远东文明。此外，他还指出了十六个已消亡的文明，这些文明孕育了其他现存文明。汤因比随后区分了原始社会（数量众多）和文明（数量相对较少）。他驳斥了现存只有一个文明——西方文明——的观点，也否定了所有文明都起源于埃及的说法。

In A Study of History, Toynbee first identifies the unit that should be the object of the historian's study. This unit is not an individual nation but an entire civilization. Toynbee identifies five living civilizations: Western Christian, Orthodox Christian, Islamic, Hindu, and Far Eastern. In addition there are sixteen extinct civilizations from which living civilizations developed. Toynbee then makes a distinction between primitive societies, of which there are many, and civilizations, which are comparatively few. He dismisses the idea that there is now only one civilization, the West, and also the notion that all civilization originated in Egypt.

第二章：文明的起源

Chapter 2: Geneses of Civilizations

文明是如何从原始社会中产生的？汤因比认为，答案不在于种族；舒适的环境也并非文明起源的关键。恰恰相反，文明源于对困境的创造性应对。正是困境而非安逸，才是文明发展的动力。汤因比指出了五项有助于文明发展的挑战：艰苦的环境；全新的环境；一次或多次打击，例如军事失败；压力，例如边境社会遭受频繁的攻击；以及惩罚，例如奴隶制或其他阶级或种族对其他阶级或种族的压迫。然而，有些挑战过于严峻，最终无法促成文明的诞生。

How do civilizations emerge from primitive societies? For Toynbee, the answer does not lie in race; nor does an easy environment provide a key to the origins of civilization. On the contrary, civilizations arise out of creative responses to difficult situations. It is difficulty, rather than ease, that proves the stimulus. Toynbee identifies five challenges that aid the process: a hard environment; a new environment; one or more "blows," such as a military defeat; pressures, such as a frontier society subjected to frequent attack; and penalizations, such as slavery or other measures in which one class or race is oppressed by another. Some challenges, however, prove to be too severe and do not result in a civilization's growth.

第三章：文明的兴起

Chapter 3: Growths of Civilizations

在探究某些文明（如波利尼西亚文明、爱斯基摩文明）为何停止发展之后，汤因比讨论了如何衡量一个社会的成长。他得出结论：军事或政治扩张，以及农业或工业技术的进步，都不是可靠的衡量标准。这些都是外在指标，而真正重要的是“精神化”。在这个过程中，社会的能量不再局限于已经克服的外部物质障碍，而是转向应对来自内部、需要内在或精神层面回应的挑战。成长源于那些具有创造力的个体，他们展现出一种时而远离社会、时而回归社会的模式。

After examining why some civilizations (Polynesian, Eskimo) cease to develop, Toynbee discusses how the growth of a society is to be measured. He concludes that neither military nor political expansion, nor advances in agricultural or industrial techniques, are reliable criteria. These are external indicators, whereas what is important is "etherialization." In this process, the energies of a society are directed away from external material obstacles, which have been overcome, towards challenges that arise from within and require an inner or spiritual response. Growth happens because of creative individuals who exhibit a pattern of withdrawal from and return to society.

第四章：文明的崩溃

Chapter 4: Breakdowns of Civilizations

汤因比认为，文明的崩溃并非源于某种不可避免的宇宙法则，也不是由于对自然环境或人文环境失去控制、技术衰退或军事侵略所致。崩溃发生在少数富有创造力的群体失去其创造力，而多数群体不再追随他们，或仅仅迫于无奈才追随他们之时。这导致社会团结的丧失和不满的“无产阶级”的出现。少数富有创造力的群体之所以失去力量，是因为他们习惯于在成功之后“安于现状”，沉湎于过去。因此，他们无法成功应对下一个挑战。

The breakdown of a civilization, Toynbee holds, is not due to some inevitable cosmic law. Nor is it caused by loss of control over the physical or human environment, a decline in technology, or military aggression. A breakdown happens when the creative minority loses its creative power and the majority no longer follows it, or follows it only because it is compelled to do so. This results in a loss of social unity and the emergence of a disaffected "proletariat." Creative minorities lose their power because they have a habit of "resting on their oars" following their success and becoming infatuated with the past. Therefore they fail to meet the next challenge successfully.

第五章：文明的瓦解

Chapter 5: Disintegration of Civilizations

当一个文明瓦解时，它会分裂成三个派别：一个是“统治少数群体”，它是曾经富有创造力的少数群体退化的阶段；一个是“内部无产阶级”，它是文明内部不再效忠于统治少数群体并可能反抗它的广大民众；还有一个是“外部无产阶级”，它存在于文明边界之外，并抵制被纳入其中。内部无产阶级可能会对统治少数群体进行暴力反抗，但之后可能会出现更为和平的反抗，最终发展成为一种“更高层次的宗教”。

When a civilization disintegrates, it splits into three factions: a "dominant minority," which is a degenerate stage of a formerly creative minority; an "internal proletariat," which is a mass of people within the civilization who no longer have any allegiance to the dominant minority and may rebel against it; and an "external proletariat" that exists beyond the frontiers of the civilization and resists being incorporated into it. An internal proletariat may react violently against the dominant minority, but later there may be a more peaceful reaction, culminating in the discovery of a "higher religion."

在一个正在瓦解的文明中，社会变革伴随着行为、信仰和生活方式的改变。人们要么感到“漂泊”，认为世界由偶然主宰；要么感到罪恶，这两种情绪都是为了弥补已经丧失的创造力。此外，还可能出现“复古主义”，渴望回归过去；或者“未来主义”，一种推翻旧制度的革命模式。文明的瓦解遵循着“溃败”与“复兴”交替的节奏。在这个过程中，各种富有创造力的人物将以不同类型的“救世主”形象出现。

Social changes in a disintegrating civilization are accompanied by changes in behavior, beliefs, and ways of life. There is either a sense of "drift," in which people believe the world is ruled by chance, or a sense of sin, both of which are substitutes for the creative energy that has been lost. There may also be "archaism," a desire to return to the past, or "futurism," a revolutionary mode in which old institutions are scrapped. Disintegration of a civilization proceeds in a rhythm of "routs" followed by "rallies." During this process, creative personalities will emerge as different kinds of "saviours."

第六章：普遍国家

Chapter 6: Universal States

在文明瓦解的“复兴”阶段，普世国家应运而生；它紧随“动荡时期”之后，带来政治统一。然而，它本身也是瓦解进程的一部分。尽管普世国家无法自救，但它们确实为其他机构，例如其内部无产阶级的高级宗教，带来了意想不到的好处。普世国家促进了不同地理区域和社会阶层之间的高度“沟通”。它们通常以宽容的统治方式进行管理，这有助于高级宗教的传播。普世国家建立的许多机构，例如通讯、法律体系、度量衡、货币和公务员制度，都被其最初设计目标群体之外的其他群体所利用。

A universal state appears as part of the "rally" stage in the disintegration of a civilization; it follows a "Time of Troubles" and brings political unity. However, it is still part of the process of disintegration. Although universal states fail to save themselves, they do offer unintended advantages to other institutions, such as the higher religions of their internal proletariats. Universal states provide high "conductivity" between different geographic areas and between social classes. They are often ruled with tolerance, which helps to facilitate the spread of higher religions. Many of the institutions established by a universal state, such as communications, legal systems, weights and measures, money, and civil services, are made use of by communities other than those for which they were designed.

第七章：普世教会

Chapter 7: Universal Churches

汤因比分析了教会与文明之间的关系。他驳斥了教会如同社会毒瘤，最终导致普世国家衰落的观点。但他指出，认为教会如同蛹，维系着文明从一种形态演化到另一种形态的进程，也并非全部真相。汤因比认为，宗教并非文明的副产品，文明的全部意义就在于为某种高等宗教（基督教、印度教、佛教、伊斯兰教）的兴起提供契机。

Toynbee analyzes the relationship between churches and civilizations. He repudiates the idea that a church is like a social cancer that leads to the decline of the universal state. But the idea that churches act as chrysalises, keeping civilization alive as it evolves from one manifestation into the next, is not the entire truth either, he writes. Toynbee argues that, rather than religion being a by-product of civilization, the whole purpose of a civilization is to provide an opportunity for one of the higher religions (Christianity, Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam) to emerge.

第八章：英雄时代

Chapter 8: Heroic Ages

当一个日渐衰落的文明被曾经与之共存的蛮族摧毁时，便会迎来英雄时代。这些蛮族虽然会用史诗般的诗歌来纪念他们的胜利，但他们的文明程度不足以让他们从被摧毁的文明的遗产中获益。英雄时代是连接一个文明消亡和另一个文明诞生的过渡时期，它最终会走向黑暗时代，而文明也会在黑暗时代中重新崛起。

When a disintegrating civilization is destroyed by barbarians who have previously co-existed alongside it, the result is a Heroic Age. The barbarians are not sufficiently advanced to benefit from the legacy of the civilization they have destroyed although they may commemorate their victories in epic poetry. A Heroic Age is an interlude between the death of one civilization and the birth of another. It leads to a Dark Age, out of which civilization reemerges.

第九章：太空文明间的接触

Chapter 9: Contacts between Civilizations in Space

当不同文明相互接触时，往往会发生冲突，并带来灾难性的后果。遭受攻击的文明可能会采取军事手段进行反击；也可能进行意识形态宣传（例如苏联对西方所做的那样）；或者大力发展自身的宗教；甚至创立一种更高的宗教。胜利的文明有时会走向军事化，最终为此付出惨痛的代价。此外，它还要承受异族文化渗透到自身社会生活的代价，这可能会带来不利的后果。征服其他文明的文明可能会犯下将征服者视为“异教徒”、“野蛮人”或“土著”的错误。

When diverse civilizations come into contact with each other, there is usually conflict, with disastrous results. A civilization under assault may fight back by military means; with ideological propaganda (as the Soviet Union did against the West); by intensely cultivating its own religion; or by creating a higher religion. A victorious civilization will sometimes become militarized, to its own ultimate cost. It also pays the price of having the culture of the alien civilization seep into its own social life, which may have adverse consequences. A civilization that subjugates another may make the mistake of regarding the conquered people as "heathens," "barbarians," or "natives."

第十章：不同文明之间的接触

Chapter 10: Contacts between Civilizations in Time

汤因比考察了历史上众多的文艺复兴时期，在这些时期，一个文明从过去的文明中汲取了新的灵感。最著名的例子是已消亡的希腊文明在中世纪晚期对西方基督教世界的影响（意大利文艺复兴）。

Toynbee surveys the many renaissances in history in which one civilization has drawn new inspiration from a civilization of the past. The best-known example is the influence of the dead Hellenic civilization on Western Christendom in the late medieval period (the Italian Renaissance).

第十一章：历史上的法律与自由

Chapter 11: Law and Freedom in History

历史的展开是遵循自然规律，还是随机发生的？汤因比支持前者。他列举了商业和经济等人类事务中的例子，表明存在可预测的规律。然后，他指出近代西方历史上战争与和平的循环模式，并指出文明的瓦解也遵循类似的溃败与复兴的模式。人类可以利用这些自然规律为己所用，但其利用程度取决于自身的心理以及与上帝的关系——上帝代表着高于自然规律的更高法则。

Does history unfold according to laws of nature or is the process random? Toynbee argues for the former. He cites examples from human affairs such as business and economics that show the operation of predictable laws. Then he points to a cyclic pattern of war and peace in modern Western history and notes that disintegrating civilizations follow a similar pattern of rout-and-rally. Humankind can harness these laws of nature to its benefit although the extent to which it is able to do so depends on its own psychology and on its relationship with God, who represents a higher law than the law of nature.

第十二章：西方文明的前景

Chapter 12: Prospects of Western Civilization

汤因比认为，对民族国家的崇拜和军国主义是现代西方文明的负面方面，但它在促进民主和教育方面也取得了积极成果。汤因比探讨了世界和平、世界政府的前景以及现代科技带来的问题。一个社会为摆脱匮乏所付出的代价是日益严格的管制，这侵蚀了个人自由。汤因比寄希望于一个机械化的社会，在这个社会里，人们拥有更多闲暇时间，从而有更多精力投入到精神领域。

Worship of the nation-state and militarism are negative aspects of modern Western civilization, Toynbee holds, but it has also achieved positive results in promoting democracy and education. Toynbee discusses prospects for world peace, world government, and issues arising from modern technology. The price a society pays for freedom from want is increasing regimentation, which encroaches on personal freedom. Toynbee holds out the hope that in a mechanized society in which there is more leisure, people will have more energy to devote to spiritual matters.

关键数据

Key Figures

安第斯山脉

Andean

秘鲁的安第斯文明兴起于公元纪元之初的安第斯山脉沿岸和高原地区。它面临的挑战是高原上几乎寸草不生的恶劣气候。安第斯文明的“动荡时期”何时开始已不可考，但一直持续到公元1430年，之后是印加帝国的鼎盛时期。此后，安第斯文明经历了一段和平时期，直到1533年西班牙人的到来摧毁了印加帝国。

The Andean civilization in Peru emerged on the Andean coast and plateau around the beginning of the Christian era. The challenge it had to overcome was a bleak climate on an almost soil-less plateau. It is not known when its Time of Troubles began, but it lasted until 1430 A.D. and was followed by the universal state of the Inca Empire. A time of peace followed until 1533, when the empire was destroyed by the arrival of the Spanish.

阿拉伯

Arabic

阿拉伯文明起源于叙利亚文明，主要在叙利亚和埃及繁荣发展。它与伊朗文明相似；主要区别在于，伊朗文明的主要信仰是伊斯兰教什叶派，而阿拉伯文明则以逊尼派为主。

The Arabic civilization developed out of the Syriac civilization and flourished mainly in Syria and Egypt. It was similar to the Iranic civilization; the main difference was that in the Iranic civilization, the predominant faith was Islamic Shi'ism, whereas in the Arabic civilization, Sunnism predominated.

停滞的文明

Arrested Civilizations

停滞文明指的是那些虽然存活下来，却未能发展壮大的文明。它们兴起于对异常严峻的自然或人为挑战的回应，这种挑战的严峻程度介于促进文明进一步发展和导致文明衰落的临界点上。它们曾尝试并取得了辉煌的成就，但却无法继续发展。例如，波利尼西亚人、爱斯基摩人和游牧民族，它们因应对自然挑战而停滞不前；而希腊世界的斯巴达人和东正教世界的奥斯曼人，则因应对人为挑战而停滞不前。

Arrested civilizations are those that have stayed alive but have failed to grow. They arose in response to a physical or human challenge of unusual severity, on the borderline between the degree that gives stimulus to greater development and that which brings about defeat. They attempted and achieved a tour de force but could not grow any further. Examples include, in response to physical challenges, the Polynesians, Eskimos, and Nomads; and in response to human challenges, the Spartans in the Hellenic world and the Osmanlis in the Orthodox Christian world.

巴比伦

Babylonic

巴比伦文明于公元前1500年之前在伊拉克兴起，它源于日渐衰落的苏美尔文明，并与后者保持着诸多共同之处。公元前7世纪，巴比伦文明经历了动荡时期，与亚述人交战。此后，在尼布甲尼撒统治时期（公元前610年至公元前539年），建立了一个新巴比伦帝国（一个统一的帝国） 。波斯人居鲁士征服后，巴比伦帝国被居鲁士的阿契美尼德帝国所吞并；巴比伦文明也融入了叙利亚文明。

Babylonic civilization emerged in Iraq before 1500 B.C. out of the disintegrating Sumeric civilization with which it continued to have much in common. It endured a Time of Troubles during the seventh century B.C. in which it was at war with the Assyrians. Following this, a Neo-Babylonic Empire (a universal state) was established from 610 to 539 B.C. under the reign of Nebuchadnezzar. After conquest by Cyrus the Persian, the Babylonic universal state was swallowed up by the universal state of the Achaemenian Empire of Cyrus; Babylonian civilization was absorbed into the Syriac civilization.

埃及

Egyptaic

埃及文明兴起于公元前4000年之前的尼罗河谷。它面临的挑战是环境方面的：如何清理、排水并开垦这片曾经无人居住的丛林沼泽。埃及文明在第四王朝时期达到鼎盛，在工程（包括金字塔）、政治管理、艺术和宗教方面都取得了重大成就。大约从公元前2424年到公元前2070/60年的动荡时期，埃及文明开始衰落，并最终形成了一个统一的帝国，该帝国大约从公元前2000年持续到公元前1788年。这个统一的帝国被喜克人入侵推翻，但后来又以新帝国的形式重建。每个统一的帝国都带来了一段“世界和平时期”，其中第二个世界和平时期持续到公元前1175年。埃及文明在公元5世纪消亡，它没有先祖，也没有后继者。

The Egyptaic civilization emerged in the Nile River Valley before 4000 B.C. The challenge it faced was environmental: to clean, drain, and cultivate what was formerly a jungle-swamp uninhabited by man. It reached a peak of growth and creativity in the fourth dynasty with major achievements in engineering (including the pyramids), political administration, art, and religion. Decline set in with the Time of Troubles from circa 2424 to 2070/60 B.C., and this resulted in a universal state, which lasted from c. 2000 to 1788 B.C. The universal state was overthrown by invasion of the Hykos but was later reestablished as the New Empire. Each universal state produced a "universal peace," the second of which lasted until 1175 B.C. The Egyptaic civilization became extinct in the fifth century A.D. It had no forebears and no successors.

远东基督教

Far Eastern Christian

远东基督教文明兴起于中亚，于公元737-741年被阿拉伯帝国吞并后消亡。它与叙利亚世界的其他地区分离了近九百年。

The Far Eastern Christian civilization arose in Central Asia and perished in 737-741 A.D when it was annexed to the Arab Empire. It had been separate from the rest of the Syriac world for nearly nine hundred years.

远东—日本分支

Far Eastern—Japanese Offshoot

日本文明是远东文明主干的一部分，公元500年后兴起于日本列岛。1185年至1597年间，日本经历了动荡时期，政治分裂，内战不断。此后，日本进入统一状态，直至1863年。禅宗佛教起源于日本文明。如今，由于受到西方文明的影响，日本文明正处于衰落之中。

The Japanese offshoot of the main body of Far Eastern civilization arose in the Japanese Archipelago after 500 A.D. It endured a Time of Troubles from 1185 to 1597 in which there was political disunity and civil war. This period was followed by a universal state until 1863. Japanese civilization produced the religion of Zen Buddhism. It is now in a state of disintegration because of the impact of Western civilization.

远东—主体

Far Eastern—Main Body

远东文明于公元500年以前在中国兴起，其根源在于逐渐衰落的汉文化。它在公元9世纪末开始衰落，经历了从公元878年到1280年的动荡时期，此后，蛮族相继建立起一系列的帝国。第一个是蒙古帝国（1280-1351年），第二个是满清帝国（1644-1853年）。远东文明至今仍然鲜活地存在着。

The Far Eastern civilization emerged in China before 500 A.D. out of the disintegrating Sinic civilization. It began to break down in the late ninth century A.D. Its Time of Troubles lasted from about 878 to 1280, followed by successive universal states founded by barbarians. The first was the Mongol Empire (1280-1351); another was the Manchu Empire (1644-1853). The Far Eastern remains a living civilization.

远西基督教

Far Western Christian

远西基督教文明主要兴起于公元375年左右的爱尔兰。凯尔特人将基督教改造以适应自身的社会传统，其独创性体现在他们的教会组织、文学和艺术中。这一文明最终在九至十一世纪被维京人的入侵、罗马教廷的权威以及十二世纪英格兰的政治统治所摧毁。

The Far Western Christian civilization arose mainly in Ireland after c. 375 A.D. The Celts molded Christianity to fit their own social heritage, and their originality can be seen in their church organization, literature, and art. This civilization was destroyed by a combination of the Vikings from the ninth to eleventh centuries, the ecclesiastical authority of Rome, and the political authority of England in the twelfth century.

希腊

Hellenic

希腊文明与米诺斯文明有着松散的联系；西方基督教文明和东正教文明都是其后裔。它最早于公元前1100年之前出现在爱琴海沿岸和岛屿上；它面临的挑战是克服贫瘠的土地、海洋以及分崩离析的米诺斯社会。希腊文明的动荡时期始于公元前431年爆发的伯罗奔尼撒战争，这场战争于公元前404年以斯巴达的胜利告终。动荡时期一直持续到公元前32年，当时一个统一的帝国——罗马帝国——建立。和平时期持续到公元378年。随着公元5世纪罗马帝国的灭亡，希腊文明也随之衰落。它在政治、艺术、文学、建筑、科学和哲学领域都取得了巨大的成就。著名人物包括柏拉图、亚里士多德、毕达哥拉斯、苏格拉底、埃斯库罗斯、索福克勒斯和芝诺。

The Hellenic civilization was loosely affiliated with the Minoan; its offspring were the Western and Orthodox Christian civilizations. It first emerged in the coasts and islands of the Aegean before 1100 B.C. ; the challenge it faced was to overcome barren land, the sea, and the disintegrating Minoan society. The Hellenic civilization's Time of Troubles began in 431 B.C. with the outbreak of the Peloponnesian war, which ended in victory for Sparta in 404 B.C. The Time of Troubles continued until 32 B.C. when a universal state, the Roman Empire, was established. A time of peace endured to 378 A.D. The Hellenic civilization collapsed with the downfall of the Roman Empire in the fifth century A.D. It produced great achievements in politics, art, literature, architecture, science, and philosophy. Notable individuals included Plato, Aristotle, Pythagoras, Socrates, Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Zeno.

印度教徒

Hindu

印度教文明兴起于公元800年之前的北印度，其前身是日渐衰落的印度文明。其动荡时期为公元1175年至1572年，之后是莫卧儿帝国（1572年至1707年）的建立。莫卧儿帝国灭亡后，不到一个世纪就被英属印度统治所取代（1818年至1947年）。印度教文明孕育了锡克教和印度教。后者与公元800年左右的商羯罗（Sankara）的名字密切相关。印度教文明至今仍然充满活力。

The Hindu civilization arose in North India before 800 A.D. out of the disintegrating Indic civilization. Its Time of Troubles was 1175-1572 A.D., followed by a universal state, the Mughal Empire, 1572-1707. When this collapsed it was replaced within a century by the British Raj, from 1818 to 1947. The Hindu civilization developed the religions of Sikhism and Hinduism. The latter is associated with the name of Sankara, who lived about 800 A.D. The Hindu civilization remains a living one.

赫梯人

Hittite

赫梯文明兴起于卡帕多西亚，位于苏美尔文明疆域之外，时间早于公元前1500年。其主要挑战在于应对日渐衰落的苏美尔文明。公元前1352年至1278年间，赫梯人与埃及持续交战。公元前1200年至1190年间，赫梯文明被一波移民潮所吞噬。

The Hittite civilization emerged from Cappadocia, just beyond the borders of the Sumeric civilization, before 1500 B.C. Its main challenge was to deal with the disintegrating Sumeric civilization. The Hittites were constantly at war with Egypt from 1352 to 1278 B.C. Hittite civilization was overwhelmed by a wave of migration from 1200 to 1190 B.C.

印度

Indic

印度文明大约于公元前1500年兴起于印度河和恒河流域，并由此扩展至整个印度次大陆。它所面临的环境挑战是恒河流域茂密的热带森林。印度文明经历了一段动荡时期，直至公元前322年。此后，孔雀王朝（公元前322-185年）建立，成为一个统一的帝国。另一个统一的帝国——笈多王朝（公元375-475年）兴起；随后，匈奴人和古尔贾拉人入侵了印度长达三百年之久。印度文明孕育了吠陀经、佛陀（公元前567-487年）和耆那教。

The Indic civilization emerged circa 1500 B.C. in the Indus and Ganges river valleys from where it spread to cover the entire Indian subcontinent. The environmental challenge to which it had to respond was the luxuriant tropical forests in the Ganges valley. Indic civilization went through a Time of Troubles up to 322 B.C. after which a universal state, the Mauryan Empire, came into being, 322-185 B.C. Another universal state, the Guptan Empire, arose c. 375-475 A.D. ; it was followed by three hundred years of invasion by Huns and Gurjaras. Indic civilization produced the religion of the Vedas, the Buddha (567-487 B.C.), and Jainism.

伊朗语

Iranic

伊朗文明与叙利亚文明密切相关。它在公元1300年之前兴起于伊朗、伊拉克、叙利亚和北非地区，源于日渐衰落的叙利亚文明。伊斯兰教是促成其诞生的温床。它的“孪生”文明是阿拉伯文明，两者于1516年融合，形成了伊斯兰文明。近代以来，泛伊斯兰运动曾提出建立一个普世国家的设想，但这一国家至今尚未出现。

The Iranic civilization was affiliated with the Syriac. It arose in Iran, Iraq, Syria, and North Africa before 1300 A.D. out of the disintegrating Syriac civilization. The chrysalis that enabled it to emerge was the Islamic Church. Its "twin" civilization was the Arabic with which it fused in 1516 to form the Islamic civilization. In modern times, the Pan-Islamic movement has suggested the coming of a universal state, but this state has not so far occurred.

玛雅

Mayan

玛雅文明大约在公元前500年之前兴起于中美洲热带雨林。它面临的挑战是如何克服森林​​的茂密植被。从一段未知的时期到公元300年，玛雅文明经历了一段动荡时期，之后玛雅第一帝国建立。尽管技术并不先进，玛雅人却达到了很高的文明水平，尤其在天文学方面成就斐然。玛雅文明在公元7世纪迅速而神秘地走向终结。其城市遗址至今仍屹立在热带雨林之中。

The Mayan civilization emerged in the Central American tropical forests before c. 500 B.C. Its challenge was to overcome the luxuriance of the forest. A Time of Troubles occurred from an unknown date until 300 A.D. after which the First Empire of the Mayas was formed. Although not technologically advanced, the Mayas achieved a high level of civilization, excelling in astronomy. The Mayan civilization came to a rapid and mysterious end in the seventh century A.D. Its ruined cities still remain in the midst of tropical forests.

墨西哥

Mexic

墨西哥文明兴起于玛雅文明瓦解之后，并与尤卡坦文明融合，形成了中美洲文明。公元1521年之前，墨西哥经历了动荡时期；阿兹特克人正处于建立统一帝国的边缘，西班牙人却突然到来。随着西方文明的传入，墨西哥文明逐渐失去了其独特的身份认同。

The Mexic civilization arose from the disintegration of the Mayan civilization and it fused with the Yucatec to form a Central American civilization. A Time of Troubles occurred up to 1521 A.D. ; the Aztecs were on the verge of establishing a universal state when the Spanish arrived. With the coming of Western civilization, the Mexic civilization lost its distinctive identity.

米诺斯

Minoan

米诺斯文明兴起于公元前3000年之前的爱琴海诸岛。它面临的主要挑战是海洋。从一段未知的时期到公元前1750年，米诺斯文明经历了动荡时期，之后统一为一个被称为米诺斯海洋王国的普世国家。它享受了350年的和平。蛮族入侵和米诺斯普世国家的瓦解之后，英雄时代在荷马史诗《伊利亚特》和《奥德赛》中得以展现。

The Minoan civilization emerged in the Aegean islands before 3000 B.C. Its main challenge was the sea. It underwent a Time of Troubles from an unknown date to 1750 B.C. before being unified in a universal state known as the Thalassocracy of Minos. It enjoyed 350 years of peace. The Heroic Age that followed the invasion by barbarians and the disintegration of the Minoan universal state can be glimpsed in the epic poetry of Homer's Iliad and Odyssey.

东正教——主体

Orthodox Christian—Main Body

东正教文明与西方文明一样，都是希腊文明的衍生分支。它源于天主教会的分裂，分裂为罗马天主教会和东正教会两个教派。这场分裂始于八世纪的圣像破坏运动，并于1054年最终完成。东正教文明在977年至1372年间经历了动荡时期；这一时期以奥斯曼帝国的建立而告终，奥斯曼帝国是一个普世性的国家，其存在时间为1372年至1768年。东正教主要分布在南欧和东欧。

The Orthodox Christian civilization is, with Western civilization, a twin offshoot of Hellenic civilization. It emerged as a result of a schism in the Catholic Church into two bodies, the Roman Catholic Church and the Orthodox Church. This split began with the Iconoclastic controversy in the eighth century and became final in 1054. Orthodox Christian civilization went through a Time of Troubles between 977 and 1372; this period ended with the establishment of a universal state, the Ottoman Empire, which lasted from 1372 to 1768. Orthodox Christianity is found mainly in southern and eastern Europe.

东正教——俄罗斯分支

Orthodox Christian—Russian Offshoot

东正教文明的俄罗斯分支于十世纪在俄罗斯兴起。其动荡时期始于十二世纪，基辅罗斯公国分裂成多个交战国；1238年蒙古人的入侵加剧了这一局面。动荡时期直至1478年才结束，莫斯科公国和诺夫哥罗德公国合并，建立了统一的俄罗斯帝国，该帝国一直延续到1881年。如同所有现存文明一样，东正教文明的俄罗斯分支也深受西方文明的影响，甚至一度走向衰落。

The Russian offshoot of the Orthodox Christian civilization emerged in Russia in the tenth century. Its Time of Troubles began in the twelfth century with the break-up of the Russian Principality of Kiev into warring states; it was aggravated by the invasion of the Mongols in 1238. The Time of Troubles did not end until 1478, when a universal state was established through the union of Muscovy and Novgorod, a state that lasted until 1881. Like all living civilizations, the Russian branch of the Orthodox civilization has been heavily influenced, to the point of breakdown, by Western civilization.

斯堪的纳维亚语

Scandinavian

斯堪的纳维亚文明兴起于罗马帝国解体之后。斯堪的纳维亚人与罗马基督教世界隔绝，他们的异教文明在公元8世纪至11世纪维京人的征服中发展壮大。然而，公元1000年冰岛人皈依基督教后，这一文明走向衰落。

The Scandinavian civilization emerged after the break-up of the Roman Empire. The Scandinavians had been isolated from Roman Christendom, and their pagan civilization grew as a result of Viking conquests from the eighth to the eleventh century. The civilization was doomed, however, after the Icelanders were converted to Christianity in the year 1000.

中华

Sinic

中华文明于公元前1500年左右在中国北方黄河下游流域发展起来。它克服了沼泽、洪水和极端气候的挑战。公元前634年至公元前221年是中华文明的动荡时期，此后，秦朝（后来的汉朝）建立，形成了一个统一的帝国。中华文明孕育了哲学家孔子和道家经典，并与源自印度文明的大乘佛教有着密切的联系。

The Sinic civilization developed in the lower valley of the Yellow River in northern China around 1500 B.C. It had to overcome the challenge of marshes, floods, and extremes of temperature. Its Time of Troubles was from 634 to 221 B.C., after which a universal state, the Ts'in Empire (and later the Han Empire) was formed. The Sinic civilization produced the philosopher Confucius and the great works of Taoism and is also associated with Mahayana Buddhism, which reached it from the Indic civilization.

苏美尔

Sumeric

苏美尔文明起源于底格里斯河-幼发拉底河下游流域，时间约为公元前4300年至公元前3100年。当时，苏美尔文明必须克服当地丛林沼泽地带的恶劣环境。公元前2677年至公元前2298年间，苏美尔城邦之间战乱不断，苏美尔文明经历了一段动荡时期。此后，苏美尔和阿卡德帝国建立起统一与和平，形成一个持续到公元前2230年的统一国家。公元前1947年左右，汉谟拉比复辟了苏美尔帝国，但汉谟拉比去世后，苏美尔帝国再次分裂。

The Sumeric civilization began in the lower Tigris-Euphrates valley c. 4300-3100. B.C., where it had to overcome the difficult jungle-swamp environment. It faced a Time of Troubles from c. 2677-2298 B.C., in which Sumerian city-states were at war. After this the Empire of Sumer and Akkad established unity and peace in a universal state that lasted until 2230 B.C. It was restored by Hammurabi c. 1947 B.C. and broke up after his death.

叙利亚语

Syriac

叙利亚文明于公元前1100年之前在叙利亚兴起，其前身是日渐衰落的米诺斯文明。公元前937年至525年间，叙利亚文明经历了动荡时期，之后发展成为统一的阿契美尼德帝国（约公元前525年至332年）。公元前最后一个世纪，叙利亚文明吸收了巴比伦文明。公元640年至969年，阿拉伯哈里发国时期，叙利亚文明再次崛起。叙利亚社会的三大成就包括：发明字母、发现大西洋，以及形成犹太教、琐罗亚斯德教、基督教和伊斯兰教共同认可的一神论信仰。通过其普世教会——伊斯兰教，叙利亚文明促进了伊朗文明和阿拉伯文明的兴起。

The Syriac civilization emerged in Syria before 1100 B.C. out of the disintegrating Minoan civilization. From c. 937 to 525 B.C. it went through a Time of Troubles before becoming a universal state in the form of the Achaemenian Empire, c. 525-332 B.C. In the last century B.C., it absorbed the Babylonic civilization. A second universal state occurred during the Arab Caliphate, 640-969 A.D. Three great achievements of Syriac society were the invention of the alphabet, the discovery of the Atlantic Ocean, and a monotheistic conception of God that is common to Judaism, Zoroastrianism, Christianity, and Islam. Through its universal church, Islam, Syriac civilization contributed to the rise of the Iranic and Arabic civilizations.

西

Western

西方文明兴起于公元700年之前的西欧，与希腊文明密切相关，它诞生于罗马帝国解体后的混乱时期。八世纪时，西方文明的范围仅限于不列颠以及查理曼大帝在西欧的领地，但此后它展现出扩张的趋势。这种扩张真正开始于十五世纪末。自那时起，西方文明遍布全球，侵蚀或吸收了其他所有现存文明。这一点在经济和政治领域尤为明显；尽管如此，西方文明的崛起并未抹杀其他文明的独特文化。西方文明可以分为四个时期或阶段：黑暗时代（675-1075年）；中世纪（1075-1475年）；近代（1475-1875年）；以及自1875年起，汤因比暂且称之为后现代的时期。

Western civilization arose in Western Europe before 700 A.D. Affiliated with the Hellenic civilization, it arose out of the chaos that followed the break-up of the Roman Empire. In the eighth century, it covered only Britain and the dominions of Charlemagne in Western Europe, but it has shown a tendency to expand its boundaries. This expansion began in earnest in the last quarter of the fifteenth century. Since then, Western civilization has spread across the globe, encroaching on or absorbing all other living civilizations. This is particularly apparent in the economic and political spheres; this ascendency has not yet obliterated the distinctive cultures of the other civilizations. Western Civilization can be divided into four periods or chapters: the Dark Ages (675-1075); Middle Ages (1075-1475); Modern (1475-1875); and from 1875 on, which Toynbee tentatively describes as Postmodern.

尤卡坦半岛

Yucatec

尤卡坦文明是玛雅文明的后裔。它于公元629年后兴起于尤卡坦半岛荒凉的石灰岩地带，必须克服干燥贫瘠的地形。同时，它还面临着如何从日渐衰落的玛雅文明中建立自身独特身份的挑战。尽管尤卡坦人精通冶金术，但其文明从未达到玛雅文明的高度；最终在12世纪被墨西哥文明所吞并。

The Yucatec civilization was the offspring of the Mayan civilization. It arose after 629 A.D. on a desolate limestone shelf of the Yucatan peninsula and had to overcome the dry, treeless terrain. It also had the challenge of forming an identity distinct from the disintegrating Mayan civilization. Although its people were skilled in metallurgy, the Yucatec civilization never achieved the heights that the Mayan had; it was absorbed by the Mexic civilization in the twelfth century.

主题

Themes

历史规律

Laws of History

《历史研究》的基本前提是，文明的兴起、发展、衰落和瓦解都遵循着一种连贯的、周期性的模式。由于汤因比认为宇宙并非混沌，而是受制于某种规律，因此他认为这些规律也必然能在人类历史中得到体现。

The basic premise of A Study of History is that civilizations emerge, grow, break down, and disintegrate according to a consistent, recurrent pattern. Since Toynbee believed that the universe was not chaotic but subject to laws, he argued that those laws must also be observable in human history.

尽管《历史研究》是一部鸿篇巨制，但其所阐述的历史运行规律的基本框架却十分简洁。原始社会之所以能发展成为文明，是因为它成功地应对了自然环境或人文环境方面的挑战。这种挑战与应对的模式不断循环往复，因为每一次成功应对的挑战都会引发新的挑战，而新的挑战又需要新的创造性应对，如此往复。汤因比借用中国哲学中的术语，将此描述为从阴（静止）到阳（行动）的转变，并宣称这是宇宙的基本韵律之一。

Although A Study of History is a voluminous work, the basic outline of this pattern of laws operating in history is quite simple. A primitive society evolves into a civilization because it successfully responds to a challenge in either the physical or the human environment. This pattern of challenge and response continually recurs because each successfully met challenge generates another challenge, which demands another creative response, and so on. Employing terms taken from Chinese philosophy, Toynbee identifies this as a movement from the state of Yin (rest) to that of Yang (action) and declares it to be one of the fundamental rhythms of the universe.

社会发展的引擎是富有创造力的个人和群体。他们也遵循着一条规律，汤因比将其定义为“退出与回归”。他们或字面意义上或象征意义上地退出社会，发展知识、智慧或力量，然后回归社会，将他们的劳动成果带给所有人。随后，大多数人效仿这些富有创造力的少数人，进行模仿，文明由此进步。汤因比指出，“退出与回归”规律不仅存在于人类领域，也存在于农作物的年度收割与收获中。他还指出，同样的规律也适用于人类的精神成长，尤其体现在基督教的复活教义中。这正是汤因比惯用的论证方式，他运用自然界以及政治以外的其他人类活动领域中的类比，来强调其关于循环模式和规律的观点。

The engines of societal growth are creative individuals and groups. They also obey a law, which Toynbee defines as withdrawal-and-return. They withdraw from society, whether literally or figuratively, and develop knowledge, wisdom, or power, and then return to society and bring the benefits of their labors to everyone. The majority then follows this creative minority in a process of mimesis, or imitation, and so the civilization advances. Toynbee points out that the law of withdrawal-and-return can be found not only in the human sphere but also in the annual withdrawal and return of agricultural crops. He also notes that the same law applies to human spiritual growth, especially in the Christian doctrine of resurrection. This is typical of the way Toynbee uses analogies from the natural world and from spheres of human activity other than the political to emphasize his point about recurring patterns and laws.

进一步研究的主题

Topics for Further Study

  • 西方为何在当今世界占据如此主导地位？西方是正义的力量吗？如果是，为何许多其他国家，特别是亚洲和阿拉伯世界的国家，对西方在其事务中的影响力感到不满？
  • Why has the West assumed such a dominant role in the world today? Is the West a force for good? If it is, why do many other countries, particularly in Asia and the Arab world, resent Western influence in their affairs?
  • 西方民主和资本主义是否总是组织人类社会的最佳方式？或者是否存在其他同样有效的方式？如果存在，这些方式是什么？它们在哪些情况下可能有效？（您可以考虑部落主义、社会主义、共产主义、君主制和/或独裁制。）
  • Are Western democracy and capitalism always the best ways to organize human society, or might other ways be equally valid? If so, what might those ways be, and in what situations might they work? (You might want to consider tribalism, socialism, communism, monarchy, and/or dictatorship.)
  • 汤因比的挑战与回应定律是否足以解释文明的起源？根据汤因比定律，艰难的环境比轻松的环境更有利于发展。这条定律是否也适用于个人生活？你能举出一些自己生活中，逆境比安逸更能带来成功的例子吗？
  • Is Toynbee's law of challenge-and-response an adequate explanation of the genesis of civilizations? According to Toynbee's law, a difficult environment is more conducive to growth than an easy one. Might such a law apply in the lives of individuals, too? Can you list any examples from your own life when hardship produced more success than ease might have done?
  • 探究汤因比关于“退出与回归”法则在创意人士生活中的应用。他列举了七位伟人的生平事迹：圣保罗、圣本笃、圣格里高利一世、佛陀、穆罕默德、马基雅维利和但丁。请再研究两位历史人物，并阐述同样的法则如何促成了他们的一些重大成就。此外，思考一下你自己的人生经历。你是否也曾经历过类似的“退出与回归”的节奏？
  • Explore Toynbee's law of withdrawal and return in the lives of creative people. He gives examples from the lives of seven great men: St. Paul, St. Benedict, St. Gregory the Great, the Buddha, Muhammad, Machiavelli, and Dante. Research two more historical figures and show how the same law operated to produce some of their significant achievements. Also, think of achievements in your own life. Was a rhythm of withdrawal and return operating there, too?

文明的衰落也遵循着可预见的规律。当少数富有创造力的人无法再成功应对挑战时，文明的衰落便会发生。“内部无产阶级”不再认为有任何理由追随它，“外部无产阶级”（那些身处文明正式边界之外，却受其内部发展影响的群体）则变得敌对。随之而来的是社会动荡和战争，汤因比将这一阶段称为“动荡时期”。在动荡时期之后，一位征服者会崛起，他通过建立普世国家来强加和平，试图扭转文明的衰落。罗马帝国就是一个普世国家的范例，也是汤因比讨论的众多模式之一，它诞生于希腊文明的瓦解阶段。然而，普世国家无法永久扭转文明的衰落，也无法避免文明最终的崩溃。

The breakdown of a civilization also follows predictable laws. It occurs when the creative minority is no longer able to meet a challenge successfully. The "internal proletariat" no longer sees any reason to follow it, and the "external proletariat" (those groups outside the civilization's formal borders who are influenced by developments within it) becomes hostile. Social disruption and wars follow, a stage that Toynbee calls a Time of Troubles. Out of the Time of Troubles emerges a conqueror, who imposes peace through a universal state, which is an attempt to reverse the decline. An example of a universal state, and one of the many models that Toynbee discusses, is the Roman Empire, which was born out of the disintegration phase of Hellenic civilization. But the universal state cannot permanently reverse the decline or avoid the eventual collapse of the civilization.

汤因比的目光所及之处，无不体现着模式、规律、节奏和循环，不仅体现在文明的兴衰大局，也体现在细微之处。例如，在分析过去四百年的西方近代史时，他发现了一个反复出现的四周期模式，并在第十一章中以表格形式呈现：首先是“序曲”，其中的小规模战争如同序幕，预示着即将到来的大战；随后是“全面战争”；接着是相对和平的“喘息期”；然后是“补充战争”（他称之为“尾声”）；最后是“全面和平”。汤因比指出，类似的模式也存在于希腊和中华文明中。

Everywhere Toynbee looks, he sees patterns, laws, rhythms, and cycles, not only in the broad rise and fall of civilizations, but in smaller details, too. For example, when analyzing modern Western history over the previous four hundred years, he discerns a recurring pattern of four cycles, which he even puts in tabular form in chapter 11: first there is an "overture" in which minor wars serve as a prelude to what is to come; this is followed first by a "general war," then by a "breathing space" of relative peace, then by a "supplementary war" (which he calls the Epilogue), and finally by a "general peace." Toynbee states that similar patterns can be found in the Hellenic and Sinic civilizations.

汤因比意识到，在发展历史规律这一概念时，他与现代历史研究的主流趋势背道而驰。他指出，许多现代历史学家拒绝接受这样一种观点：历史可以从一个基于可辨识规律运作的有序模式来理解。他引用了一位不知名的英国小说家的话，这位小说家为了表达历史不过是一系列毫无意义的事件，创造了“Odtaa”一词，意为“一件又一件的糟糕事”。但汤因比对此持不同意见。

Toynbee was aware that in developing the concept of laws of history he was going against the grain as far as the modern trend of historical study was concerned. He noted that many modern historians rejected the idea that history could be understood in terms of an orderly pattern based on the operation of discernible laws. He quoted an unidentified English novelist who, wishing to express the idea that history was merely a succession of meaningless events, coined the word "Odtaa," standing for "one damn thing after another." But Toynbee begged to differ.

汤因比体系的一个结果是，如果被接受，它将使整个人类历史变得可以理解。这也意味着，鉴于存在反复出现的模式，历史学家将能够对人类历史的未来走向做出预测，汤因比确实在《历史研究》第十二章中尝试了这项任务，尽管他当时有些不情愿。

One consequence of Toynbee's system is that, if accepted, it would make the entirety of human history intelligible. It would also mean that given the presence of recurring patterns, the historian would be in a position to offer predictions about the future course of human history, a task that Toynbee did indeed undertake, if somewhat reluctantly, in chapter 12 of A Study of History.

宗教

Religion

汤因比在1955年写道：“宗教再次在我构建的宇宙图景中占据了中心地位。”这一点在《历史研究》第七章中得到了清晰的阐述。在前几章中，汤因比认为文明是历史的主导因素，而高等宗教（基督教、佛教、伊斯兰教和印度教）只是文明衰落的副产品。在第七章中，他提出了一种新的观点。他不再从文明的角度看待宗教，而是从宗教的角度看待文明。根据这种观点，文明的全部意义在于为高等宗教的出现提供契机。（汤因比所说的“高等宗教”指的是那些普世的宗教，与那些仅限于某个部落或狭隘国家的低等宗教相对。）汤因比新观点的一个结果是，一个文明的崩溃和瓦解并不意味着它的失败；相反，宗教或许已经成功完成了它的主要任务。与其说宗教是文明的副产品（而文明可能还有其他主要目的），不如说它是文明最宝贵的成果。

Writing in 1955, Toynbee stated, "Religion has come, once again, to take the central place in my picture of the Universe." This is made clear in chapter 7 of A Study of History. In earlier chapters, Toynbee takes the view that civilizations are the dominant element in history, and that the higher religions (Christianity, Buddhism, Islam, and Hinduism) are only by-products, coming into existence as the civilizations disintegrate. In chapter 7, he describes a new view. Rather than seeing religions in terms of civilizations, he now sees civilizations in terms of religions. According to this view, the whole purpose of civilization is to provide an opportunity for a higher religion to come into being. (By "higher religions" Toynbee means those religions that are universal, as opposed to the lower religions that are merely local and restricted to one tribe or parochial state.) A consequence of Toynbee's new perspective is that the breakdown and disintegration of a civilization does not mean that it has failed; on the contrary, it may have succeeded in its main task. Rather than being a by-product of a civilization that may have some other main purpose, religion is civilization's most valuable fruit.

汤因比用车轮的比喻进一步阐述了他的观点。他认为文明的兴衰如同车轮的转动，车轮“承载着它所承载的载体”。汤因比所说的“车轮”所承载的载体就是宗教。

Toynbee uses an analogy of the wheel to explicate his view further. He sees the rising and falling of civilizations like revolutions of a wheel, which "carry forward the vehicle which the wheel conveys." The vehicle conveyed by Toynbee's wheel is religion.

汤因比也用同样的意象来解释为什么高等宗教诞生于世事低谷、衰落时期。他认为这是必然的，因为存在一条精神法则：进步唯有通过苦难才能实现。世俗生活的低谷或许正是精神历史的高峰。

The same image helps Toynbee explain why the higher religions are born during the downward turns of the wheel, in periods of decline. He argues that this is necessary because there is a spiritual law according to which progress comes only through suffering. Low points in secular life may be high points in spiritual history.

尽管汤因比深受其自身文明的基督教影响，但他并不认为基督教是通往精神启迪的唯一途径。他认为所有高等宗教都发挥着作用，尤其是在当今时代，全球通信促进了不同信仰之间前所未有的交流。他设想，在即将到来的包含全世界的普世国家中，或许会出现这样一种可能性。

Although he is steeped in the Christianity of his own civilization, Toynbee does not regard Christianity as the only route to spiritual enlightenment. He sees all the higher religions as having a role to play, particularly in the modern age in which global communications have facilitated an unprecedented level of contact between different faiths. He considers the possibility that in a forthcoming universal state that includes the whole world

现存的四大宗教的信徒们或许会意识到，他们曾经相互竞争的体系，其实是通往唯一真神的各种替代途径，这些途径提供了对至福景象的不同片段的窥见。

the respective adherents of the four living higher religions might come to recognize that their once rival systems were so many alternate approaches to the One True God along avenues offering diverse partial glimpses of the Beatific Vision.

汤因比甚至设想，未来各种不同的宗教可能会走到一起，组成一个统一的教会。

Toynbee even envisions the possibility that in the future, the diverse religions might come together and form a single church.

风格

Style

明喻和类比

Simile and Analogy

汤因比经常运用明喻和类比，将两个看似截然不同的事物进行比较。这些明喻的目的是帮助读者形象化地理解所提出的概念。书中反复出现一个较长的明喻，即汤因比将文明比作攀登高山。原始文明就像躺在悬崖边上睡觉的人，上下都是悬崖，他们无法继续前进。停滞不前的文明就像攀登者，已经到达一定高度却发现自己被困住了，既不能前进也不能后退。而蓄势待发的文明则像刚刚起身开始攀登悬崖的登山者，他们不会停下脚步，直到要么跌回悬崖边，要么到达更高的悬崖。

Toynbee frequently makes use of similes and analogies in which two apparently dissimilar things are compared. The purpose of these similes is to enable the reader to visualize the concept that is being presented and make it easier to grasp. One extended simile recurs at several points in the book, and that is Toynbee's comparison of civilizations to humans climbing a mountain. Primitive civilizations are like people lying asleep on a ledge with a precipice below and a precipice above. No further progress is possible for them. Arrested civilizations are like climbers who have reached a certain height but now find themselves blocked; they can go neither forward nor backward. Civilizations that are ready to grow, however, are like climbers who have just risen to their feet and are beginning to climb the face of the cliff. They cannot stop until they either fall back to the ledge or reach another, higher ledge.

在另一个延伸的比喻中，汤因比将文明中少数富有创造力的人的影响力比作向外辐射的光束。他称之为“文化射线”，即一个文明的经济、政治和文化层面辐射到其正式边界之外的人们。他对这一过程的描述体现了汤因比散文中常见的诗意特质：

In another extended simile, Toynbee compares the influence of a creative minority in a civilization to a physical beam of light that radiates outward. He calls this a "culture ray," in which the economic, political, and cultural aspects of a civilization radiate to those living outside its formal borders. His description of the process is an example of the poetic quality of much of Toynbee's prose:

光线会尽可能地照射到它所能到达的最远之处，直到消失。光线的渐变极其细微，根本无法划定暮色最后一丝微光熄灭、黑暗彻底吞噬一切的界限。

The light shines as far as, in the nature of things, it can carry until it reaches its vanishing-point. The gradations are infinitesimal, and it is impossible to demarcate the line at which the last glimmer of twilight flickers out and leaves the heart of darkness in undivided possession.

汤因比认为文化射线是一种非常强大的力量，因为尽管文明是人类历史上相对较新的发展，但在这段时期内，文明已经成功地或多或少地渗透到几乎所有原始社会中。

Toynbee believes the culture ray to be a very powerful force because although civilizations are a comparatively recent development in human history, they have succeeded during that period in permeating, to a greater or lesser extent, virtually every primitive society.

第三个延伸比喻是水坝。汤因比用这个比喻来说明一个正在瓦解的文明如何建立一道军事屏障，将自己与境外的异化群体隔离开来。这道屏障就像一座实体水坝。然而，从长远来看，它总是会被突破。在第八章中，汤因比运用水坝的比喻，对这一过程进行了详尽而精妙的阐述。水坝上方积聚的水（相当于文明中敌对的外部无产阶级）试图与下方的水（文明本身）达到同一水平，并为此不断施加压力。正如工程师建造水闸作为安全阀一样，防御的文明也试图采取各种措施来防止自己的“水坝”坍塌。其中一种方法就是拉拢一些蛮族敌人加入自己的阵营。但安全阀失效了，因为军事屏障之外的力量（相当于大坝上方的水位）一直在不断上升。当蛮族最终突破防线时，其结果就像实体大坝决堤一样，是普天同庆的灾难。

A third extended simile is that of a dam. Toynbee uses this to illustrate the way a disintegrating civilization creates a military barrier to insulate itself from the alienated people outside its borders. The barrier acts like a physical dam. However, in the long run it is always breached. In chapter 8, Toynbee elaborates at great length and sophistication, in terms of his simile of the dam, on how this happens. The water that is piled up above the dam (the equivalent of the civilization's hostile external proletariat) seeks to find the same level as the water below it (the civilization), and it continually exerts pressure to that effect. Just as engineers construct safety valves in the form of sluices, so does the defending civilization attempt techniques that will keep its own "dam" from collapsing. One technique is to enlist some of their barbarian adversaries on their own side. But the safety valves fail because the forces outside the military barrier (the equivalent of the water above the dam) are forever on the rise. When the barbarians finally break through, the result, as with the bursting of a physical dam, is calamity all round.

风格

Style

有人将历史研究比作一条奔腾不息的河流，蜿蜒流淌，汇聚众多支流的力量。它也可以被视为一座宏伟的大教堂，教堂内的每一块石头、每一扇彩色玻璃窗、每一处历史遗迹，都共同构筑起这座最终的丰碑，展现出人类精神追求的崇高境界。在构建这座宏伟的殿堂时，汤因比不仅似乎对众多不同的文明了如指掌，他的论述风格也同样兼收并蓄。除了作为历史学家职业工具的事实和历史研究之外，他还经常运用神话和世界文学。他善于找到恰如其分的引语来完美地表达自己的观点，例如，他引用美国诗人沃尔特·惠特曼的一首诗中的一段话来阐释汤因比关于文明发展中挑战与回应循环往复的理论：“万物皆有其本质，任何成功的果实，无论其结果如何，都必然会带来新的挑战，使之成为更大斗争的必要条件。”莎士比亚被用来阐释文明的毁灭并非源于外力，而是源于内部；他再次引用莎士比亚的作品，是为了阐释汤因比的“以太化”定律，即一个新兴文明的生命力领域从外部世界转向内部世界。歌德的诗作《浮士德》被用来阐释挑战与回应的模式，而对《圣经》和古典神话的引用则不胜枚举。汤因比运用这种兼收并蓄的方法来探讨他的主题，旨在捕捉人类历史中一个至关重要的维度，而这或许是仅凭严格的经验事实方法所无法企及的。

A Study of History has been compared to a mighty river, meandering along its course and gathering strength from many tributary streams. It might also be thought of as a great cathedral, in which every stone, every stained-glass window, and every historical monument in the interior help to create the final edifice that reveals the grandeur of humanity's spiritual aspiration. In constructing this imposing edifice, not only does Toynbee seem to know almost everything about so many different civilizations, his style of exposition is equally eclectic. In addition to the facts and historical research that are the tools of the historian's profession, he makes frequent use of mythology and world literature. He has an eye for the apt quotation that perfectly captures his idea, as when a passage from a poem by American poet Walt Whitman illustrates the Toynbee theory of a recurring rhythm of challenge and response through which a civilization grows: "It is provided in the essence of things that from any fruition of success, no matter what, shall come forth something to make a greater struggle necessary." Shakespeare is employed to illustrate the idea that civilizations are destroyed not by outside forces but from within; Shakespeare is brought in again to illustrate the Toynbee law of "etherialization," in which the vital sphere of a growing civilization shifts from the external to the internal world. Goethe's poem Faust is used to illustrate the challenge and response pattern, and references to the Bible and classical myths are too numerous to detail. Toynbee uses this eclectic approach to his subject to capture a vital dimension of human history that may be unobtainable by a method that deals only with strict empirical facts.

历史背景

Historical Context

民族主义

Nationalism

民族主义的兴起是十九世纪和二十世纪西方历史上最重要的发展之一。汤因比厌恶民族主义，认为它是现代世界诸多弊病之一，也是战争的根源。他还认为，过分强调民族国家会导致历史的扭曲。因此，他选择文明而非民族作为历史研究的基本单位。汤因比还希望对抗另一种危险的现代倾向——欧洲中心主义或西方偏见，他称之为“自我中心的错觉”。他不认为西方文明是人类发展的巅峰，因为这样就无法客观评价起源于中国和印度的文明，更遑论南美洲和中美洲的文明。

The growth of nationalism was one of the most important developments in nineteenth-and twentieth-century Western history. Toynbee disliked nationalism, regarding it as one of the besetting evils of the modern world. He believed it was the cause of war. He also believed that emphasis on the nation-state led to distorted versions of history. This was why he took civilizations rather than nations as the units of historical study. Toynbee also wanted to combat another dangerous modern tendency, that of Eurocentric or Western bias, which he called an "egocentric illusion." He did not view Western civilization as the apex of human development since this left no room for objective evaluation of civilizations originating in China and India, let alone South and Central American civilizations.

第一次世界大战和第二次世界大战

World Wars I and II

《历史研究》深受汤因比所处时代的影响。1920年，西方文明正面临着从第一次世界大战的破坏中恢复的挑战。正是在那一年，汤因比首次萌生了文明兴衰可能存在统一规律的想法。他注意到西方当时的处境与公元前五世纪伯罗奔尼撒战争后希腊文明所面临的挑战有着惊人的相似之处。这两种文明之间的相似性正是《历史研究》一书的雏形。

A Study of History was influenced by the times in which Toynbee lived. In 1920, Western civilization was facing the challenge of recovering after the devastation of World War I. It was in that year that Toynbee first conceived the idea that there might be uniform laws governing the rise and fall of civilizations. He noted striking parallels between the situation facing the West and the challenge faced by the Hellenic civilization following the Pelopponesian war in the fifth century B.C. This parallel between the two civilizations was the seed idea that led to the structure elaborated in A Study of History.

第二次世界大战也影响了汤因比的著作。《历史研究》第四至六卷出版于战争爆发的那一年。由于这两卷主要探讨文明的崩溃和瓦解，因此出版得非常及时。

World War II also affected Toynbee's work. Volumes 4-6 of A Study of History were published the year the war broke out. Since they dealt for the most part with the breakdown and disintegration of civilizations, they were very timely.

由于战争，汤因比直到1947年才得以恢复工作。战争期间发生的种种事件改变了他的观点。由于新研制出的原子弹的毁灭性威力，他对西方文明和其他文明的未来前景持更为悲观的看法。此外，他对世界各大宗教的重视程度也远超以往。

Toynbee was unable to resume his work until 1947 because of the war. His views had been modified by the intervening events. Because of the destructive power of the newly created atomic bomb, he now possessed a more pessimistic view of the future prospects for Western and other civilizations. He also allocated a far more important place to the higher religions of the world than before.

比较与对比

Compare & Contrast

  • 1950年代：美国和苏联之间的冷战主导了世界政治格局。

    如今，世界不再由两个相互竞争的超级大国组成。美国通常被认为是唯一的超级大国，但还有其他区域性权力中心，例如欧盟成员国，以及日本、俄罗斯和中国。

  • 1950s: The Cold War between the United States and the Soviet Union dominates the political landscape of the world.

    Today: The world is no longer divided into two competing superpowers. The United States is generally considered to be the sole superpower, but there are other regional centers of power, such as the countries that comprise the European Union, as well as Japan, Russia, and China.

  • 20世纪50年代：工业化世界普遍预期，机器和机器人很快将取代目前由人类完成的许多工作。人们预计这将带来更多的休闲时间。汤因比认为，这可能意味着人们会花更多的时间参与宗教活动。

    如今，美国人的平均工作时间比上一代人更长。尽管科技发展带来了更多样的休闲方式，但机械化并未带来更多的休闲时间。

  • 1950s: There is a wide expectation in the industrialized world that machines and robots will soon take over many of the tasks now performed by humans. This is expected to result in greater leisure. Toynbee believes this may mean that people will spend more of their time participating in religious practices.

    Today: The average American works more hours than his or her counterpart did a generation ago. Mechanization has not resulted in more leisure although technology has produced a greater variety of choices as to how leisure time is spent.

  • 20世纪50年代：全球人口接近30亿，控制人口增长的努力开始出现。约翰·D·洛克菲勒三世成立了人口理事会；国际计划生育联合会也成立了。

    如今：联合国估计，世界人口在1999年底突破60亿，并以每年7700万的速度增长。人口控制工作不仅强调避孕和计划生育，还强调改善妇女生活的各个方面，包括健康、教育、政治权利和经济独立。众所周知，高教育水平和经济繁荣与较低的出生率密切相关。

  • 1950s: Global population nears three billion, and efforts to curb it begin. The Population Council is formed by John D. Rockefeller III; the International Planned Parenthood Federation is also formed.

    Today: The United Nations estimates that world population passed six billion in late 1999 and is growing at the rate of seventy-seven million a year. Efforts at population control emphasize not only contraception and family planning but also the improvement of every aspect of women's lives, including health, education, political rights, and economic independence. High levels of education, coupled with economic prosperity, are known to correlate with lower birth rates.

冷战

The Cold War

在汤因比撰写其最后几卷著作时，二战后崛起的美苏两大超级大国正处于冷战时期，主导着世界。汤因比在《历史研究》第十二章中对此形势进行了深入探讨。他指出，如果爆发第三次世界大战，其后果可能是地球上人类的灭绝。他认为唯一的出路是实现世界政治统一，并将核武器的控制权掌握在一个超级大国手中。他认为联合国无法胜任这一角色，必须由美苏两大超级大国之一来承担。在这两个超级大国中，汤因比更倾向于美国。他认为，美国拥有联邦制的历史，更适合在世界政府中担任主导地位。

When Toynbee was writing his final volumes, the Cold War between the United States and Russia, the two superpowers that had emerged after World War II, dominated the world. Toynbee gave considerable attention to this situation in chapter 12 of A Study of History. He noted that if a third world war should break out, the result might be the annihilation of human life on Earth. He concluded that the only alternative was to move toward political unification of the world with control of nuclear weapons in the hands of one power. He did not see the United Nations as capable of fulfilling such a role. Instead, it would have to be carried out by one of the two superpowers. Of these two powers, Toynbee held a more favorable view of the United States. Toynbee found the United States, with its history of federalism, more suited to assuming the principal position in a world government.

人口增长

Population Growth

汤因比认为，二十世纪下半叶，世界面临的主要问题之一是人口无节制增长。他指出，从十九世纪中叶到二十世纪中叶的一百年间，中国的人口翻了一番。印度和印度尼西亚这两个世界上人口最多的国家，也呈现出类似的增长速度。人口增长很大程度上是由于婴儿和儿童死亡率的下降。汤因比认为，如果这种趋势持续下去，世界人口将超过地球的粮食承载能力。即使是最先进的技术也无法阻止这种情况的发生。毕竟，汤因比认为，地球表面是有限的；地球能够生产的粮食数量是有限的。除非建立一个世界政府来限制人类的生育权，否则人口过剩将导致饥荒。汤因比也承认，这种对个人自由的剥夺会造成诸多困难和紧张局势。

In the second half of the twentieth century, one of the major problems facing the world, Toynbee believed, was unchecked population growth. He pointed out that the population of China had doubled in the hundred years from the middle of the nineteenth century to the middle of the twentieth. India and Indonesia, two of the most populous countries in the world, showed a similar rate of population growth. Much of the growth was due to reduced infant and child mortality rates. Toynbee believed that if the trend continued, world population would outstrip the earth's capacity to feed everyone. Even the most sophisticated technology would not be able to prevent this. After all, Toynbee argued, the Earth's surface is finite; only so much food can be produced from it. The result of over-population would be famine unless a world government was formed to restrict the rights of humans to procreate. Toynbee acknowledged the many difficulties and tensions this loss of personal liberty would create.

关键概述

Critical Overview

《历史研究》一书引起了截然不同的两种反应。普通大众对这本书反响热烈，但学院派历史学家普遍对汤因比的著作持严厉批评态度。

There were two markedly different reactions to A Study of History. The general public gave the book an enthusiastic reception, but academic historians were in general severely critical of Toynbee's work.

1947年，索默维尔对汤因比著作第一至六卷的节选本出版后，汤因比在美国赢得了广泛的赞誉。《国家》杂志上E·D·迈尔斯的评论正是非专业人士对这本书赞誉的典型代表。迈尔斯评论说，汤因比的概念和分析“重要且卓越，值得认真研究；他的论述正如索默维尔先生所说的那样‘引人入胜’”。迈尔斯建议，如果读者当年只能读一本书，就应该选择《历史研究》。

In 1947, when Somervell's abridgement of volumes 1-6 appeared, Toynbee won wide popular acclaim in the United States. E. D. Myers' review in the Nation was typical of the praise heaped on the book by nonspecialists. Myers commented that Toynbee's concepts and analysis were "of sufficient importance and excellence to merit serious study; his presentation is as 'entrancing' as Mr. Somervell suggests it is." Myers suggested that if readers had time to read only one book that year, they should select A Study of History.

许多美国购书者也有同感。节选版成为每月读书俱乐部的推荐书目，并成为畅销书。仅1947年一年，销量就超过12.9万册，接下来几年的总销量接近25万册。汤因比本人也因此小有名气。他受邀在布林莫尔学院举办系列讲座，1947年3月，他的照片登上《时代》杂志封面，该杂志刊登了一篇对《历史研究》的长篇赞誉之词。报刊、广播和电视节目对这本书进行了大量的讨论，汤因比被誉为二战后时代的先知。在1956年出版的《汤因比与历史：评论文章与评述》一书中，M.F.阿什利·蒙塔古评论道，这本书“是当时最著名、讨论最广泛的书籍之一”。

Much of the American book-buying public felt the same way. The abridged version became a Book-of-the-Month club selection and a bestseller. In 1947 alone, over 129,000 copies were sold, and total sales in the next few years reached almost 250,000. Toynbee himself became a minor celebrity. He was invited to give a lecture series at Bryn Mawr College, and in March 1947, his photograph appeared on the cover of Time magazine, which contained a lengthy and laudatory review of A Study of History. There were numerous discussions of the book in the press and on radio and television, and Toynbee was hailed as a prophet for the post-World War II era. In Toynbee and History: Critical Essays and Reviews, published in 1956, M. F. Ashley Montagu commented that the book "constitutes one of the most famous and most widely discussed books of its time."

然而，大多数专业历史学家并不认同这位同行的著作所受到的如此崇高评价。1954年，《历史研究》（1957年由索默维尔删节）第7至10卷出版后，历史学家们对其进行了猛烈的批评。英国著名历史学家休·特雷弗-罗珀在《星期日泰晤士报》（后收录于《汤因比与历史》）上撰文指出：

However, most professional historians did not share this exalted opinion of their colleague's work. In 1954, following the publication of volumes 7-10 of A Study of History (abridged by Somervell in 1957), there was a barrage of harsh criticism by historians. Prominent British historian Hugh Trevor-Roper wrote in the Sunday Times (reprinted in Toynbee and History) :

汤因比教授的基本假设不仅常常值得怀疑，而且他对这些假设的应用也常常是武断的，更何况他的技术方法根本就不是“经验性的”。这些理论并非从事实中推导而来，也并非经事实检验：事实是经过挑选，有时甚至是人为调整的，其目的仅仅是为了阐释这些理论，而这些理论本身却建立在空洞的理论之上。

Not only are Professor Toynbee's basic assumptions often questionable, and his application of them often arbitrary, but his technical method turns out to be not 'empirical' at all. The theories are not deduced from the facts, not tested by them: the facts are selected, sometimes adjusted, to illustrate the theories, which themselves rest effortlessly on air.

另一位杰出的英国历史学家A.J.P.泰勒同样对汤因比的著作不屑一顾。他在伦敦《新政治家》杂志上（后收录于《汤因比与历史》一书中）宣称，汤因比的著作并非他所理解的历史，泰勒评论道：

Another distinguished British historian, A. J. P. Taylor, was equally dismissive of Toynbee's work. Declaring in London's New Statesman (reprinted in Toynbee and History) that the work was not what he understood history to be, Taylor commented:

汤因比教授的方法不是学术研究，而是碰运气，而且非常注重运气……如果将过去的事件按照合适的模式排列，它们就可以用来证明任何事情；汤因比教授成功地将它们强行塞进了他从一开始就在脑海中构想的方案中。

Professor Toynbee's method is not that of scholarship, but of the lucky dip, with emphasis on the luck.… The events of the past can be made to prove anything if they are arranged in a suitable pattern; and Professor Toynbee has succeeded in forcing them into a scheme that was in his head from the beginning.

其他学术批评家也纷纷提出类似的批评，抨击汤因比的研究方法和结论。一些人认为这部著作是失败的，汤因比不仅事实错误，而且还将历史与过多的神学和形而上学混为一谈，试图扮演先知和宗教道德家的角色。不同历史领域的专家也试图反驳汤因比在其各自专业领域内的论述。

Other academic critics weighed in with similar criticisms, attacking Toynbee's methods and his conclusions. Some said the work was a failure, that Toynbee got his facts wrong and also mixed up his history with too much theology and metaphysics, trying to take on the mantle of prophet and religious moralist. Specialists in different fields of history attempted to refute what Toynbee had written about their particular area of expertise.

美国学院派历史学家也对汤因比的著作提出了类似的批评，尽管著名社会学家皮蒂里姆·索罗特金在《现代史杂志》（后收录于《汤因比与历史》）上发表了一篇较为积极的文章。尽管他质疑汤因比关于文明是一个统一整体的基本观点，并认为汤因比将所有文明都生硬地套入起源、发展和衰落的模式过于僵化，但他仍然认为汤因比的著作是“我们这个时代在历史综合领域最重要的著作之一”。

There was similar criticism of Toynbee's work from academic historians in America, although Pitirim Sorotkin, an eminent sociologist wrote a more positive article that was published in The Journal of Modern History (and reprinted in Toynbee and History). Although he disputed Toynbee's fundamental idea that a civilization is a unified whole and argued that Toynbee's fitting of all civilizations into a pattern of genesis, growth, and decline was far too rigid, he still expressed the view that Toynbee's work was "one of the most significant works of our time in the field of historical synthesis."

任何学科的经典著作，无论是历史学还是其他学科，都需要定期进行重新评价，《历史研究》也不例外。1989年，为纪念汤因比诞辰一百周年，出版了两本书。在收录于《汤因比：重新评价》一书中的文章中，我们可以清楚地看到，专业历史学家们如今已准备好以更积极的态度看待汤因比的成就。而著名历史学家威廉·H·麦克尼尔在其传记《汤因比传》中，也为重新评价汤因比的著作提供了令人信服的论证。尽管存在一些批评，但《历史研究》很可能与奥斯瓦尔德·斯宾格勒的《西方的没落》（1918-1923）一样，成为二十世纪最具学术性和启发性的历史分析著作之一，流传后世。

It is customary for major works, of history or any other discipline, to undergo periodic reevaluation, and A Study of History has been no exception. In 1989, two books were published to celebrate the centenary of Toynbee's birth. In the essays collected in Toynbee: Reappraisals, it is clear that professional historians were now prepared to look more favorably on Toynbee's achievement. And in his biography Toynbee: A Life, William H. McNeill, himself a noted historian, made a persuasive case for the positive reevaluation of Toynbee's work. It seems likely that, criticisms notwithstanding, A Study of History will endure, along with Oswald Spengler's The Decline of the West (1918-1923), as one of the most learned and provocative works of historical analysis written in the twentieth century.

What Do I Read Next?

  • 记者罗伯特·D·卡普兰在《即将到来的无政府状态：粉碎后冷战时代的梦想》（2000 年）一书中指出，后冷战世界远非和平时代的序曲，对除少数人以外的所有人来说，它很可能是一个黯淡的地方。
  • Journalist Robert D. Kaplan, in The Coming Anarchy: Shattering the Dreams of the Post Cold War (2000), argues that the post-Cold War world, far from being the prelude to an era of peace, is likely to be a bleak place for all but a few.
  • JM Roberts 的《世界史》（1993 年）是一部图文并茂的单卷本人类历史概览，描述了 Toynbee 讨论的许多文明的性质和主要发展路线，尽管 Roberts 也包括 Toynbee 所忽略的非洲文明。
  • J. M. Roberts' History of the World (1993) is a well-illustrated one-volume survey of human history that describes the nature and main lines of development of many of the civilizations that Toynbee discusses although Roberts also includes African civilizations, which Toynbee ignores.
  • 威廉·H·麦克尼尔所著的《世界史》（1998年）是一部广受欢迎的单卷本历史著作，重点讲述了中东、印度、中国和欧洲兴起的文明。麦克尼尔巧妙地运用了最新的考古发现，并阐释了这些发现对历史研究的影响。本版还收录了对1976年以来世界历史上最重要事件的讨论。
  • A World History, by William H. McNeill (1998), is a popular one-volume history that emphasizes the civilizations that have arisen in the Middle East, India, China, and Europe. McNeill makes excellent use of recent archaeological discoveries and explains how they have impacted historical scholarship. This edition also includes a discussion of the most important events in world history since 1976.
  • 汤因比的《世界宗教中的基督教》（1957 年）收录了汤因比 1955 年在美国发表的演讲。他探讨了基督徒对其他主要现存宗教的信徒的态度——既包括这种态度是什么，也包括这种态度应该是什么样的。
  • Toynbee's Christianity among the Religions of the World (1957) consists of lectures given by Toynbee in the United States in 1955. He discusses the attitude of Christians toward followers of the other great living religions—both what that attitude is and what it should be.
